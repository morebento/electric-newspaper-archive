Tuesday, February 3, 1998

How to net super cheap fares!
By Ray Thomas

War has broken out in the Australian travel industry with some amazing fare deals for intending travellers, particularly those heading to Europe.

And the Internet has become the latest battleground!

On offer, incredibly cheap prices which will help to compensate would-be travellers for the erosion of their European purchasing power brought about by the lower dollar.

The plunge in the Australian dollar, down against the UK pound by some 20 percent in 12 months, has sent international airline bookings out of Australia to their lowest levels in years.

As a result, the carriers are offering deals, (the word phenomenal even becomes an understatement) to get some bums on their many vacant seats.

The fare war is well and truly on and Internet users are among those who will benefit the most.

For instance, did you know that you can get to London with Singapore Airlines for just $1379, providing that you depart before the end of March and complete your journey home by the end of June?

And there are equally good deals to other European cities.

Malaysian goes one better. Depart for Europe by May the 15th (you have up to 12 months to make your return journey) and the fare will be just $1299.

Deals like this are available through a company with a net address of www.travel.com.au a site I stumbled across recently. This company is an amalgamation of three successful Sydney travel agencies.

Bill Gair, its managing director, says that since its formation 12 months ago, the net has provided 25 percent of its overall business.

He says that clients using the net expect "clean, honest information" and that his company goes out of its way to avoid exploiting clients, who are often presented with travel arrangements negotiated only with preferred carriers.

Customers visiting www.travel.com's Sydney office in person find that they sit on the same side of the desk as the travel consultant, ensuring that they have access to the same computer information available to the consultant.

And clients also have free internet access to enable them to independently access travel information.

Bill Gair says that www.travel.com is the most successful commercially operating internet address in Australia in terms of the amount of business being transacted on the site.

There are, however, quite a number of travel companies with sites on the net. Many, though, aren't regularly updated, although a number are worth browsing. .. sites run by Jetset and Flight Centre among them.

The site, www.travel.com.au, however, is updated every day, offers specials such as $1180 return to Paris or Frankfurt with departures up until the end of March, and has a response mechanism for requests for the lowest fares on specific days.

For example, if you decide that you want to fly to London from Adelaide on Sunday, 15 April and you feed the appropriate information into your computer, you'll soon learn that there are no less than 58 different ways to get there at prices ranging from $1300 to $3700.

Your options include Malaysian at $1300, Singapore Airlines at $1344, Cathay Pacific at $1504 and Qantas at $2277.

So far, most of the deals on offer are for flights leaving Australia by the end of May. But rest assured, industry sources say that bookings for the European summer (June, July, August) are also well down, so the fare war will, no doubt, benefit travellers departing then too.

And keep an eye on the net.
