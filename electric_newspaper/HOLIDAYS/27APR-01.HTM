Monday, April 27, 1998

Oz boffin meets Mary Poppins
by George Aldridge in the Mother Country

Story so far: Our cartoonist, the often-imitated George Aldridge, set off from Australia last week on assignment for The Electric Newspaper. Now read on...

A couple of hundred years ago some poor sods endured a painful, tiresome and totally unbearable journey out to Oz, crammed into tiny and somewhat unbelievably small spaces, reaching our shores in sometimes appalling health and physical condition.

Last week I retraced that awesome trek not exactly closeted away in some festering hulk, but discovered no less that the wonder of the modern jet age has done little to provide any greater comfort for the returning convict.

All numb vertebra aside, the journey Hawker-London via Rome completed, and family now comfortable and resident in East Grimstead (I couldn't find central Grimstead) I aled up on the "Bishop's Finger" with locals Peter and Chris Dettmar and went exploring.

One immediately discovers an area brimming with history - crikey - nearly every stone fortress and hedgerow once provided some meagre resistance to the vile and oppressive forces of Attila the Hun or one of his close cousins. Mind you, a night at one of the many great pubs in the region like the "Old House at Home" at Dormansland would have stopped Attila dead in his tracks - and given him a hangover to boot. These delightful little "cubbyhouse" like boozers are welcome refuge for the tired and bag-slung traveller or ear-ringed invader alike, providing REAL furniture... comfy chairs and couches, not a pokie in sight, and more beer taps than bowsers at the local B.P. station.

My advice therefore, fellow adventurers, at this early stage is exit the M25 at J6; that is, the suicidal lunge left through the Porsches and Pantechnicons, (U.K.-style "Who Dares Wins") and tootle off to "Winnie the Poo" country, take a lung-full of some of the cleaner stuff for the next sling shot ride into London for a tongue tattle with those speaking the 200 versions of 'English'.

More of that later...

Cheers,

Cartoon correspondent,

George Aldridge
