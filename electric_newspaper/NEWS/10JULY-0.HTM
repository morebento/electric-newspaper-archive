Friday July 10, 1998

All in a day's work

Unemployment in South Australia remains the highest in mainland Australia, according to official figures released yesterday. The official unemployment rate in SA is now 10 per cent, down point four per cent since last month.

The State Government welcomed the figures, with Employment Minister Joan Hall saying that while the results demonstrated the volatility of the local labor market, other indicators suggested improving signs for jobs growth.

"Monday's ANZ job vacancy series shows job advertisements in SA are at their highest level since July 1990, before Labor's recession," Ms Hall said.

"Along with the Government's $100 million jobs package, and the Premier's announcement of 800 Optus jobs this morning, this is an encouraging sign for our State," she said.

Ms Kotz was referring to Optus Communications' decision to establish a customer service centre in Adelaide, which will employ 400 people by this time next year and 800 people in 18 months.

The Electric Newspaper understands that the deal with the State Government was agreed to some time ago, with the announcement postponed until yesterday to coincide with the release of the official jobless figures.

The Government has offered an undisclosed range of incentives and publicly-funded inducements to entice Optus to set up the centre in the building at Technology Park which had been vacated by the now-defunct Galaxy pay television operator.

Premier John Olsen yesterday refused to reveal the cost to the taxpayer of bringing Optus to Adelaide.

"This type of investment is a major focus of the SA Government's back office and call centre investment attraction strategy and follows successful investments by companies such as Westpac, Bankers Trust and Boral," Mr Olsen said.

The State Opposition welcomed the Optus announcement, though less enthusiastically than The Advertiser.

Labor Leader Mike Rann said the Optus decision equated to halting less than two weeks' job losses, and the shadow Treasurer Kevin Foley said taxpayers should be told how much of their money was used to bring call centres to SA.

"It looks very much like we are paying twice for the same 800 jobs, remembering that Galaxy promised 1000 jobs which cost the taxpayers tens of millions of dollars," Mr Foley said.

The worst aspect of the unemployment situation in SA is still the participation rate - the percentage of people who are in the job market.

SA's participation rate is very low. This is because many unemployed people have ceased trying to find work. If SA's participation rate recovered to the national level, the unemployment rate in SA would be over 15.5 per cent.
