Tuesday April 14, 1998

Who are we?
by Hendrik Gout

Glad you asked! Thank you! We're a bunch of journalists, production staff (read: web designers), and general all-round bad guys. But it's hard to generalise - Ray Thomas, who wakes the roosters every morning while compiling the popular 'News Digest' is a bespectacled gentle man whose greatest passions include his coffee machine and his A.F.L. He's also one of SA's most senior news journalists and producers. This is quite a contrast with Andrea Stylianou, who's a graduate in journalism studies and science, but has not before worked in daily news.

And there's lots of people in between. Go to The Team page and see some of them. Tania has mastered the digital camera, and the Ideas Department is constantly cooking up a new brew. We have people who do errands, do scans, do the dishes (that's me!), and do a hell of a lot else. We're running a daily newspaper.

We also have regular columnists. People like John Rau, a South Australian intellectual with a compassion for the State and its people. His weekly "Viewpoint" is stimulating, at times confronting us with a new way of looking at today's events.

And we have columns by prominent elected representatives. They vote on legislation. It's time we all knew more about what individual Members of Parliament were thinking, and why they do what they do.

We have book reviews, stories about places to see in a wonderful State - our own - and cultural happenings. Our film reviewers know their stuff, and may help you choose a movie to see.

We're here because we choose to be. It's one of the great freedoms in life, choosing. We have the technical and journalistic skills to produce something some of you seem to like. For the period 30/Mar/1998:00:06:29 and 06/Apr/1998:00:22:01 we had 173, 421 total hits. That's not bad for a paper that's publicised mainly by word-of-mouth!

We need to get a lot better. We need more staff, and wider coverage. We're getting a new engine to drive our site - a very powerful engine which will let us try all sorts of things better. Less typos. Faster on-line stories. Even easier for you to find stuff.

We need a few quid, so if you want to subscribe please do. Being a subscriber means sending us a subscription of at least $15, but generally $30. Send it to The Electric Newspaper, 108 L'Estrange St, Glenunga, SA 5064. We'll send you a receipt and tell you how you'll be notified of an instant news flash, and you get to go to our celebratory Electric Newspaper picnic, where you can bend our ears.

The Electric Newspaper is you. It is its readers, its contributors, its letter-writers (we publish 'em all, as long as we don't go to jail for it), and the you who makes up part of a diverse, curious, active readership which keeps us on our toes as we keep the rest of the joint on its toes.

Thanks.

Hendrik Gout
Editor
