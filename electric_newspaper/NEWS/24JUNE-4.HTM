Wednesday June 24, 1998

Locked out of key commitment

Three full-time jobs have been created by setting up Tames Water's Asia-Pacific headquarters in Adelaide, according to the Opposition.

This is 97 jobs short of the commitment given by John Olsen as a "no ifs, no buts, no maybes" part of the United Water contract in 1995 when the deal was signed.

Yesterday, the Minister responsible, Michael Armitage, told Parliament's Estimates Committee he had been informed "that 100 people is not a key commitment".

Mr Armitage revealed no penalties had been applied against United Water for its failure to meet this contractual obligation.

"Similarly, no penalties have been applied to United Water for causing the Bolivar pong or for its failure to offer shares to allow it, as a wholly-owned company, to be Australianised," Labor frontbencher Annette Hurley said.

John Olsen was Minister in charge of the outsourcing and privatisation of Adelaide's water supply management.

Opinion polls showed it was overwhelmingly opposed by South Australians. The Government insisted water would be cheaper and privatisation would create hundreds of jobs, and make Adelaide the capital of a huge and growing water industry with exports and projects throughout South-East Asia.

Mr Olsen also said the consortium which won the contract, United Water, would soon become locally-owned to large degree. Later, the only SA shareholder sold out to an international company.

"According to the Opposition's leaked copy of the water contract, a key commitment was the relocation of Thames Water's Asia Pacific headquarters to Adelaide from Melbourne with the creation of 100 jobs," Ms Hurley said.

"Mr Armitage didn't know this was a contractual obligation under his own water deal."

The contract said the relocation had to occur within three months of the signing of the agreement, two and a half years ago.
