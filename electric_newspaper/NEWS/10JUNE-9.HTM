Wednesday June 10, 1998

Premier slides back to SA

Premier John Olsen has returned to Adelaide from a three-day trip to Japan, leaving as he came - as quietly as possible.

On Sunday, when he left, lingering television images on the evening news showed the Premier rushing past reporters, declining to answer questions.

In Japan, Mr Olsen met executives of Mitsubishi Motors Australia Ltd, who told him the company had a longterm focus on its South Australian operations. This was never in doubt.

Mr Olsen also confirmed that the Japanese cycling team will do some of its pre-Olympic training in Adelaide. One Nation supporters need not fear a large influx: there will be just eight.

The cycling announcement had already been made before Mr Olsen left.

While he was away, though, there has been some news. Mr Brown's supporters are again talking about when to challenge the Premier.

A Brown backer told The Electric Newspaper this morning that speculation about Mr Olsen's hold on the Premiership would continue while his personal support was low among Liberal Party branch members and the public, while backbenchers felt bullied and were not consulted about major Liberal Government decisions, and while the Premier's media performances remained patchy.

But he said that was a long way from saying there would be a spill motion in the near future.

"The Premier has a lot of listening to do," the senior Liberal said. "There's a view that he surrounds himself with advisers who either aren't up to the job or who are too afraid to speak their mind.

"I don't think he knows a quite a few of us who'd genuinely like him to do better. But as you blokes in the media know, his office is a bit of a fortress."

Mr Olsen passed through the airport this morning, with the Premier's office not advising all media.
