Friday July 3, 1998

Ringing in the changes

Telstra chief Frank Blount has announced another 2000 redundancies, despite the release this week of a report from the Australian Communications Authority (ACA) highly critical of Telstra's service standards.

Since 1996 more than 25,000 Telstra jobs have been cut.

"You don't have to be Einstein to make the connection between plummeting staff numbers and soaring complaint levels," union spokesperson Michelle Bisset said yesterday.

"The ACA report shows that complaints about billing and service have risen by 37% yet Telstra seems determined to slash another 2000 jobs."

The union has been angered by Frank Blount's statement that Telstra need to focus on "delivery of increased shareholder value".

"This push to increase benefits for shareholders is coming at the great expense of average householders and Telstra staff. During the next election all Australians will be asked to consider the sale of the rest of Telstra. Before deciding people should think very carefully and remember what has happened to service and jobs as result of its partial sale," Ms Bissett said.

The Senate will consider legislation about the sale of the rest of Telstra in the next two weeks.
