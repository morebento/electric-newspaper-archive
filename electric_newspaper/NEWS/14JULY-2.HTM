Tuesday July 14, 1998


closed to the public, open to the public purse


not an entirely bad pad

People's garden closed for winter
by Chris Warren

Here's an interesting twist on the privatisation debate. While the State Government is selling every State-owned asset it can find, Environment Minister Dorothy Kotz is reluctant to part with the State's most exclusive Botanic Garden.

It's called Beechwood, a charming collection of exotic trees and shrubs near the Adelaide Hills town of Stirling, and it is open to the public for only a few weeks a year.


A private garden maintained at public expense
With a handful of professional gardeners and an annual budget of $100,000, Beechwood is "a waste of taxpayers' money", according to Labor's Environment spokesperson John Hill.

Mr Hill wants Beechwood sold and the resources directed to other botanic gardens and parks which, he claims, are being starved of funds.

The problem for Ms Kotz is that while Beechwood Garden is government-owned, Beechwood Mansion, the centre-piece of the four hectare property, is in private hands and is occupied by the Fletcher family.

"It's a scandal that such a large sum of money is being used on a piece of infrastructure which gets very little public use," Mr Hill told Parliament.

"In fact the majority of the use of the garden is enjoyed by private landowners of the property which used to go with the gardens."

It is quite extraordinary to have a heritage-listed garden surrounding a privately owned house, with the Government meeting the cost of maintaining the grounds, yet having little control over when it's opened to the public.

Ms Kotz agrees "it's quite a problem for the Government", but the Minister won't comment on what options are available to her.

One may be getting the Fletcher family to mow their own vista.

Any suggestion to sell Beechwood Garden for private sub-division is likely to meet with a hostile reception from the local gentry who enjoy the benefits of a magnificent floral display every spring and autumn courtesy of the taxpayers.
