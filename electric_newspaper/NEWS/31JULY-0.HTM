Friday July 31, 1998

Olsen sticks by Ingo like doggydoo sticks to shoes

South Australian Premier John Olsen yesterday once again gave his support to disgraced Minister Graham Ingerson, who was found last week to have deliberately misled Parliament and who nevertheless escaped - in a close vote - censure by the House.

Yesterday, No Pokies MLC Nick Xenophon said since the Ingerson affair, he could not bring himself to trust the Government over other matters such as its reasons for selling the State's power utilities.

Later, Opposition Leader Mike Rann said the Premier's inability to sack Mr Ingerson meant in MP's were losing faith in the Government. He said the Government needed to win the trust of those members to get the ETSA Bill through.

"I can understand Mr Xenophon's cynicism in dealing with the Olsen Government over the sale of ETSA and Optima," Mr Rann said.

He said secrecy surrounding ETSA and broken public promises were worrying many MP's.

"I have been calling for months for John Olsen to release about 1200 documents which it is keeping secret from the public.

"Many of those documents are taxpayer-funded consultants reports which examine in detail the issue of the sale of our electricity assets," Mr Rann said.

The Premier said yesterday that last week's events were last week's events. He was, he said, looking at rebuilding the State's economy.

Whether Mr Ingerson should resign was a question for Mr Ingerson himself, Mr Olsen said.

Mr Ingerson has apparently answered that question. He's staying. Not a lot of MPs fell over with surprise.
