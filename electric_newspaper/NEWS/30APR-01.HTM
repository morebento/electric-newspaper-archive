Thursday April 30, 1998

Support our Health Centres - report

State and Federal governments should increase support for Women's and Community Health Centres and expand their provision of medical services, according to a report just released.

It argues that community health doctors working in three centres in Adelaide's western suburbs provide a much-needed service to some of the city's most needy people and an important alternative to private GPs.

And the authors also call for greater experimentation with alternative funding models for comprehensive primary health care.

Not Just a Doctor, a joint project by the Department of Public Health at Flinders University and the SA Community Health Research Unit, is a comprehensive study of how community members feel about the services they are offered.

Three-quarters of the 101 people interviewed over a year strongly supported the medical services provided by the centres, praising the accessibility and quality of care, the welcoming environment and the doctors' empathy and communication skills.

In particular, they appreciated the opportunity to spend more time with a doctor when necessary, to be treated as a "whole person", and to develop relationships in which trust and rapport were established. These qualities often were described in contrast to experiences with medical services elsewhere.

The interviewees also stressed both the value of one-to-one consultations and the wider contributions the community health doctors and other members of the multi-disciplinary teams made to the health and well-being of the community.

"Participants' perceptions of health, illness and well-being incorporated a wide range of views and included physical, social, emotional and environmental factors," the report says.

"The overwhelming majority reported that their views were accommodated and supported by the underpinning philosophy and environment of W&CHCs and by the doctors who worked there."

The report notes that: * people using the Women's and Community Health Centrees are more likely than those attending private GPs to have multiple disadvantages, including such situations as domestic violence and poor housing; * demand for the medical services far outweighs supply and for many clients these services have been the entry point to other activities at the centres.

The three centres - The Parks Community Health Service, Port Adelaide Community Health Service and Dale Street Women's Health Centre - are now part of the Adelaide Central Community Health Service.

The new study, which involved questionnaires and focus groups as well as the interviews, complements a 1993 investigation of GP attitudes to different organisational models.

The study team was lead by Professor Fran Baum, the director of both SACHRU and the Flinders University Department of Public Health, and included Ms Libby Kalucy, Ms Charlie Murray, Dr Bronwyn Veale and Ms Megan Warin.
