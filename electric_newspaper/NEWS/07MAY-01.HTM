Thursday May 7, 1998

Salmon rusty: the fatwa decreed

For the first time, an Australian quarantine decision has been challenged - and overturned - by the World Trade Organisation.

Despite an Australian decision to ban the importation of fresh North American salmon, a World Trade Organisation appeals panel has ruled that the salmon must be allowed to be allowed into the country.

Australia was concerned about the risks of importing foreign diseases, which could devastate the Australian industry.

The Australian Democrats' Rural spokesman, Senator John Woodley, says lifting Australian import bans could expose Australian fish to up to 20 new diseases.

"Australia's reputation as a producer of disease-free salmon is at stake here," Senator Woodley said.

"There may be grounds for the Australian Government to appeal the WTO decision. The Government should move heaven and earth to oppose the report."

Existing controls on the importation of cooked chicken and New Zealand could also now be challenged.
