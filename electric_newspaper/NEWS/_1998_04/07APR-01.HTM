Tuesday April 7, 1998

Secret Government meeting on beach future

A secret Government workshop has been held over concerns surrrounding the design and future maintenance of the Patawalonga channel, the new marina at Glenelg and the West Beach Boat Harbour.

The report discusses the lack of an adequate impact assessment process and raises concerns about removal and disposal of polluted silt, shell grit and organic matter such as seaweed from the area.

Opponents of the West Beach project say they are concerned that no Environmental Impact Assessment has been carried out on the harbour proposal.

They say their concerns have now been justified by the Glenelg Marine Ingress Governmental stakeholders workshop last Monday, 30 March.

No community interest groups or representatives were invited. The meeting was attended by representatives from five government departments and three local government authorities including Primary Industries and Resources and City of Holdfast Bay.

"This paper says that the Government does not know how much the maintenance of the boat harbour or Patawalonga mouth is going to cost," said spokesperson for the campaign to stop the boat harbour, Bridget Bannear.

"They confidently state that it will cost $250,000 per year but according to the paper they do not have data or information on which to base this estimate," she said.

"The maintenance costs of the marina and boat harbour will cost South Australian taxpayers an enormous amount if adequate research is not conducted into this Government project."

Protesters are at the site this morning.
