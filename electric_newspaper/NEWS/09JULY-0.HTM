Thursday July 9, 1998

Blowhard on Kangaroo Island
by Alice Carey

Freak winds blew at up to 100 kms per hour through the town of Penneshaw on Kangaroo Island yesterday. Century-old pine trees were uprooted and houses partially de-roofed. Locals believe it was the most ferocious wind they had ever seen on the island.

Galvanised iron sheeting dislodged from numerous houses and one of the hotels lost half its balcony roof. One streetsign lay bent, flattened to the ground by the force of the wind as though had been driven over by a tank.

"It was like a war zone, it was loud and it was cracking," said post office restaurant owner David Allen.

"We (my wife and I) were in a state of shock. We ran into each other looking for a place to hide - wondering what would fall on our heads."

The front section of the Allen’s roof lifted up from the walls of the sturdy old building and miraculously came back down, leaving large gaps around the cornice.

At the Penneshaw jetty the last car had just loaded on the Sealink ferry when the squall struck. The concrete mooring post went crashing down and the ferry drifted 10 metres from its dock. Construction workers nearby threw ropes to the drifting ferry and used their excavator to tow the ferry to shore.

"Its lucky that the winds didn't pass over those cardboard houses," said one construction worker, pointing at the many little weatherboard houses on the hillside leading down to the bay.

Penneshaw locals were quick to respond to the disaster. Farmers brought in equipment to help clear away fallen trees, a hot soup kitchen was set up. About 50 Country Fire Service, State Emergency Service and local council workers strapped tarpaulins over exposed roof beams and cleared away the huge trees. The ETSA cherry-picker was used to remove broken branches.

By five o'clock the street was near-empty again. The Sealink ferry was due to return from the mainland and would continue operating. However the locals were double-checking that all was strapped down last night - the Bureau of Meteorology issued another strong wind warning.
