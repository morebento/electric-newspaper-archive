Monday July 27, 1998

SA mosque gets facelift, well Worth it.

Federal Environment Minister Robert Hill was in Adelaide yesterday congratulating members of the Adelaide Islamic Mosque. The historical mosque will have $45,000 dollars spent on its renovation as part of the National Estate Grants Program. Member for the marginal seat of 'Adelaide', Trish Worth was there, listening with a pert smile as Senator Hill fended off journalist's enquiries of pre-election sweeteners.

The $45,000 grant to restore and preserve the Adelaide mosque's minarets is one of 45 grants approved by Senator Hill as part of the Australian Heritage Commission.

In South Australia there are five grants totalling $139,261 . The overall value of funding nationally is $1.5 million. The aim is to restore aspects of built heritage, preserving cultural and religious history in buildings around Australia.

"It is also making good economic sense as heritage-based tourism becomes more prominent. By preserving the character of their town, local communities are able to offer tourists a unique look into Australia's past," said Senator Hill.

Although the Islamic Mosque is not open to tourists, it is a national trust building. Built in 1888 by the Afghan community, it was a place of worship for the many Afghan camel drivers before they journeyed outback with their camel teams.

Senator Hill was keen to impress on the Islamic community present the reasons for their winning the grant.

"The point I wanted to stress is we see this mosque here in Adelaide, not just something for local significance, not just something for your community, but really an asset of national historical significance and want to help you preserve it - not only as a place of worship for you, " Senator Hill explained.

Other South Australian projects approved include;

  • restoration of the Lighthouse Station Cottage on Althorpe Island - $7,446
  • restoration of the Olivewood Homestead in Renmark - $18,000
  • cultural and site survey, Parachilna Ochre Mine (indigenous project) - $29,815
  • conservation of the Moonta Mines Church pipe organ and bell tower - $39,000

    Asked whether these announcements coincided with a looming election the Senator said the election would be before May and he just happened to finish approving the projects.

    This particular restoration project just happens to fall in the most marginally held Liberal seat in South Australia.
