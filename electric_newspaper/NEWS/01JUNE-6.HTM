Monday June 1, 1998

Keepsake owner kept safe

A Russian cargo ship early this morning picked up two Australians from their stricken 10-metre yacht in the Tasman Sea, almost 1000 kilometres east of Hobart.

Australia picked up a distress signal from the yacht yesterday morning. A small plane left Hobart and found the yacht, more than halfway to New Zealand, dragging a sea anchor in a huge swell.

Two Orions then consecutively left Adelaide to keep watch over the yacht, the Keepsake II, as the bulk carrier Ob was diverted to rescue the two sailors, Karel Fontaine and Kai Olaussen.

Fontaine suffered head injuries in the storm. Olaussen broke a rib.

At half past five this morning, the Ob dropped one of its lifeboats over the side, and the two sailors were picked up in two-metre seas.

The Ob is now heading for the northern Tasmanian port of Burnie, and is scheduled to dock there on Wednesday. The sailors are being treated by the ship's doctors.
