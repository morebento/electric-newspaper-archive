Monday June 1, 1998

Greens leader urges harbour scuttling

Australian Greens Senator Bob Brown made a flying visit to South Australia today to urge protesters to continue their fight against the West beach boat launching facility.

Senator Brown asked the Premier, Mr Olsen, to confess that he'd made a mistake by deciding to build the West beach boat harbour.

Inspecting the construction site, Mr Brown said it was already plain the quarter of a million dollars which the Government estimates has to be spent annually to pump and truck sand around the groyne was well below the amount needed.

Senator Brown said it would cost "many millions of dollars" annually to stop the erosion of Adelaide's northern beaches when the groyne is complete, because it will stop the natural northward drift of sand.

"It's not too late," Senator Brown told protesters yesterday.

"Come to your senses," Senator Brown urged Mr Olsen. "Do the right thing by Adelaide and by future generations, and stop this environmentally destructive and costly, publicly-funded wall, and go back to giving the beach its own eco-system."

Senator Brown said a path to the beach on the southern side of the groyne was already 80 cm under the sand since it was built last month - evidence, he said, of how sand will build up on the southern side of the wall.
