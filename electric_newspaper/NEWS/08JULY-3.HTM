Wednesday July 8, 1998

Pants on fire
by Hendrik Gout

The most lingering television images of ex-deputy Premier Graham Ingerson on Monday night were not that of the disgraced and shambling Member for Bragg. They were those of his staff leaving his Ministerial suite, lying that Mr Ingerson was not inside.

That Mr Ingerson's behaviour caused his normally honest staff to have to lie on his behalf is an indication of how desperate Mr Ingerson had become.

But were these the only untruths told on Monday night?

In his media conference, given on the way down from his 11th floor Riverside office in a lift crowded with television cameras, Mr Ingerson said that the Premier had asked him to stay on as Minister, and that the decision to resign from the deputy Premiership was taken by himself and his wife.

Did the Premier really ask his embattled deputy to remain a Minister?

At a media conference on the lawns of Government House after the swearing in of South Australia's new deputy Premier yesterday, and shortly afterwards Parliament House in Question Time, this question was repeatedly asked of the Premier.

"Mr Ingerson told us last night that it was his decision to resign," ABC reporter David Bevan asked the Premier. "Did you tell him to stay on or did you tell him that you thought it was time for him to go?"

"No, Graham's view is an accurate account of the sequence of events," Mr Olsen replied.

"Did you ask Graham Ingerson to stay in Cabinet?" Mr Bevan persisted.

"Well, Graham is continuing in his portfolios. He stepped aside as deputy, as you know, and he'll be continuing those portfolios," Mr Olsen obfuscated.

Mr Bevan is made of sterner stuff. Once again he asked, "Premier, that's what's happening. But did you ask him - did you ask him - to stay in Cabinet?"

Mr Olsen sucked his teeth. "Graham is going to continue in those portfolios. He took the decision to stand aside as deputy and I welcome his continuing involvement."

The answer to the question that Mr Olsen would not answer, according to senior Liberals, is "No".

In Parliament, under repeated questions along the same lines from the Opposition, Mr Olsen again would not give a straight answer.

Let's look again at what Mr Ingerson said on Monday night. He said that Mr Olsen had asked him to continue as a Minister.

"If you believe that you still believe in Santa Claus," a Liberal MP said last night.
