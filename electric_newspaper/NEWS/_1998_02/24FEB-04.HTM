Wednesday February 25, 1998

Misled, he was.
by John Rau

Premier Olsen has every right to be furious.

The Auditor-General has now revealed that our most senior public servants were made aware that the State's finances were in a hopeless mess as far back as June 1997.

It was clear to the Premier in December 1997, when he first saw this report, that the only way to save our State from total financial chaos was to sell ETSA and the State's other few remaining assets.

How do you think the Premier feels? He was kept in ignorance of this looming financial disaster by his most trusted, senior advisers.

Their withholding of this crucial information lured him into making, in good faith, the uncategorical promise that if he won the October 1997 election, he would not put ETSA up for sale.

He must have been devastated when the public servants finally told him what they had known all along.

If the Premier had known the truth before the election, there can be no doubt that a man of his moral courage and honesty would have taken this sale of ETSA proposal to the people. He would have delighted in the task of explaining why it was necessary. He would have patiently answered his critics. He would have removed all doubts. He would have made Mr. Rann look foolish .

Instead, the Premier has been made to look shifty by his public servants. Mr Rann, who continually asked him about an ETSA sale, now looks like a prophet! This is an outrage.

Vengeance is not Christian, but removing incompetent public servants is the duty of any worthy Government.Those who knew about this report and kept the information secret from the Government must, by any definition, be incompetent. They must be identified and dismissed at once.

The damage that has been so unjustly done to the Premier's high reputation for honesty and a love of truth may never be fully repaired. It is a burden of high office that he must alas bear. Let him now act swiftly and with his characteristic strength.

Sack the culprits now, every one of them, in the interest of our State.
