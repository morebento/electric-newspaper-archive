Saturday February 14, 1998

The Idiot Box
By Toni Jupe

Adelaide-based national children's media watchdog Young Media Australia has taken a swipe at a new TV show which makes fun of babies and young children.

Other major national children's interests groups including the Australian Early Childhood Association and Action for Children are horrified at the program's content.

But the New South Wales Department of Community Services appears to have approved the show, which portrays one-year-old babies being verbally abused by the adult host.

The Nine Network screened a half-hour 'special' called Little Aussie Battlers earlier this week (7.30pm, Tuesday, February 10).

It showed former Doug Anthony Allstar Tim Ferguson hosting contests between teams of three children aged between three and seven years, in such events as Spaghetti Darts where the babies in highchairs were exhorted to throw their spaghetti bolognaise as far as they could.

In another event called H2O Slammers , the three seven-year-olds were perched at a 'bar' complete with a backdrop of mirrored bottles and exhorted to slam down eight shot glasses of water the fastest.

Young Media Australia says the program, described in publicity material as "the ultimate test of endurance" was certainly that for any viewers who cared about children.

"It really reached the pits with the H2O Slammers segment," said YMA executive director Ms Barbara Biggins.

"This and other programs of the same ilk, such as Australia's Funniest Home Video Show, which set up children for accidents, frights, messes and to make fools of themselves all for adult enjoyment, appear to not contravene TV program standards. They just offend against standards of common decency.

After the show screened on Tuesday night, the Children's Employment Unit received calls from parents wanting to know how they could get their children on the program.

Ms Biggins said Young Media Australia believed some of the host's comments on Tuesday's episode were reprehensible.

During the Nappy Ever After segment involving four-year-olds changing the nappies on lifesize baby dolls, Ferguson says: "Don't worry, you can be as rough with the baby as you like".

At the end of the Advanced Driving School segment involving the four-year-olds driving little plastic cars around an obstacle course consisting of metre-high dummies of policemen, "little old ladies" and little girls (and in the process knocking them over, sometimes deliberately), Ferguson says: "Well, road rage was definitely the theme today".

During a skit involving Ferguson and a young boy barracking at the footy, Ferguson yells: "Shoot the ref".

Ms Biggins said the purpose of the show clearly was to make money, using children, "all in the name of entertainment for grown-ups".

"According to the voice-over at the end, all nine participants received a $250 voucher from the major toy store which sponsors the program," she said.

"There also appear to be a number of other opportunities to get brand names and other program names (including other Nine Network shows) in front of the audience through 'product placement', and the potential for children saying brand names in response to questions."

Meanwhile, the Australian Broadcasting Authority has finally delivered its decision on a complaint from Young Media Australia about an episode of Australia's Funniest Home Video Show in May, 1997.

The ABA did not agree with YMA's claim that a segment showing parents frightening their children with a chainsaw breached the Commercial Television Industry Code of Practice. YMA claimed the segment was not "suitable for children to watch under the guidance of a parent or guardian".

The ABA said it was " sympathetic to the view that it is questionable for parents to scare their own children in such a way and to tape such a prank against their own children," but it did not consider that segment harmed or distressed ordinary children watching the program with a parent's guidance.

Young Media Australia will continue to monitor Australia's Funniest Home Video Show and any further episodes of Little Aussie Battlers.

Young Media Australia is happy to receive public comments about either program. Its web-site is http://www.youngmedia.org.au
