Thursday February 12, 1998

SA unionists in Webb Dock protest

The South Australian Secretary of the United Trades and Labor Council, Chris White, is among 40 S.A. wharfies who travelled to Melbourne yesterday to protest against non-union labour on the Australian waterfront.

The Federal Government is backing, and financially supporting, a plan by the National Farmers Federation to start a new stevedoring company at Webb Dock in Melbourne, which will exclude members of the Maritime Union of Australia.

The union says that far from being over-paid, the average award rate for stevedores is around $30 000 for a 35-hour week.

The MUA says those who do earn high wages are the men who work up to 80 hours a week in big container terminals.

"The NFF's Donald McGauchie, on the other hand, is a multi-millionaire and one of the largest land owners in Queensland," union secretary John Coombs said.

"The myth is that the NFF's company is a sincere attempt to bring competition into the wharves and help battling farmers export their produce.

"The fact is that Webb Dock handles no rural produce."

At yesterday's demonstration, a prominent member of the National Farmers Federation said most Australian farmers were very worried by the NFF's proposal.

He said farmers generally agree that primary producers and the union have enjoyed cordial relations for years, with grain and other produce handling being exempt from industrial action.

Over the years the union has donated substantially to rural drought relief funds.

Mr White said the Federal Government paid $1 million for consultants' advice on how to provoke an industrial dispute and then use the inevitable union response as an excuse to sack the workforce and replace it with non-unionised labour.

"These events are an astounding way for a Government to conduct industrial relations," Mr White said.

"Such tactics are very worrying for the nature of our so-called democratic community."
