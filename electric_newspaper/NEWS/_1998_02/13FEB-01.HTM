Friday February 13, 1998

Demonstration against marina project
By Hendrik Gout

Demonstrators chanted "Steve conned us!"
About 50 Henley and Grange residents yesterday called on their State MP, Steve Condous, to resign over the controversial marina development at Glenelg, which will cost South Australian taxpayers many hundreds of millions of dollars in perpetuity.

The marina project involves the handing over to its developers land now publicly owned, the building of a rock wall out to sea for an artificial harbour, and the closure of existing public boat launching facilities.

In compensation, the State Government - in legislation passed with the concurrence of the Labor Opposition - will build a new public launching facility further north along the Gulf St Vincent, affecting suburbs which include West Beach, Henley, and Grange.

The project is criticised because the new groyne will restrict natural northward flow of beach sand, and unless thousands of tonnes of replenishment sand are continually trucked in, these popular beaches will rapidly erode.

In published reports which had to be obtained from the Government under Freedom of Information Legislation, engineers say hundreds of trucks will need to work virtually continuously to bring in new sand or the northern beaches will be destroyed.

... and the door stayed closed
Local Liberal MP Steve Condous promised voters before the election he would lie in front of the bulldozers to stop the project.

Opponents say he has kept his promise about lying, but not about bulldozers.

Many Henley and Grange Labor Party members are equally unhappy about the role played by their own Party in capitulating to the Government about the development.

There was a strong movement to resign for the local Labor Party Branch immediately after the Opposition voted for the amended Government legislation which allowed the marina to proceed.

This disaffected vote is likely to swing in the short term towards the Australian Democrats, which has consistently fought against the proposal.
