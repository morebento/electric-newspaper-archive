Tuesday February 17, 1998

Long protest ahead at West Beach
By Chris Warren

Almost 400 people have signed up for a community picket line as opposition grows against the new West Beach boat harbour.

The residents will form a human barricade across the beach access point at Barcoo Road to block trucks loaded with rocks needed for the building of controversial breakwaters.

Construction is expected to start in a few weeks, and the residents are prepared for a long campaign.

Public concerns about how the Boat Harbour will change the West Beach coastline have been reinforced by a detailed report from the Coast Protection Board to the Development Assessment Commission.

Both these reports are available through The Electric Newspaper.

It is not yet known how much sand will have to be shifted around the boat harbour every year, or at what cost.

But the Coast Potection Board warns of the "possible removal of the facility (boat harbour)" if it fails to meet its designed use or is not economically feasible.

The cost of sand replenishment could be anywhere between $700,000 and $1.5 million annually, and involve 5,000 truck movements with sand being brouight in from other sources to prevent the complete erosion of West Beach as it is today.

The Urban Planning Minister, Diana Laidlaw, says "detailed consideration is being given to all of the issues" raised by the Coast Protection Board and Parliament's Public Works Standing Committee.

This will entail some design changes and re-assessment of the project, but the State Government is still committed to building the boat harbour.
