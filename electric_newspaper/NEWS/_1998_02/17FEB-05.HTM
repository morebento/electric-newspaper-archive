Tuesday February 17, 1998

Anglican General Synod

The Anglican Archbishop of Melbourne, Dr Keith Rayner, has criticised the Federal Government for failing to recognise the importance of an apology to indigenous Australians. Dr Rayner was speaking at the opening of the Anglican Church's 11th General Synod in Adelaide.

"Especially where self-esteem has been crushed, apology is powerful in breaking down barriers," Dr Rayner said.

"It does not stem from personal guilt for the actions of our forebears. Rather, it recognises the continuities of life and the reality that the painful consequences of past events live in people today.

I hope the government may yet change its mind on the matter," Archbishop Rayner said.

About 1300 parishes within the twenty-three dioceses (self-governing regions led by a Bishop) are represented at the General Synod by bishops, priests and lay-people.

The proceedings follow a parliamentary style of presenting and debating motions which are relevant to both the Anglican Church and the wider society.

Brisbane's Archbishop Reverend Peter Hollingworth moved that the synod acknowledge the 'deep hurt and trauma' suffered by the indiginous people of Australia and apologised "unreservedly for any part played, knowingly or unwittingly, by the Anglican Church that has contributed in anyway to that hurt or trauma."

The motion was carried unanimously.

High on the agenda at the synod are issues of reconciliation and yesterday delegates voted to adjust the church's constitution to provide a specific number of representative seats at the synod to indigenious individuals.

This will continue to be discussed today, although it has already been agreed to provide two positions for indigenious Bishops within the House of Bishops (2 of the 23 dioceses) , one position for an Aboriginal and one for a Torres Strait Islander.

Despite embracing equal opportunities for indigenious people in the church, the same consensus does not apply to the issue of women becoming bishops within the Anglican Church.

It is a contentious issue, with some dioceses firmly believing it is not an appropriate development because it is not supported within the Bible.

The issue of female bishops will be discussed tomorrow.
