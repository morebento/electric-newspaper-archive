Wednesday July 8, 1998

Curving the death rate
by Krista Eleftheriou

Aboriginal Affairs minister Dorothy Kotz yesterday announced the allocation of $2.7 million to Aboriginal aged care in an attempt to increase the life expectancy of elderly indigenous Australians.

But Aboriginal aged care project co-ordinator Daniel Dollard said with indigenous Australians dying 15 to 20 years before non-aboriginal people, this was not enough.

Mr Dollard has been involved in lobbying the Government to build an urgently need Aboriginal aged care facility in Ceduna on the State's west coast.

The facility will cost $1.7 million to build and house 10 people.

"The Commonwealth allocated only $10 million for the whole of Australia for residential Aboriginal aged care facilities. It is simply nowhere near enough to combat the high premature death rate," Mr Dollard said.

Mr Dollard said the reasons why elderly Aboriginals were dying so young stemmed from their loss of culture which centres around their land.

He stressed that many of the other issues effecting Aboriginals, including health and social problems, were by-products of their loss of cultural identity.

"An Aboriginal aged care facility will ensure the elderly can pass on their culture to the younger ones. It allows the elderly to practice their culture within their community and to not feel they are being locked away," Mr Dollard said.

He said an Aboriginal aged care facility would provide proper nutrition and access to medical treatment, increasing the life expectancy of indigenous Australians.

Mr Dollard said that it was a myth that Aboriginal people were receiving too much money.

"You only have to go and look at any Aboriginal community and see how little those people have, to understand how disadvantaged Aboriginal people are," Mr Dollard said.

Mr Dollard said the $43,000 allocated to the Ceduna-Kooniba area by Ms Kotz would go towards nutritional education and home assistance programs for the Aboriginal elderly.

Health minister Dean Brown has provided $60,000 towards the construction of the aged care facility for the west coast Aboriginals.
