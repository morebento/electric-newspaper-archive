Monday March 16, 1998

Yep, we're officially up!

And you're cordially invited to our celebratory launch today.

In 1997, a team of journalists, graphic designers, and web servers produced on-line coverage of the South Australian election.

The Electric Newspaper, as it was called, expected to have several hundred hits a day.

It got up to 13,000.

The massive response caused the team to re-think. Our experience as journalists and editors proved we were able to reach a previously un-tapped audience.

The decision was taken to launch a permanent Electric Newspaper.

A committed team of journalists has now been assembled. They include writers with experience in commercial television, radio news, and daily newspapers.

Forget all your ideas about newspapers - The Electric Newspaper does it differently.

Our travel and wine sections, for example, run as they would in a monthly magazine.

Our film reviews, essays, and feature articles can be compared with those in a weekly newspaper.

And our news coverage is virtually instantaneous.

The Electric Newspaper had the ETSA sale story on-line while the Premier was still on his feet, announcing the decision in Parliament.

The paper will have ultimately the capacity to have virtually instant news photographs, sound bites from interviews. News stories are written on computer, sent via the net to the editors, and inserted immediately. With current lap-top access we can write Parliamentary stories from the House and send them on-line via hard-line or, in the field, via modem-compatiable mobile networks. The latest South Australian-related news from Federal Parliament is already on-line through our correspondent in Canberra.

The paper is funded in its initial stages through the generous support and assistance of SA information technology companies.

In the medium term, we hope to attract sufficient people to send in a voluntary $15 yearly subscription, and long-term we are confident of generating enough revenue from advertising to keep us in the life-style to which we’re accustomed - such as three square meals a day.

The Minister for Information Services, the Hon. Wayne Matthew, will press the "Enter" key, and we'll be officially in the ether.

So we hope you can join us in our optimism and tentative first steps, and share a little fermented grape juice or Mr Cooper's finest.

It's on at High Noon, today, Monday March 16, at the Atrium, second floor, 187 Rundle Street.

We look forward to your company.
