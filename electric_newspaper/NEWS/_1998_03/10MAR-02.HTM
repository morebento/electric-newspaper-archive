Tuesday March 10, 1998

Hole in the head

SA's dental care program is broke.

77,000 South Australians now have dental problems, including toothache, and the system can't afford to treat them.

State Human Services Minister Dean Brown is in Canberra today to argue with Federal Health Minister Michael Wooldridge for more money.

Other State Ministers are also there.

Mr Brown also wants urgent action on private health insurance.

"The Federal Government has approved an eight to 10 per cent increase in private health insurance premiums, yet is offering the States only a one per cent real increase in funding for public hospitals under the Medicare Agreement," Mr Brown said.

"The public health system is experiencing the same cost increases as the private system.

"In the public system we are treating an aging population. We have a system which is at breaking point.

"The public system is facing extra demand because of the continuing decline in the number of people who are privately insured. Premium increases will lead to further pressure on the public hospital system unless the Commonwealth introduces further reforms to encourage people to take out private insurance," Mr Brown said.

Mr Brown said the Federal Government must reinstate funding under the Commonwealth Dental Health Program.

People who wake up with a toothache today will have to wait until the turn of the century to get a filling - the waiting period is 27 months.

The Howard Government has ripped $15 million out of the dental health scheme. The State has maintained its $27.5 million funding, but obviously this still leaves a big hole.

The State Opposition has called on the Olsen Government to make up for the Commonwealth cuts. Mr Brown says the State can't afford it.

Health is certain to be a big issue in this year's federal election, with all State Ministers - including the Liberals - attacking the Commonwealth's miserly health spending.
