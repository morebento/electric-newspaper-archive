Thursday March 5, 1998

ETSA sale report zapped
by Hendrik Gout

A secret report on which Premier John Olsen has relied sell ETSA and Optima was written specifically to justify the sale, a Government source has claimed.

Mr Olsen told Parliament when announcing the sale on Tuesday, February 17, that he had three reasons to sell: the Auditor-General's report which had been prepared before the election, a report by the Institute of Public Affairs, and a report compiled for Government which Mr Olsen would not table.

The Opposition has called for the release of this third report. The Government has refused.

But a Government source says the reason for its confidentially is not because it contains commercially sensitive information, but because it is "pretty thin, looks like it was slapped together in a dreadful hurry, and is embarrassingly short on detail".

The Treasury official said it would not take a great deal of perception "to work out that the authors were hand-picked to come up with the result the Premier wanted."

"Who do you think that might have been? Ask yourself if it could have involved at least some of the people who recommended the sale of other Government assets. Also ask yourself if the report discusses social or regional implications.

"The short answer is no. The long answer is no, too."

ALP Leader Mike Rann last week filed a request under Freedom of Information legislation for the release of the third report.

"He won't get it," said the Treasury official. "We understand the Government would wait until near the statutory limit of complying with the F.O.I. and then pass it around the cabinet table."

Cabinet documents are exempt from F.O.I.

"That's not to say there isn't a good argument to sell ETSA. Treasury could have provided him (the Premier) with a comprehensive independent report, and we may well have come down on the side of a sale.

"But other departments, like Consumer Affairs or Regional Development, may have had another view.

"But don't go waving this other report in the air. It's so thin it's almost transparent. If you wiped with it, you'd need to wash your hands afterwards."
