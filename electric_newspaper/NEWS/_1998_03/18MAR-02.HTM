Wednesday March 18, 1998

Retail therapy

Shopping hours in Adelaide look certain to be extended, following the announcement yesterday that it would "review" the current regulations which limit the hours in the city and suburbs.

The Government claims many advantages to extended shopping hours. The ALP is opposed, partly because of pressure from the SDA, a firey union which covers shop assistants.

Premier John Olsen used several arguments in Parliament to support further deregulation of shop hours. He said Adelaide was the convention capital of Australia, and lack of weekend trading was harming the tourist industry.

Mr Olsen also said families with two parents working full-time needed extended shopping hours.

And Mr Olsen cited national competition policy as a further reason.

"The State is obliged to review the Shop Trading Hours Act by the end of the year to comply with competition policy by the year 2000," Mr Olsen told Parliament.

"And so we must investigate in this review any present shopping hour situations that would be classed as anti-competitive."

He said if SA did not lift trading hour restrictions, the NCC's Graeme Samuel may apply penalities to our competition payments.

The Opposition is dismissive of that argument.

Shadow Industrial Affairs Minister Steph Key said the NCC had no power to withhold competition payments from our State.

"Labor believes John Olsen is using the NCC as the new 'boogie man' - the person who will haunt this State and starve us of hundreds of millions of dollars unless we do as he says," Ms Key said.

"This is nonsense from John Olsen - and he knows it.

"The fact is John Olsen was always going to succumb to pressure from the big urban-based shopping complexes and open up Sunday trading to them."

During the election campaign, the Opposition leaked minutes of a Westfield Tea Tree Plaza management meeting which clearly stated trading hours were expected to be changed after the election.

The Government's review will take six weeks.

Before coming to power in 1993, the Libverals assured small business there would be no extention to Sunday trading after that election.

After winning Government, the Government held a quick inquiry and Sunday trading was introduced in the city.

The latest review will almost certainly see this extended to the suburbs.
