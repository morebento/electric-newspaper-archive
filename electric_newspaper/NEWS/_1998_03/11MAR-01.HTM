Wednesday March 11, 1998

More money on ETSA propaganda

The State Government has announced it will embark on a television advertising campaign to persuade regional South Australia it will be better off it ETSA and Optima is sold.

Premier John Olsen was in Berri last night to muster support for the sale.

Liberal polling has shown that electricity privatisation, which Mr Olsen did not reveal before the election, is most unpopular in smaller cities and towns.

Mr Olsen declined to hold a public meeting in Berri. Attendance at the affair was by invitation only through a guest list hand-picked by the Premier's office.

Less than one third of those invited chosed to hear what Mr Olsen was going to say, and more than 100 invitations were declined.

In response to the secret polling, the Government this morning announced it would use a television campaign to get its message across.

The Government did not say how much this was going to cost, but acknowledged it would be taxpayer funded.

"The people who'll be watching the ads. will be the people paying for them," a Government source said this morning.

The campaign will also use radio advertising for an as-yet undisclosed price.

Mr Olsen said today that regional South Australians would be the major beneficiaries of the ETSA and Optima privatisation.

He said residents of small towns would be able to 'band together' to use their collective bargaining power to drive a very hard price bargain with an electricity supplier.

Echoing the pitch he used on water privatisation, Mr Olsen said electricity would still arrive at homes in exactly the same way as it does today.

"It will simply cost you less," Mr Olsen promised today.

The advertising campaign begins on Sunday night, the most expensive night of the week for television advertising.
