Monday March 30, 1998

Motor show organisers delighted
by Piston Stoned

Organisers and exhibitors of the 1998 Adelaide Motor show are delighted with what appears to be renewed consumer confidence, following the conclusion of the 1998 Adelaide Motor Show.

The motor show, held as usual at the Wayville showgrounds, is regarded by the trade as a barometer of what will happen in the new car market in the coming months.

"We don't neccessarily find people driving new cars away from the show itself," said a local dealer.

"But we can certainly tell what sort of interest there is, and this usually translates into sales for the next three or four months."

Adrian Harry, one of SA's best-known trials riders, gave a thrilling display of two-wheeled control. Observed trials are where riders have to negotiate difficult terrain, including riding over logs, through creekbeds, and over obstacled. Points are lost when the rider puts their feet down. The sport is a combination of throttle control and balance, brake control, and the ability to manoeuvre the bike.

The riders are known as the musicians of motorsport.

The next Observed Trials will be held at Rockleigh on April 26.
