Friday July 24, 1998

ALP spinning as MP yo-yos

Prominent Labor MLC Terry Cameron last night stunned the Opposition and delighted the Government when he announced it was his opinion that there were economic benefits to electricity privatisation.

"It would reduce our debt down to much more manageable levels," Mr Cameron said.

"It would certainly put us in a position to compete with the Eastern seaboard, where within two years they could be debt free," Mr Cameron said after telling his views to a deserted House while speaking on a Budget Bill.

"It would put us in a much better position to withstand the economic tidal wave that will sweep across our economy over the next year or so."

Mr Cameron's defiance of Labor policy has hardly surprised the Opposition Leader's office, who regard Mr Cameron as a maverick who's out to embarrass Mr Rann for not supporting him for a shadow portfolio.

Mr Rann last night issued an unveiled threat to Mr Cameron.

"He is aware of the rules of the Party," Mr Rann said. The ALP's policy convention opposed the ETSA sale, and Party members who vote against official Party platform can face disciplinary action and even expulsion.

Mr Cameron believes his position is strengthened because the NSW Labor Premier, Bob Carr, is trying to sell that State's power utilities.

"If the case has been made out by the Premier of New South Wales and the Treasurer (Mr Egan) to sell off their electricity assets is a compelling one, then one can draw their own conclusions from an economic analysis of South Australia."

Several MLC's believe Mr Cameron was ‘quite impressed' after a briefing from the director of Adelaide University's Centre for Economic Studies, Professor Cliff Walsh, who is a strong advocate for the SA sale.

Mr Cameron has been keeping the Labor Party nervous with his increasingly open support for the benefits of privatisation, but last night he was unequivocal.

He said that based on the information he had seen, it would be in the best economic interests of SA for ETSA to be sold.

Mr Olsen's already tenuous hold on the Premiership would come under even more pressure if he could not secure passage of the ETSA Bills. It could be the twig that breaks the dromedary's back.

But even if Mr Cameron votes with the Government, Mr Olsen still needs the support of No Pokies MLC Nick Xenephon, who has so far declined to say how he'll vote.

Last night, the Leader of the Government in the Upper House, Treasurer Rob Lucas, said he was not yet convinced Mr Cameron would break Party ranks.

"Terry Cameron's shown some leadership on this but there's a long way to go yet before the legislation's passed and there's a long way to go yet for even Terry Cameron to move from his acknowledgment of the benefits of the sale of ETSA and Optima to a position where he might support the Government on a vote," Mr Lucas said.

Labor sources last night were also saying that while Mr Cameron is openly advocating the benefits of the sale, this did not guarantee Mr Cameron would eventually vote for the Bills.
