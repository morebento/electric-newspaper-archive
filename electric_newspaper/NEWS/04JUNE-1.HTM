Thursday June 4, 1998

Thar's gold in them thar Hills
by Hendrik Gout

A few late nights have been had over at Planning SA where everyone is getting excited about a possible Ministerial intervention to allow the Adelaide Hills Face to be used for a boldly commercial venture.

As we know, Andrew Garrett wants to build a hotel, restaurant, accommodation complex, and grape plantation on the famous Adelaide Hills escarpment overlooking the city of Adelaide.

Proper zoning laws saw the council reject a far smaller proposal by the same Andrew Garrett two years ago.

Since then public land adjacent to Mr Garrett's already large holdings slipped off the for sale list, and the buyer is understood to be Andrew Garrett.

The Electric Newspaper was unable to contact Mr Garrett overnight. Mr Garrett is understood to be overseas.

In Parliament yesterday, Democrat Leader Mike Elliott asked the Minister for planning, Di Laidlaw, what value she placed on the Hills Face Zone. She didn't say, but she did explain that the planning process is certainly not being circumvented.

It's in the Act.

Anyone can approach the Minister and ask for a declaration that their baby is a major development. Such a declaration means exemption from zoning laws, but not from the planning process. Exemptions are part of the planning process, you see.

It's in the Act.

Mr Elliott also asked whether the Minister felt the Hills Face Zone was sufficiently eroded, or was she prepared to undertake further erosion.

The Minister quite rightly called this inflammatory. Inflammation is not part of the Act.

So at this moment, planning office staff are seeing whether this gynormous thing meets the criteria, set down, to be accepted for major development status.

The Department of Environment, whose voice has grown pretty soft through a succession of weak Ministers, is also following the future of the Hills Face, but we were told last night that it may not have a big imput into this decision.

The Premier is listening to it from within Premier's Department. He has considerable interest in the subject.

While down in the suburbs, the local Mitcham City Council doesn't have much of a clue what's happening. Last time the folks down there heard, they'd successfully knocked back Mr Garrett's few grape vines. Now it's a much more ambitious project. The question is, is it a Major Project?

(If Mr Garrett is still overseas when the answer comes, he can read it here on the Net.)
