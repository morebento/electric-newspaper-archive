Tuesday July 21, 1998

3,000 people now feared dead in PNG disaster

The death toll from the tidal wave that devastated northern Papua New Guinea is likely to rise to 3,000.

West Sepik provincial governor John Tekwie said this estimate was based on the number of bodies recovered so far and the number of people seen still hiding in the jungle.

Mr Tekwie is assisting with the relief operation from Vanimo, close to the region struck by three tidal waves late on Friday.

Witness say the 10-metre waves, triggered by two major earthquakes, sounded like a fleet of bombers as they crashed into a 30-kilometre stretch of coast, wiping out seven heavily populated villages and leaving an estimated 6,000 people homeless.

Mr Tekwie said it had been thought thousands had taken refuge in the high ground, but he now believed there were 500 or less still hiding in the bush.

"I've had a look and all there is is bodies," he said.

"The stench of the dead is overpowering."

The dead were being buried where they lay because of the intense heat and because the dismembered remains were being eaten by animals.

Four helicopters were working frantically Monday to ferry the injured still stranded near their destroyed villages.

"It's great that the world knows what has happened," Mr Tekwie said. "It's a horrific catastrophe."

The three hospitals in the area were overwhelmed with injured Monday but doctors said they were coping with the demands.

Many of the injured, lying under tarpaulins and on hospital floors, told horrific stories of how they survived the black wall of water that engulfed them.

One heartbroken father, Bonney Sule, said he had been standing outside his house in the village of Arok with his son and pregnant wife when they heard the rumbling that they first thought was a warplane.

"The sea came and we didn't know what it was," he said. "Some people went to the beach to see and they were caught up in the wave. Those who went to the sea all died."

Sule was swept into the lagoon behind his village of Arok which was completely destroyed. He lost his wife, but was able to save his three-year-old son.
