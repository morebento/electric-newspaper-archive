Tuesday May 5, 1998


M.U.A. members, Mark Welsh, Jeff Peterson, Neil Harvey, Kevin Jarman and Robert Hokin at Patrick's office in Port Adelaide, celebrating todays decision.
Adelaide wharfies celebrate prematurely

There were cheers and shouts as more than 300 waterside workers and their supporters heard the news at Port Adelaide yesterday of the High Court's decision ordering Patrick's 1400-strong sacked workforce to be reinstated.

Elected officials of other unions crowded into the camera shots to be seen victorious on television. Labor Leader Mike Rann made an impassioned speech. State Maritime Union secretary Rick Newlyn gained admittance to Patrick's offices.

But not one of the men sacked a month ago are back in paid work.

The High Court has left the administrator of Patrick's four shell labour companies with the immediate decision as to whether or not to take the men back. The companies were deliberately sent into liquidation when the men were sacked.

There is no guarantee that all or any of the men will be taken back despite the High Court ruling.

Nationally, the Maritime Union says it's a question of “all or none”. It may be the latter.

The labor force companies' administrators say that before it takes men back, the union will have to accept forced redundancies. Non-union labor will also be taken onto the payroll - if the companies resume trading.

Patrick may not re-start its Adelaide operations. If not, their work may be transferred to P&O or Sea-land.

The Federal Government, which set aside a fistful of taxpayer dollars to fund redundancy payments to assist Patrick to sack its men, may not make that money available to re-hire the men. Federal Workplace Relations Minister Peter Reith, who is zealously determined to crush the M.U.A., will meet the companies' administrators in Canberra today to find a way of keeping the men out of work.
