Friday July 31, 1998

No time to say hello, see ya!

The Electric Newspaper is seven months old this week. It's now been seven months of hard work. In that time, we've averaged almost 50,000 hits a week. That's some 200,000 readers a month. Not bad.

And now it's time for a little R&R. Editor and publisher Hendrik Gout is taking a few weeks annual leave, heading for places warm and sunny. He'll send The Electric Newspaper a postcard but expects most of the office to be out to lunch. In the meanwhile, Chris Joyner and Ben Moretti are working to get the new VCE - a powerful web engine being developed in Adelaide - and our new(ish) Mac up for our return. We hope you'll like it. And our columnists - the wine, literature, film, sporting, and political commentators - will sharpen their quills for a new assault.

We could say we'll miss getting up at 4 a.m. these winter mornings, but we won't. We could say we'll miss the 18-hour days in front of the terminal... but we won't!

Have a flick through our past stories - you get there by pressing the icons on the left - there's plenty of reading to keep you going.

We will miss your (sometimes contradictory) feedback, your rousing on us when we make a blue or when we've missed a story, your always helpful suggestions about how to make this a better paper, and your generous praise when you think we've done ok.

But if you think we're going to think of that for the blink of an instant while we're scampering into the sunset these holidays, forget it!

To celebrate our success so far, and to give us a bit of cheer for the future... we're getting together for a few drinks tonight. We'll see you when we're back! Take care, and we hope you're looking forward to our return.

Hendrik Gout
on behalf of all The Team
