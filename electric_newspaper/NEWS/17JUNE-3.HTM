Mighty Murray gobsmack

The mouth of the mighty Murray River is just about closed.

It will be the first time since 1981 that Australia's longest and most important river system does not flow to the sea.

The once-proud Murray is now choked along its length by a series of dams and locks which stop the normal seasonal flow. But even more damaging to the natural life of the river is the tremendous amount of water drawn off for irrigation.

The flow has now been reduced to a mere trickle - not enough to flush away the sand which is constricting the river's mouth.

Where once it was dangerous - if not impossible - to cross the mouth in a small boat, today it's possible to wade across. Such is the effect of drawing off so much water upstream.
