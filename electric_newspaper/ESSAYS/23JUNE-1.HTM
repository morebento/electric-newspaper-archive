Tuesday June 23, 1998

High rolling with the punches
by John Rau

In amongst the tobacco smoke, flashing lights and jingling change of Australia's countless gambling establishments, you won't find John Howard. Unlike his great supporter Kerry, John does not have a reputation as a "high-roller". This may however have to be re-assessed. John Howard may yet be shaping up as the biggest "high-roller" this country has ever seen.

John Howard's economic policy agenda is a good example. Mr Howard has pursued policies which take our collective economic future to the Global Casino. Mr Howard’s relentlessd devotion to economic rationalism has delivered our nation's future to currency speculators. For these people, our dollar is just another chip to be tossed at the roulette wheel. Speculators contribute nothing, yet Mr Howard is content to let these unelected people gamble with our future.

Of course, gambling with other people's money is easy. Taking a big personal risk is another. In the Mel Brooks classic "Blazing Saddles", an angry mob are about to lynch the town's black sheriff when suddenly, the sheriff pulls his gun. He puts it to his temple. The crowd hushes. "Anyone moves and the nigger gets it!" shouts the sheriff. He escorts himself to safety at gun-point. His bluff works.

John Howard is engaged in a similar exercise. His is a political gamble, in which he could blow his head off if his bluff is called. To play this game Mr Howard must be either foolish, or desperate.

The game goes like this. One Nation is threatening to virtually wipe out the National Party. It also threatens to seriously weaken the Liberal Party. To openly oppose One Nation and to direct preferences to Labor instead, will cost votes in the bush. The National Party will not do this.

To put Labor last will disgust urban Liberal voters in Sydney and Melbourne. It will deliver Liberal seats to Labor, just as it did in Brisbane.

John Howard has to stick to his Wik 10-point plan. If he caves in, he will undermine what remains of the National Party vote in rural NSW and QLD. He would be seen as weak. This means that he is locked into a double-dissolution election if his Wik Bill is not passed by the Senate.

Just to make things more difficult, he has also locked himself into promising a nasty new tax on everything that moves (and a lot of things that don't) and the sale of the remainder of Telstra, neither of which are popular in the bush.

He has staked everything on one card, Brian Harradine. Perhaps he already has a deal with the Tasmanian Senator. In a way this would be re-assuring. The political theatre now unfolding would all just be an elaborate but calculated stunt to make him look decisive. But what if John Howard really is gambling on Senator Harradine "blinking" first ?

The most likely scenario still is that this is an elaborate hoax designed to make John Howard look good or, that a compromise will be found. Any compromise would no doubt involve more "cargo" for Tasmania, just like the Telstra deal. This would save face for John Howard and Senator Harradine. This would let Mr. Howard out of his double-dissolution bind. This would give Mr. Howard time to sell the GST shit sandwich to a hostile electorate. It would give him time to discredit One Nation. It would give the National Party a chance of survival beyond the next election.

If Senator Harradine does not come to the party, John Howard is in a lot of trouble. In "Blazing Saddles", the Sheriff got away without having his bluff called. John Howard may not be so lucky.
