Wednesday July 8, 1998

Too little, too late
by John Rau

Former deputy Premier Graham Ingerson has at last grudgingly acknowledged that he is not up to the job. In doing so, he is probably the last person in South Australia to work this out. His ham-fisted conduct has been an endless source of embarrassment and distraction to a Government struggling to survive on a day to day basis.

The really irritating aspect of this most recent drama is that Mr. Ingerson obviously thinks that he is hard done by. Mr. Ingerson is the subject of an unprecedented Parliamentary inquiry. The allegation is that he, as Minister for Racing, sought to improperly influence the SA Thoroughbred Racing Association by contacting a member of the authority, Mr Hodge. To compound this, he is alleged to have "misled" (a gentle word) Parliament in the answers he gave to several well targeted questions from Labor frontbencher, Kevin Foley.

If Mr Ingerson is found to have sought to influence Mr Hodge and to have misled Parliament, he is not fit to be a Minister. Mr Howard has been prepared to enforce standards at this level in Canberra. Why are there different standards here? The answer of course is that the Premier is clinging to office by his fingernails. He is scared to do the right thing and sack Mr Ingerson because he fears that this will bring him down . As a result, South Australians must endure not only a third rate State Government, but a smelly one at that.

Mr Olsen came to office with blood on his hands. He now has the choice of spilling Mr Ingerson's and showing some guts, or wimping out and enduring the stench until he is removed in a coup.
