Thursday April 30, 1998

Roxby Downs - the world's biggest mirage
by Legh Davis, Liberal Member in the Legislative Council

Remember John Bannon, Premier of South Australia? And who could forget Mike (Also) Rann, current Leader of the Labor Party? They were both dead wrong on the two biggest things that have happened in South Australia in the last 20 years.

Firstly, they both thought Tim Marcus Clark was crash hot and would not hear a word against him. Rann is famous for his inspired and spirited defence of Marcus Clark when the Liberal Party were vociferously voicing their concerns about the State Bank.

As we all know, there are 3.15 billion reasons to say the Liberals were right and Bannon and Rann were wrong.

Secondly, 15 years ago when the debate about the development of Roxby Downs was in full swing, Bannon claimed that Roxby Downs was "a mirage in the desert". Rann gave his full-blooded support to Bannon’s negative view on Roxby. In 1982 Rann published a 31 page booklet campaigning against Roxby, in which he claimed, "To put it crudely, the Roxby partners had Premier Tonkin over a barrel and the indenture publicity, full of ifs, rather than whens, smacked of a political stunt." To make the point, Rann then wrote, "No serious commentators are now likely to join the Premier (Tonkin) in trumpeting the economic impact of Roxby." Could you run that by us again Mike?

Well Roxby Downs is 560 kilometres north of Adelaide and maybe out of sight and out of mind for most Merv and Melba Metros. But at the risk of actually reporting some good news, (Terry Plane please take note!) readers may be interested to know what a magnificent contribution Roxby has made to the South Australian economy.

The ore body was first discovered in 1975 and it took 13 years to bring the project to fruition. $1 billion was spent on developing the Olympic Dam mine and infrastructure, which included the attractive township of Roxby Downs, 16 kilometres south of the mine.

At present there is a massive $1.5 billion expansion of the copper, gold and uranium mine, which is expected to generate an additional 5,500 jobs in the construction phase. When the expansion is completed in two years, 1,100 new permanent jobs will be created and the multiplier effect will result in thousands more. This project is currently the largest private sector development anywhere in Australia and will boost the population of Roxby Downs to more than 4,000.

The annual value of products exported from Roxby is around $300 million. The State Government receives annual royalties of over $10 million and the Olympic Dam mine attracted about 8,000 visitors last year.

So, both Bannon and Rann were absolutely wrong on the two biggest things which have happened in South Australia in the last two decades, Roxby Downs and the State Bank. But you won’t read about that in their curricula vitae, so I thought I should tell you.

Meanwhile Rann is facing a continuing and bloody battle between The Machine and the recently formed Independent faction. You might remember that I wrote about this recently, although of course the matter has not been seriously reported in mainstream media. Could someone please tell Terry Plane?

But after getting both Roxby and the State Bank dramatically wrong, how confident could anyone be about Rann getting the Labor Party right?
