Monday May 4, 1998

Why I marched today
by Bridget Bannear

The Mums and Dads of the anti-boat harbour campaign joined the biggest ever Adelaide May Day march on Saturday.

We were protesting against the fairly inevitable construction of a massive rock groyne at West Beach which will protect boat launching facilities to be provided there. Current facilities at Glenelg will be displaced by a private residential development being undertaken by the Holdfast Shores Consortium.

We think there is a huge body of evidence to predict that the groyne will cause beach degradation to the north, and the boat harbour will cost hundreds of thousands of dollars to the taxpayers every year to maintain it in a useable state.

I joined joined the march because it's a day of protest, to highlight the links between community campaigns and the Union movement and because it adds to the litany of bullying currently underway by Governments.

Our Big Picture concerns are that the State Government is unwilling and perhaps incapable of negotiating win-win solutions with the people who elected them.

They are elected representatives who refuse to represent. They have no mandate to give away public land and facilities, to wreck beaches or to incur ongoing financial liabilities to future generations of South Australians. There are perfectly reasonable alternatives that would satisfy everyone's wishes.

In their bid to attract development and growth to the State, the Government is rolling over and taking whatever the developers want to dish out.. No demand is too great. What a responsible Government would do is to listen to the concerns of the people and negotiate with the developers to come up with a really clever solution.

We need much better consultation processes where people with concerns can have meaningful input before everything is set in concrete. We need respectful processes that don't wind up in confrontation. To date we have been conned, patronised, dismissed, ridiculed and undermined. At no stage have we had true consultation, let alone an EIS on the plan.

There are people in our community who know a great deal about the coast and the effect obstructions have on our beaches. They are very well informed, love the beach and have the State's best interests at heart. They would not be wasting their time and effort on this campaign if they were not extremely concerned.

We are holding a public rally today, Sunday 3 May at 11am in the Henley Town Hall, corner of Seaview Road and North Street Henley Beach to discuss the future of the campaign. If you can spare an hour, be there.
