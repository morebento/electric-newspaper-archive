Monday June 29, 1998

Truth is in the "I" of the beholder
by John Rau

Isn't it funny how conventional wisdom changes over time ? In the bad old Soviet Union private capital was evil. The State was the beneficial owner of all property on behalf of the people.

This was the logical outcome of great theoretical concepts espoused by Marx and Lenin. It was generally accepted that the Western Democracies were on a path of moral and economic decay. This was the honestly held view of the vast majority of citizens. Sure, they had been influenced by a State-controlled media and dissent could be dangerous. But this was the prevailing view. Anyone looking at the new Russia, run by gangsters and thugs, might be excused for looking back fondly to the old days.

In Germany another conventional wisdom was promoted by similar coercive means. Most citizens accepted that Germany had been "stabbed in the back" by cowards and communists at the end of the first World War. Most citizens accepted Hitler's program to restore prosperity and National pride.

During the cold-war, it was dangerous for an Australian to publicly embrace a view at odds with the Bob Menzies world view. One risked being branded as a "communist". Once branded as a communist, a citizen was marginalised and excluded. Eventually, anyone with whom one disagreed could be labelled as a communist and vilified. This was very handy. One did not have to consider the issue at hand. One did not have to muster arguments. By attaching the label "communist " to an opponent, the opponent was reduced to the status of a dangerous fool. Arguments were not won, opponents were destroyed.

Political correctness is the name that is now given to this well-established shabby political technique. During the last decade or so, social trendies have taken to using it. They have done this to the exclusion of conducting any debate. Any idea which is at all challenging, is as a matter of course, derided as "sexist" or "racist". At long last this fraud on the democratic process is being exposed. In the arena of social policy the exposure is well advanced but it is still with us.

The last bastion of political correctness is economics. This is the field in which political correctness is most deeply entrenched. It is as if economic theory has been elevated to the level of a "truth". What is this great "truth" ? That there is an international "level playing field". That the "market" will deliver equitable solutions to the eternal problem of dividing the cake. That "globalism" is unstoppable, inevitable and good for Australia.

This great economic "truth" is as all pervasive in Australia today as the Soviet and German "truths" of the 1930's were in their time. It is every bit as flawed. If nothing is done, this country is headed for real trouble. Increasingly our Governments have handed over sovereignty to speculators and private corporations with no vested interest in our people. The public are sick of it and are looking for a voice.

If One Nation persists in refusing to play the political game, we may yet see a debate about these "truths". The established parties still have no idea what is emerging all around them. They still think in terms of the comfortable mutual silence on major issues which has squashed public debate. The Queensland election was a warning. Our political system is poised to implode.
