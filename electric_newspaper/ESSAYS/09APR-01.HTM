Thursday April 9, 1998

People Power Opposes Pokies for Parkland Pub
by Nick Xenophon, Independent M.L.C

A protest meeting attended by over 50 patrons of the Regency Tavern was held on Wednesday night, to oppose the proposed installation of 40 pokies at the Tavern.

The Regency Tavern, situated at Days Road, Regency Park, was built 20 years ago as part of The Parks Urban Renewal Program, and overlooks a golf course, parkland and a lake. It is one of the few meeting places for residents in an area which many regard as being under significant socio-economic pressure - with a 67% rate of public housing and a 24% unemployment rate.

The charge against the pokies has been led by local identity, and former 60's and 70's Port Adelaide footballer Ron Elleway. Mr Elleway has lodged a formal objection with the Liquor and Gaming Commissioner over the proposal.

Mr Elleway told the meeting that poker machines would block the unique views from the Tavern and destroy its community based atmosphere.

"The Tavern's Patrons Club of which I am a member, is overwhelmingly opposed to the installation of poker machines into the Tavern," Mr Elleway said

"There are a number of regulars at the Tavern - including people who have become hooked on pokies elsewhere - who go to the Tavern because it's a pokies free zone.

"The suburbs around the Tavern are struggling enough as it is, without having pokies making it worse."

People power managed to succeed in the fight to keep pokies out of the historic Dover Castle Hotel at North Adelaide just last month. The Regency Tavern case is in many respects much more important, because many local residents won't have alternative pokies free facilities to get to easily if machines are installed at Regency Tavern.

Regency Tavern will be the major test case to determine the extent to which patrons at a hotel can use the current provisions of the Gaming Machines Act to prevent pokies being installed.

An interim decision from the Liquor and Gaming Commissioner, Mr Bill Pryor, is expected to be handed down in the next two weeks.
