Monday June 15, 1998

Exposing one's self
by John Rau

When I was a child I remember being told the story of the "Emperor's new clothes". I remember laughing. Even as a child, I could see the obvious stupidity of the Emperor's subjects. The story suggested more than just sucking up to those in authority. It suggested the suspension of reason in a desperate attempt to conform. But this was just a child's story. It could not really happen in the world of grown-ups. Or could it ?

During the last decade or so, media outlets and political figures on all sides have become captivated by "experts". These self-styled visionaries generally have qualities in common. They usually have a university education. Often, this is in one of the "new" disciplines which have proliferated in step with the chaotic expansion of tertiary education. They congregate in coffee shops. They live in trendy suburbs. They have read a little, about a lot.

These "experts" and foolish political figures have been playing out the story of the Emperor's new clothes. We have been their subjects. They have forced us to don the cloak of economic rationalism. They have swaddled us in minority politics. They have shod us in the concrete boots of internationalism.

Both of the major parties are now in a mode of advanced denial. They still refuse to see that the Emperor isn't wearing any clothes and never was. They respond to criticism with irrational invective and abuse instead of confronting the facts.

Pauline Hanson's "One Nation" made history over the weekend. One Nation came from no-where to a position of real political power in one of Australia's largest and richest States. This is not the product of a few gun-loony's. This is not evidence that 25 or 30% of Queenslanders are racists. This is evidence that One Nation alone is seen to be saying what millions of decent Australians feel.

To ridicule One Nation in the language of Political Correctness as "racist" is to attack 30% of the electorate as racist. These attacks are like water off a duck's back. They give One Nation strength. They confirm One Nation's view of itself as a lone voice. These attacks are as petrol on the flame.

The message for the established Parties is clear. You are not looking after Australians. You are more interested in looking good at an IMF cocktail party than in providing job security for your people. You are more interested in economic purity than you are in real people's lives. You are on the cusp of irrelevance.

Wake up!
