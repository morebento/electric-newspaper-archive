Friday July 31, 1998

Up jumped the squatter
by Ralph Clarke

This week I was in Mount Gambier for a few days, campaigning for the Labor Party's Federal candidate for the seat of Barker, David Detchon, and I was surprised by the depth of ill feeling that exists amongst the residents of Mount Gambier, and the South East, generally towards their absentee Federal MP Ian McLachlan.

Ian McLachlan won the blue ribbon Liberal Federal seat of Barker in the 1990 election.

Mr McLachlan disposed a sitting Liberal MP, Mr James Porter, in a preselection battle in the lead up to the 1990 Federal election, and was touted in the 1980s as a future possible Liberal Prime Minister. Mr McLachlan was a very high profile president of the National Farmers Federation, and established his anti-union credentials as the head of that organisation in some particularly bitter industrial confrontations with the Australian Meat Industry Employees Union, both in South Australia and elsewhere.

It would be true to say that Ian McLachlan is amongst the driest of Liberal Federal MPs.

He has always favoured the complete phase-out of tariff protection for Australia's automotive industry and for the textile and clothing and footwear industry, not withstanding the damage that would have done to his own constituents working for Fletcher Jones in Mount Gambier.

Whilst in Mount Gambier with David Detchon, I had the opportunity of meeting a number of journalists and business people and other community leaders, who all expressed their deep resentment towards Mr McLachlan. He is clearly perceived to be totally out of touch with the average person, as well as arrogant and high-handed in his dealings with them. This can be clearly demonstrated by the fact that even though his electorate of Barker covers all of the south east of South Australia, his actual electorate office is on the 12th floor of the Commonwealth Bank Building in King William Street, Adelaide. As I said in an interview with the local media in Mount Gambier, "Mr McLachlan needs a road map to find Mount Gambier."

There is talk of a One Nation candidate receiving considerable support in Barker. However, in my view Mr McLachlan is more at risk from other Independents such as Mr Tony Beck, a farmer in the electorate, then he may be from One Nation. It is known that Mr Beck may be able to count on the support of State Liberal Independents, Rory McEwen and Mitch Williams, the Members for Gordon and MacKillop respectively, whose electorates are entirely within the Federal Seat of Barker.

Mr McLachlan's arrogance has been highlighted by the fact that on a number of occasions when there have been articles critical of him in the local Boarder Watch Newspaper, he has taken to telephoning the journalist concerned, including junior journalists, and berating them for daring to write articles which are critical of him.

On local ABC radio recently he also complained they were beginning to sound like the Boarder Watch, in the news items they were carrying about his effectiveness as a local Member.

Mr McLachlan and the Liberal Party had the Seat of Barker with something like a 20 per cent party preferred margin, and therefore it is highly unlikely that Labor's candidate David Detchon, a 28 year-old lawnmowing contractor from Goolwa, is going to wrest the Seat from him at this election. However, an astute political campaign by Labor in Barker, adding fuel to the fire around the perceptions that Mr McLachlan is an arrogant, aloof Federal Member, totally indifferent to the needs of the local electorate could well assist an Independent in unseating him.

David Detchon is to be commended for the efforts that he is putting in to his election campaign. It is well known the ALP has virtually no funds within the Barker electorate and the Party Office is not going to, as it sees, it waste money in a blue ribbon Liberal seat. Therefore he has to rely on his own shoe leather and to campaing at his own expense, in forsaking his own time earning an income for himself and his young family (which includes four young children), to visit Mount Gambier and elsewhere, standing on streets and arcades in the main population centres handing out leaflets introducing himself to potential constituents.

On election night, Barker could well surprise political commentators. It has the same ingredients as that which existed in the electoral upset that occurred in the South East at the State election in October 1997, when the two Independent Liberal candidates for MacKillop and Gordon won on Labor Party preferences, in formerly rock solid safe State Liberal Seats.

From my perspective as a Labor MP and a former Trade Union official, I certainly have no love for the ideology of Ian McLachlan. It would give me a great sense of vindication for any "born-to-rule squatter" to be tossed out of the Federal Parliament by his own kind.
