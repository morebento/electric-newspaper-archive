Tuesday April 21, 1998

Peter Reith: what does Mr Hyde have to hide?
By John Rau

The current waterfront dispute is destined to have few winners.

The Federal Government has been itching to rough up the MUA since the Liberal Party won the election in March 1996.

For those who had better things to do with their lives than observe Australian politics, Peter Reith seemed to appear from no-where as Industrial Relations Minister in John Howard's new Government.

Pete had a nice hair-do for a chap who is a bit thin on top. He smiled a lot. He wore large spectacles and looked like a Kid's Show host. He reminded some of us of our old household friend, Mr. Sheen. He seemed benign and reasonable.

Then, Pete did all that great TV with Saint Cheryl. He beamed as they declared Industrial "Peace in our time". This was his triumph. His Industrial Relations Bill .The sky seemed the limit. Pete was PM material, or so it seemed.

Years before, a very much less endearing Pete walked amongst us. He was the then Opposition's Terminator. He displayed breathtaking rudeness when interviewed. He talked over his opponents. He almost foamed at the mouth saying foolish, intellectually dishonest things about referenda proposed by the then Government. He was like an opposition mad dog, unleashed on sensitive political targets.

When the new warm and fuzzy Pete was wheeled out on to the tarmac for the first time in 1996, those with a memory of the old Pete smelt a rat.

Perhaps Pete had an evil alter-ego, a "Mr. Hyde". Perhaps this alter ego had suppressed the warm lovable man locked inside him for all those years.

Perhaps just before the 1996 election, exhausted on a diet of conflict, this evil alter-ego had vanished, allowing the warm, lovable Pete out again.

Well, if the last few weeks are any indication, its no more Mr. Nice Guy. The "Mr. Hyde" is back in charge.

Pete's problem is that he did some things during his warm and fuzzy phase which may come back to haunt him. Worst of all, he proudly sponsored legislation making it illegal to terminate a person's employment because they are in a Union.

In the last year or so, the Government has made numerous attempts to induce individuals and companies to pick a fight with the MUA. Now the fight has started, with the dirty work being done by proxy on the Government's behalf.

The Government is giving encouragement and promises of financial support to provoke an escalation of the conflict. The strategy is classic Reith. Stir the protagonists up to a point where the ACTU or the MUA does something stupid, then hit them with every weapon in the legal arsenal.

Never mind that Pete's proxy had to use elaborate legal arrangements to attempt to keep clear of Pete's own law protecting Unionists. According to Pete, it is the Union that is acting unlawfully. This is a bit like a firebug who lights a bushfire and then joins in attempts to put out the blaze. The difference is that in this case Pete is still urging more fuel to be thrown on the flames.

A Government should be trying to sort this out, not provoking more outrage.

Perhaps Pete has a strategy that links this dispute and his ambition to beat that other well known Pete to the Lodge. Perhaps the strategy is like this:

Stir up a dispute on the waterfront-escalate it . Goad the ACTU into action which can be portrayed as radical and dangerous Goad the ALP into the ever -widening vortex of conflict. Give John Howard an election issue - a scare campaign about Union power-go to the polls, 1960's style. Wait for John Howard to win an historic victory - claim to be the principal architect of victory. Stake a claim on the top job as a reward for services rendered. Manipulate a stab in the back for John Howard - hopefully appearing to come from the Costello Camp. Cry crocodile tears for the fallen leader, then step over Costello and into the Lodge.

This dispute is now entering a very unpredictable phase. Pete is playing for high stakes. The MUA has a lot riding on the outcome. The MUA will not go quietly like a lamb to the slaughter. If this goes terribly wrong, Pete may wind up finishing himself off in the process .

The PM has a responsibility to acknowledge the tainted conduct of Pete's proxy and to ensure that the law is honoured, on all sides.
