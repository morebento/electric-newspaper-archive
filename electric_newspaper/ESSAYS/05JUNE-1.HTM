Friday June 5, 1998

Roxby's allright, Jack
by Legh Davis, Liberal Member in the Legislative Council

Jack Hoogland on June 2 nearly fused The Electric Newspaper with an extraordinary contribution on Roxby Downs.

Jack's main beef seemed to be that Roxby Downs, owned and operated by WMC, was indeed a "mirage in the desert". This was the phrase which John Bannon, as Leader of the Opposition, had been proud to call his own when he vehemently opposed the development of Roxby Downs in 1982. The Hoogland article also paraded some ideological and inaccurate claptrap about uranium, which totally ignored the facts.

Seventy-five percent of revenue from Olympic Dam is derived from copper, 5% from gold and silver and only 20% from uranium. Mr Hoogland claimed there were only 946 direct jobs relating to production in Roxby Downs, well below the forecast 2,200 or more jobs. Mr Hoogland neglects to mention the accepted industry multiplier effect of 3 to 1 means there are another 2,000-3,000 people in the wider community employed as a result of Olympic Dam. He also fails to mention 2,000 people were employed in the first $800 million construction of Olympic Dam (1986-88) and that more than 2,500 jobs have been created during the current $1.64 billion expansion programme, with even more jobs flowing from the multiplier effect.

Mr Hoogland laments that the value of production estimated over 15 years ago at $1,200 million reached "only $350 million" by 1997. Does he really expect a mine to crank up to full capacity from day one? The company's current environmental impact statement provides an approval to produce 350,000 tonnes per year of copper, which will result in the value of production he is talking about. Presumably if that target is reached, he will claim the mine is too big!

WMC is currently paying the State Government about $12 million per year in royalties and when the current expansion is completed in 1999, royalty payments will increase by the same ratio as the extra products produced.

The Olympic Dam legislation passed Parliament only through the bravery of Labor defector, Norm Foster. It required the State Government to spend $50 million on public infrastructure. This amount has not quite been spent but the State has already received back over $60 million in royalties, more than it has paid out in infrastructure spending.

There will be long lasting benefits to South Australia from Olympic Dam. Mr Hoogland doesn't want to spoil his story so he does not mention the ore body at Olympic Dam consists of more than 560 million tonnes of ore, which will provide 60 100 years of mining life, even at the expanded rate of production. Further exploration seems certain to dramatically increase the economic life of one of the world's great underground mines.

Mr Hoogland ignores the other significant economic and intangible benefits which have flowed from this project. For example, 80% of the current $1.64 billion expansion programme is being spent in South Australia. At Port Augusta and Whyalla pre-assembly facilities have been established by WMC, which employ 240 locals to prefabricate items for the Olympic Dam expansion.

Anyone who has visited Roxby Downs (does that include Mr Hoogland?) could not fail to be impressed by WMC's commitment to the environment. There has been an enormous amount of environmental knowledge of arid Australia gained by its committed environmental team. This has been recognised with national environmental awards.

The design of Roxby Downs, its housing and public amenities, have been widely applauded by town planners. Roxby Downs has won two consecutive Tidy Towns awards and when the current expansion phase is complete, it is expected to boast a population of close to 4,000.

Recently, publicity was given to the fact that the annual income per capita at Roxby Downs was the highest of any town or suburb in South Australia and one of the highest in Australia.

Mr Hoogland apparently lives and works in South Australia. South Australians have been fed with a diet of negative news in recent years, much of it stemming from the massive $4 billion of losses racked up by the Bannon Labor Government through State Bank, SGIC, and Scrimber. Just remember the Labor Government's memorable quinella - Labor was against Roxby Downs and ignored the Liberal Party's warnings over two years about problems at the State Bank.

It's a bit rich when Mr Hoogland shafts a mining venture which is internationally acclaimed and bills Roxby as a bad news story. From John Bannon's mirage in the desert 15 years ago, to a population of 4,000 and massive export income for South Australia, should score at least 8 out of 10 with most unbiased observers. So, if Roxby Downs is bad news for Mr Hoogland, he will be positively frothing with adjectives when describing the State Bank, SGIC and Scrimber losses. I look forward to Happy Hoogland's contribution on this subject in the coming weeks.
