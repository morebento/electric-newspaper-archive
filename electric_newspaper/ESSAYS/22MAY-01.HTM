Friday May 22, 1998

Coming soon: a public health disaster in your suburb
by John Rau

The Labor administrations of Bob Hawke and Paul Keating succeeded in removing the issue of public health from the front line of political controversy. Perhaps this is why people forget that this was not always the case.

The introduction of a universal public health service was a major policy plank upon which Gough Whitlam was elected in 1972. Medibank was one of the real achievements of the Whitlam Government. Medibank was very popular with a public, sick of a mish-mash of private health insurance and uneven State public health systems.

Even Malcom Fraser was too scared to attack Medibank directly. He knew that this would be electoral poison. Instead, he set about white-anting Medibank and favoring the Private Health Funds. By 1983 Fraser had all but ruined the public health system.

When Bob Hawke was swept into office in 1983, he promised to restore the public health system. Medicare in its present form was born. Medicare was to be funded by a special levy on income set at 1.5%. The community has come to value and rely upon the medicare system. John Howard promised not to ruin it.

It has been revealed that only 31% of Australians now have private health insurance. This is yet another record low achieved by this Government. Most of the State Governments are still locked in a battle with Canberra over funding for their decaying public hospital systems. On any reckoning the Government's health policy, if it can be called that, is an abject failure.

We are witnessing a Government, dominated by bean counters, which is in the process of bleeding our public hospitals white. This same Government is scratching its head wondering why Australians are turning their back on private health. Surely, its theory must go, the gutting of the public system will drive desperate people into the grasping arms of the private funds.

Wrong!

Why would any sane Australian spend the vast sums of money required on private health insurance ? Let me illustrate with one simple, but typical example. A woman with private health insurance is having a baby. She can have a pivate bed. This may be in a public or private hospital. Remember, she has already paid the Medicare levy and some thousands of after-tax dollars to a private health fund. She must have been in the fund for at least a year just to qualify for maternity benefits. She may have been paying fees for years. If the birth is at all complicated, a public hospital is the only place to be. Public hospitals have the best equipment and staff.

When she enters the hospital, a public patient is in the next bed. She receives the same treatment. She pays nothing above and beyond the Medicare levy. The private patient gets the same care, in the same hospital. But, because she was silly enough to be privately insured, she pays the Medicare levy, PLUS extortionate private health fees, PLUS a huge gap payment of perhaps $500 at the end!

You would have to be rolling in money or a complete imbecile to touch the private insurance option with a barge poll!

If the Federal Government is serious about public health it will pump some money back into our public hospitals before they grind to a complete halt.

If the Federal Government is keen to see more people taking up Private insurance it will reduce the cost of insurance to the consumer, by direct subsidy if necessary. The endless spiral of cost increases must be reversed.

Finally, the Government must legislate to eliminate the obscene gap payments demanded from private patients.

Health is shaping up to be a big election issue for the first time in over a decade. Mr. Howard's tax reform package may well take a back seat in the face of this looming crisis. Make no mistake, once the wheels actually fall of the public health system, political heads will roll down the road.
