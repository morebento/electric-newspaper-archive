Friday May 8, 1998

Take your money and Rann
by Legh Davis, Liberal Member in the Legislative Council

The Labor Party leader, Mike (Also) Rann, loves to talk about his bipartisan approach to matters of moment. That bipartisanship was never evident when he was a Minister in the Bannon/Arnold Labor Governments. He laughed at the Liberal Party attacks on the State Bank. And in earlier days he sneered at Roxby Downs.

But just what does Mike Rann stand for? Well today he is against privatisation because he’s finished doing all the privatising that he wants to do. Yes, he was there when the Labor Party privatised the State Bank and Rann was larger than life when the Labor Government sold off its majority holding in the South Australian Gas Company. And where was Rann when Federal Labor was privatising the Commonwealth Bank, Qantas, and indirectly Australian Airlines and the Commonwealth Serum Laboratories? Any reader who can find a speech by Mike Rann attacking privatisation by the Federal and State Labor Governments will score from me not only a tour of Parliament House, but a lunch as well.

Nowadays, Mike doesn’t like privatisation. Why? Well he is a political chameleon. He is into political mayhem rather than bipartisanship.

Consider this. If you are keeper of the household purse-strings and suffer a massive and unexpected financial setback beyond your control, there would be three options. Firstly, you could prune your expenditure, secondly, you could increase your income and thirdly, you could borrow more to meet the gap between income and expenditure. Of course it may be a combination of all three. The members of the household may not like what has happened. They will grizzle and whine. But unless a disciplined financial approach is adopted, you run the risk of bankruptcy.

This is no different to what happened in South Australia. Under the Bannon/Arnold Labor Governments over $4 billion was lost. That is $4,000 million or around $4,500 for every person between the ages of 15 and 65 in South Australia. $3.15 billion was blown on the State Bank, around $800 million in SGIC (including $500 million on just one building in Melbourne!) and $60 million on the extraordinary Scrimber project.

In 1993 the Liberal Party Government inherited this problem and had to fix it. The Liberals were like the head of the household who had an unexpected financial setback. The voters have grizzled and whined. The Liberal Government has tried hard not to increase taxes and charges and yes, it has had to cut back on expenditure.

The Labor Party, led by Rann whose financial acumen I will not comment on because it may risk a defamation action, has suddenly become an expert in financial management. It is a bit like saying that infamous escapee, Brendan Abbott, should become the next Queensland Minister of Police!

Take SA Water for example. The Liberal Government has saved $10 million per annum on water charges following the outsourcing of the management of SA Water. These savings have been used in the areas of health and education. United Water’s management contract has led to an increase in jobs in the water and waste-water industry in South Australia. Rann presents this positive development in a bad light. But he doesn’t tell you that in the last year of the Labor Government, SA Water (EWS) lost $47 million. So just what was Rann’s policy on water? Well don’t bother asking because there wasn’t one.

The Liberal Government has committed itself to selling off ETSA and Optima. The National Electricity Market has introduced great uncertainties and high risks. The Carr Labor Government in New South Wales is adamant that it will privatise its electricity industry. But of course Rann is now in his post-privatisation period.

The successful privatisation of ETSA and Optima will slash $4-6 billion off the South Australian debt. It will give the Government a chance to be generous rather than mean. The long-suffering taxpayers of South Australia will start winning again.

When all is said and done, good financial management is about getting it right. Whatever one may say about the style and the problems of the Liberal Government, there have been no major financial failures or significant losses over the last four years as it has undertaken the hazardous task of repairing the enormous financial damage done by Labor. And most importantly of all, Mike Rann has not once come forward with an alternative financial plan. Perhaps that’s not so surprising in view of his track record.

Ask yourself this question. If you were the keeper of the purse-strings in the household which had suffered a dramatic financial setback, would you go to Mike (Also) Rann for advice?
