Friday July 10, 1998

Ground zero
by Sandra Kanck, Auistralian Democrat MLC

It seems almost a heresy to say it, and definitely not politically correct, but One Nation and the Australian Democrats have a common policy, which is that Australia should have zero net immigration.

By some strange set of circumstances the Australian Democrats and One Nation find agreement that the numbers of people moving in to the country should not exceed the numbers leaving. But it is on that point and that point alone that we agree.

How we got to that point and where we go from that point are vastly different.

Because the Democrats believe that, within those reduced numbers, refugees should be significantly increased above their current levels, Australia's immigrants would be far more likely to come from Asia, Africa and South America. One Nation would be unlikely to find themselves in agreement with us that a far greater proportion of our migrant intake should come from countries which are not culturally similar. And because of their refugee status, many of them would arrive in Australia unable to speak English. - heaven forbid!

By contrast One Nation's preference for immigrants who will fit in easily with white Anglo-Saxon ways is repugnant to the Democrats.

Just a few days ago the Australian Bureau of Statistics released its population projections for the year 2050: it predicted an Australian population of more than 26 million, which is of major concern from an environmental perspective. It is concern for the environmental impact which is the chief motivator for the Democrats population policy. By contrast One Nation doesn't like 'greenies'.

The consequence of any policy to decrease immigrants is the logical emergence of a congruent policy for an increase in Australia's foreign aid budget. Such a policy is anathema to the adherents of One Nation who want to stop all foreign aid, viewing such 'gifts' as occurring at the behest of the United Nations and the One World Government! (Don't laugh - some of them believe John Olsen is determined to sell ETSA and Optima because he was given the word by the UN!)

There are opportunities available for those who desire to help people in other countries who are less well off than ourselves. You can sponsor children in third world countries through groups like Plan International or World Vision (I have been doing this for more than 20 years and have sponsoring children in Bolivia, Colombia and currently in Vietnam).

Donate to/become involved in groups like Amnesty International and Community Aid Abroad, or become more political by assisting groups like Campaign for an Independent East Timor.

Choose to travel in third world countries if you go overseas and support their struggling economies. Join Australian Volunteers Abroad.

For those of us who are involved in these ways it is an expression of passionate concern for the individual, but it must not be allowed to become the Australian Government's cheap way out.

At a time of emergence and growth of a new and right-wing political force, there are some in the Democrats who would prefer that we hide the zero net immigration population policy for fear that we will somehow be aligned.

In my view, people should never resile from promoting a policy merely because of who you could line up with. Foe example, if one campaigns against violent pornography, one lines up with right-wing fundamentalist Christians. Oppose the death penalty and you are in the company of murderers.

Support equality and in whose company does one stand?
