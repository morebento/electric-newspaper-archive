Monday June 22, 1998

Hanson is as Hanson does
by Legh Davis, Liberal Member in the Legislative Council

This morning I received a fax from friends in the United States, who live in Germany but spend about half their time on the Sunshine Coast in Queensland. This handwritten note said, "Can't anyone explain to the Queensland population what damage the Hanson credo is doing to Australia and its reputation overseas?"

The fax was accompanied by a column from the highly respected magazine, The Economist, published just before the Queensland election on June 13. The Economist observed that Hanson's "campaign language draws heavily on a brand of right wing populism imported from America. She promises to abolish gun controls and to set up a rural bank to make loans to farmers at just 2% interest. She would pay for her promises by scrapping spending on various causes promoted by urban Australians, such as the arts, aborigines..."

The Economist also noted that Mrs Hanson's popularity has "tarnished Australia's image in Asia, where the former fish and chip shop proprietor is seen as representing the discredited ‘White Australia' policy. Queensland has been Australia's fastest growing State and receives much Asian investment in property and tourism."

Perceptions can be quickly created but take much longer to erase. It is a bit like an intricate stained glass window made up of many different pieces of glass and painstakingly crafted. But one stone from an errant child can smash many hours of labour.

As the twentieth century draws to a close, it can be fairly said that one of the nation's great achievements has been the bipartisan migration programme which has seen 5.6 million migrants settle in Australia since 1945. Nearly one in four Australians were born overseas and at least another one in five have one or more parents born overseas. In other words, around 43% (or 8 million) of the country's population of 18.6 million was either born overseas or have one or more parents born overseas. People from well over 100 countries now call Australia home. Succeeding waves of migrants from the United Kingdom, Italy, Greece, Germany, Holland and the Baltic States dominated our intake until the late 1970's. Since then New Zealanders, Asians, South Americans and South Africans have accounted for the bulk of our migrants.

In the 1990's our annual migration intake has averaged around 60,000 to 65,000, or only 0.35% of total population. This is in sharp contrast to 1950, when net migration to Australia was 152,500. This represented 1.8% of Australia's population in 1950 which was around 8.4 million. If we took the rate of intake in 1950 and had the same rate of intake in proportion to the population, our immigration would be now running at around 330,000 a year.

Pauline Hanson is ignorant and ill-informed. In 1996 she made headlines by claiming that Asians would account for 30% of the population by the year 2000 and 50% by the year 2040. However, the 1996 census reveals that people born in Asia represent 4.8% of the population. The Hanson figure was just pure fiction!

Notwithstanding the benefits of migration to Australia over the last 50 years, One Nation has a goal of slashing net migration to zero. This is populist nonsense.

In the last few days I have perused the policies of Pauline Hanson's One Nation party for the Queensland State election, as they appear on the Internet.

One Nation appeared to go to the polls with no coherent policies in transport and health and many other key areas. Its education policy of just a few paragraphs had as much substance as a mirage in the desert. Although this policy trumpeted that “all young Australians .... should ..... be taught Australian history, the meaning and purpose of our Constitution, our system of government, patriotism and loyalty to our country”, elsewhere One Nation stated it would save $0.56 million by abolishing the Queensland Centenary of Federation Committee which presumably would be planning to direct a significant sum of money to history projects.

Its economics was straight from the funny farm. To quote the policy, "Pauline Hanson's One Nation stands by the principle of developing a people's bank and .... will create the Queensland Trust." This Trust "is intended to assist farmers with a financial capacity for survival. On a case by case basis, funds will be made available at a flat fixed interest rate of 2%. ....New industry/manufacturing ventures will be provided with funds on a loan basis also at .... 2%. Monies will be available on a dollar for dollar basis for new ventures in city areas and a $2 for $1 basis for new ventures in rural areas. Funds will be made available to existing small businesses for the purposes of expansion .... at a flat fixed interest rate of 2%."

Let me at it! Where does the queue start? At the funny farm of course and, as we have heard in the last few days, One Nation was going to print money to turn this policy into reality!

But wait - One Nation leaves the man from Demtel in the shade. There is much, much more.

One Nation proposes in its Law and Order, Crime and Punishment policy, "that in order to make the judiciary accountable, at each State election, the public of Queensland, the ordinary citizens, will have the opportunity to elect 12 of its peers who will form the membership of a State Judiciary Review Board. ....which .... will conduct annual reviews of the judiciary and make recommendations with regard to, or termination of, the appointment of those who fail to uphold the sentencing policies of the State government of the day." Wow! This is better than the Wild West!

You need to be sitting down for the next one. Let me again quote directly. "One Nation will give police the discretionary power to remove people under the age of 16 years from the streets and return them to their home or to a suitable venue". This is a superficially popular policy which is inherently dangerous.

In addition, "One Nation will allow parents to administer just and fair domestic discipline within their homes, without fear or favour of interference from external agencies, thus restoring the authority of the family". And perhaps also adding to the queues in Casualty at Queensland hospitals!

Finally, One Nation trots out the old chestnut, "Queensland's crime rate is increasing exponentially" - that is very rapidly. Again, this is a hoary myth. Contact with the Queensland Attorney-General's Department reveals that there was in fact a reduction in homicide convictions over the last three years and that after taking into account population increases, there was arguably a reduction in major crimes on a per capita basis over that period.

Many of the above extraordinary policies have never been headlined or scrutinised by the media. The same media is quite often obsessed with highlighting negative trivia about the major players on the political stage. It's just a shame they didn't subject Hanson's inadequate policies to the same scrutiny.
