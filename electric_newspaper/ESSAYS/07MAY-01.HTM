Thursday May 7

Dean Brown: reinventing the palette
by John Rau

Human Services Minister, Dean Brown has had a pretty good run since last year's election. He has not been associated publicly with any moves to topple his old rival, John Olson. He has assumed a high public profile on a number of important issues. Most notably he has taken the bat to the Federal Government over its policy of gutting the Public Hospital system.

Mr Brown is in the process of re-inventing himself. Gone is the colorless "Dean Beige". Gone is the patronizing, school prefect behavior. Gone also are the shifty responses to questions requiring direct answers. Mr. Brown has started to take a principled stand on issues of great importance to all South Australians. What a shame that Mr. Brown had to be sacked as Premier before he discovered his better side.

Contrast the new look Mr. Brown with the oily performance of the Premier and his Deputy over the ETSA privatization. What little credibility this pair had, is well and truly spent. To make matters worse for the Premier, he still has to face a court-room battle over the allegation that he was responsible for the damaging leaks which ultimately destroyed Mr. Brown's Premiership. Any adverse finding in this case will kill him stone dead.

If the Premier has any common sense, he will choose to exit the scene in the next month or two, perhaps citing illness. If Mr. Ingerson has any common sense, (a far more problematic assumption), he will go too.

These two are political "nosferatu" or "the undead". They are beyond redemption. Their only remaining purpose is to further soil the standing of their host, the South Australian Liberal Party. In the forthcoming federal poll, the stench coming from these two could cost the Liberal Party the seats of Adelaide, Kingston, Makin, Grey and Hindmarsh. If the South Australian Liberal Party has any sense of self-preservation, it will exorcise these two quickly.

Whether the Party seizes the moment and acts, or events are allowed take their own course, a new Premier and Deputy will soon be required. Mr. Brown is skillfully positioning himself for this eventuality. The question is, has he really become a decent chap like his hero, Tom Playford, or is he just the same old dull Dean? We should remember that Peter Reith went through a cuddly phase too, but it was just an act.

South Australians need new leadership. Leadership which is credible and principled. We need a Government that doesn't smell like Bolivar.

Whether Mr. Brown has matured enough to offer what is required is hard to say. If he gets another crack at the top job, his former performance just won't do. If he hasn't matured, we would all be better off with a clean skin, even perhaps a novice, like Mr. Buckby.
