Tuesday February 24, 1998

The bigger the lie
By John Rau

A former German political identity, Adolf Hitler, is generally credited with having formulated the idea that the bigger the lie the more likely it is to be believed. It would seem that this philosophy is still alive and well in South Australia.

Before the State Election, the Premier swore black and blue that he would not, under any circumstances, be selling ETSA. The Premier knew full well that most South Australians detested his decision, taken without consultation, to sell South Australia’s water management assets to SA Water. He knew that if he went to the election advocating a sale of ETSA he would be wiped out. He went into the election promising to do what the overwhelming bulk of the South Australian community want and expect him to do - keep ETSA in South Australian hands.

To say that the Government’s decision to sell ETSA is contemptible still fails to adequately express the level of community outrage. If the Government thought it was reasonable to go to the people a few months ago promising not to sell ETSA, it should have the decency to stick to this fundamental promise. If they have now changed their minds, they should have the decency to immediately call an election and put their proposal to sell ETSA to the people. Let the people of South Australia decide whether they want to have ETSA sold.

The Premier will not of course do this because he does not have the guts.

He knows that if he fought an election on the sale of ETSA now he would be thrashed.

It is comforting to know that certain American based utility companies are already interested in buying ETSA. It gives me a warm and fuzzy feeling to imagine that one day decisions about my power supply will be made in some boardroom in Dallas instead of at the top of Anzac Highway.

It makes me feel good to think that American utilities entrepreneurs will be deciding the pricing of electricity supplies for South Australian industries. I am particularly pleased that South Australian pensioners and concession holders will be able to benefit from the well known charitable behaviour of international energy companies.

The Government has of course promised that no concession holders or country residents will in any way be disadvantaged. Imagine that. The Government that promised only a few months ago prior to the election that it would never sell ETSA, is now promising that when it does sell ETSA, certain disadvantaged people will not suffer.

They have got to be joking. Even the most gullible South Australians have now woken up to them.

Not content with throwing ETSA onto the bonfire, the Government has also decided that it will sell the TAB and Lotteries Commission. There was an argument at one time to the effect that the Government involving itself in gambling was, to some extent, taking gambling out of the back rooms of pubs. There is probably some truth in this argument.

The fact is, though, that the Government stepped in to the role of SP bookmakers.The compensating factor was that the profits were to be directed back into public assets such as hospitals and schools.

This made it tolerable. What is now proposed is of course to sell this licence to print money to priivate enterprise. In other words, entrepreneurial gambling licensed by the Government.

The people of South Australia do have some prospect of getting rid of this type of Government behaviour. The Government depends for its numbers in the House of Assembly upon a two Independents. People throughout South Australia could make an effort to contact these Independents and urge them to bring down this Government.

The people of South Australia deserve an opportunity to cast a vote at a general State Election before vital State assets such as ETSA are sold off to foreign utility companies.

The call should go out now. We want an election and we want one now.
