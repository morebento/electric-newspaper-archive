Monday March 23, 1998

Mike Rann and privatisation
by Legh Davis, Liberal M.L.C.

The Leader of the Opposition, Mike Rann, is jumping up and down about the privatisation of ETSA. He is against it.

But the facts about Mike Rann and privatisation should be put on the record.

In 1991 the Federal Labor Government proceeded to privatise the Commonwealth Bank. At the time, John Bannon was Premier of South Australia and also Federal President of the Labor Party.

His very close associate and former staffer, Mike Rann, had become a Member of Parliament in 1985 and was elevated to the Cabinet in 1989. Can any of your readers recall a public denouncement by Mike Rann of the privatisation of the Commonwealth Bank and later Qantas?

More to the point is that in 1991 the South Australian Government owned 82% of the South Australian Gas Company which had reticulated gas to metropolitan Adelaide since 1861.

In 1991 the Government reduced its holding and in July 1992 Premier Bannon announced the Government would sell its remaining 57% interest in Sagasco. This eventually occurred in September 1993 when Boral made a takeover bid for the company.

My Labor spies tell me that at Cabinet meetings Rann did not once exercise his vocal cords to speak against this de facto privatisation.

It was alright with Mike Rann for the Labor Government to sell off its 82% interest in one energy company, but it is not alright with Mike Rann for a Liberal Government to propose the sale of 100% in another energy company.

Again in February 1993 new Labor Premier, Lynn Arnold, announced that the Cabinet had decided to sell and privatise the State Bank. This decision was subsequently endorsed by the State Council of the Labor Party in April 1993. Rann was again silent on the issue of privatisation.

It seems that Mike Rann is more than happy if Federal and Labor Governments move to privatise public sector commercial operations, but gets his knickers in a twist if Liberal Governments attempt to do the same thing.

The Labor Party is in a mess on the privatisation of ETSA. Paul Holloway, Deputy Leader of the Labor Party in the Legislative Council and apparently its financial spokesman, revealed the leaps in logic by Labor.

This week in Parliament, by way of interjection, he claimed that because a Labor Member might oppose privatisation it didn't prevent them buying shares in these privatised companies. So if ETSA was to be floated to the public of South Australia (although this is not the preferred option), we could well see State Labor MP’s dipping into their pockets for shares!

Certainly it is quite clear that Federal and State Labor Members of Parliament or their spouses have not been shy about buying Telstra shares, although of course Kim Beazley and the Labor Party have been fervently against the privatisation of Telstra.

Only this week, Rann was claiming credit for his role in the de facto privatisation of the Adelaide Airport, which had previously been managed by the Federal Government.

I guess all of the above gives principle a new meaning - and Mike Rann plenty of headaches!
