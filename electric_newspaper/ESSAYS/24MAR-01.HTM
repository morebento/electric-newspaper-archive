Tuesday March 24, 1998

Calling all Australians
by John Rau

The announcement last week to a breathless assembly of Liberal Party heavies and corporate vultures that the remaining two-thirds of Telstra would be put up for sale surprised no-one.

To the ideologically pure, to the greedy and to the downright stupid, this seems a good idea. The Morgan poll in this week's Bulletin suggests that well over 60% of Australians are sensible and community-minded enough to see through this cynical stunt .

Sadly for Australia, the decision may not be ours. Again it seems destined to come down to that honest eccentric, Senator Brian Harradine from Tasmania and Labor rat, Senator Colston. What have we as a people done to deserve this?

There are certain inevitable consequences of a full sale of Telstra. John Howard will deny these, but he was never going to sell the rest of Telstra was he? He has no credibility on this issue .

Unfortunately, the Labor Opposition has little credibility either. After all, Labor sold the Commonwealth Bank and countless other government assets when it was in power. Even worse, Labor used the proceeds of these public asset sales to fund recurrent expenditure. Very irresponsible.

The first consequence of a full sale is obvious.The directors of Telstra will have an obligation to maximize the profits of the business.There will be pressure to maximize returns to shareholders.This means that inefficient or low return activities will be wasted or cut out. Attention will be given to highly profitable functions.

There is no doubt that Community Service Obligations, special assistance for the bush and un-timed local calls are all impediments to greater profitability.The Government has promised to pass laws obliging a fully privatised Telstra to perform as it presently does in these areas. What a comfort! A promise from any Government these days is of no value.

Once Telstra is in private hands the pressure will mount to change the law. Governments are notoriously gutless at standing up to big business when the pressure is on. When Governments cave in, they typically rush to join the howls of protest as if it wasn’t their doing at all! This pantomime is played out before our eyes several times a year when private Health Insurance funds raise their fees yet again.

So kiss goodbye to un-timed local calls. Kiss goodbye to Community Service.

The next inevitability is that foreign owners will seek ever greater shares in Telstra. Gutless governments will sell this as "strategic alliances" and use other lies to conceal a sell-off of this nation's greatest single public asset. Look at the Qantas sale if you want an example.

The bottom line is this: in spite of foolish attempts by past governments to create "competition" in the market, Telstra is essentially a monopoly. If its hands were untied, Telstra would wipe Optus and others out in no time.Telstra is a natural monopoly which is still owned 66% by all of us. If it misbehaves now we can vote out its bosses at the next Federal poll . We have some influence. A privatised monopoly, perhaps one that is foreign owned, is a nightmare.

This proposal is cynical and un-Australian. It must be fought and condemned.
