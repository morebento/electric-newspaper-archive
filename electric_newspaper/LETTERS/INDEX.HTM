Friday July 10, 1998

Straw clutching

The Government continues to clutch at straws in the ETSA/Optima debate as vividly demonstrated in "Viewpoints" (29/6 & 6/7/98) by Liberal MLC, Legh Davis.

The Democrats analysis (not mere "acceptance" as Mr Davis states) of the Quiggin-Spoehr Report showed that it had far more credibility than the report prepared for the Government by former Auditor-General, Tom Sheridan.

Mr Davis says "No-one has yet showed me any evidence of Quiggin and Spoehr ever saying anything nice about privatisation", as if this provides some proof that their economic analysis of the loss of revenue to the State is wanting. On the contrary it remains the most authoritative analysis on the issue.

In my research I addressed the reasons the Government had given to justify sale i.e. loss of competition payments, the risks of the National Electricity Market, and retiring of State debt. At no stage did I interrogate lobbyists as to their positions on the philosophy of privatisation, nor would it have been appropriate. The correct process always was to look at the arguments given by the Government and test them: ultimately, on each point the Government's arguments foundered.

What is more puzzling about the Davis opinion piece is that he repeats the claims made in stage-managed and distorted questions and answers which he has asked of the Treasurer in Parliament. For the record, the document we handed out to journalists at the time of announcement of our decision to oppose the sale was 10 pages long plus a cover sheet and appendices.

This is matter which must be resolved on substance, and despite Legh Davis' debating points and the Government's desperate attempts to save face, ultimately their arguments just do not stack up.
