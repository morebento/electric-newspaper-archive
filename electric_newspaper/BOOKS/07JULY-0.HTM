A Touch of Mortality
by Ann Granger
pub Avon Books
rrp $12.95 copyright 1996
reviewed by Denise Wels

I have read all of Ann Granger's eight previous Meredith and Markby mysteries available in paperback. Initially I enjoyed them but have noticed my interest dwindling with time until I found myself loth to continue reading 'A Touch of Mortality' once begun. Perhaps a reader, encountering her writing for the first time in this novel, might enjoy it. I am afraid I found my interest kindled only by two characters... the corpse and his billy goat.

Ann Granger, in a bio I found, discloses that she obtained a Modern Languages degree then went to work for consulates in Yugoslavia, Czechoslovakia and Austria. Presumably it was during this period of her life that she gained the experience to create her character Meredith Mitchell who worked for the Foreign Office both abroad and in England. Ann Granger now lives in England with her husband.

Granger began her writing career using the nom-de-plume Ann Hulme, under which name she wrote historical romances none of which is still in print.

Granger's protagonists, Meredith Mitchell and Superintendent Alan Markby are another pair of fictional characters carrying on a seemingly passionless sporadic affair from book to book. Will they? Won't they? Who cares! The state of their love is not sufficient to make me want to buy another book of the series. Perhaps Granger feels the same way since I have seen advertising for a book by her featuring a different protagonist. Perhaps some of the earlier enthusiasm she had in her Meredith series will be transferred to the new series. I hope so since, as I said before, I was interested in her earlier work.

In this book, Sally, the wife of research scientist Liam Caswell, receives a letter bomb which, despite its not being opened, explodes and injures her. Liam has been involved with animal experimentation and has infuriated animal rights groups throughout the area despite claiming to be no longer involved with animals.

Sally is a friend of Meredith Mitchell, to whom she appeals for support. Markby, naturally, finds himself in charge of the investigation so once again relies on Meredith's skills.

Sally, who is wealthy as opposed to her impoverished husband, bought the cottage in which they are living. Liam is not kindly disposed to the neighbour, Mr. Bodicote, whose cottage adjoins theirs. Mr Bodicote owns goats...one billy and some nannies... whom he does not seek to restrain from wandering into the Caswells' property. Meredith, when bending over to investigate something is introduced a bit too intimately to Jasper, the billy, who seems in high good humour at carrying out his prank and claims Meredith as a friend.

Sally works for an antiques auctions house and is interested to discover that Mr. Bodicote owns all kinds of antiques which could be quite valuable.

Then, Mr. Bodicote is found dead. He appears to have fallen and struck his head on a lump of concrete which he has filched from the detritus of renovations on the Caswells' property. After Bodicote's death, Sally, Meredith and Markby become involved in enquiries since Bodicote was shown to be too much of a lover of books.

The story meanders through investigations into the letter bomb which could have killed Sally, discoveries about Liam Caswell's professional life and associates and the time he spends at home writing his book, Bodicote's unusual hobby, to which we are made privy only in the last few pages, and machinations within the animal rights groups.

Alas, the characters are convincingly dull and the action slow. The English countryside is pleasant but if I read the next Ann Granger book featuring her new detective, someone will have to give it to me, I shan't be buying it.
