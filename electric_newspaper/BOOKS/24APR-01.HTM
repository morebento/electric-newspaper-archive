Mystery Review
Maggody Militia by Joan Hess pub Onyx rrp $12.95

Another writer of lighthearted mysteries with only sketchy characterisation is Joan Hess. She writes about the mythical Ozarks town of tiny Maggody. Hess' protagonist is Arly Hanks who, after surviving a failed marriage in New York decides to return home and against all regular male wishes, become the Maggody Chief of Police.

Unlike Sharyn McCrumb's series about the Appalachians, Hess does not make much of the spirituality of the region nor introduce a supernatural element to her mysteries ... unless one categorises lecherous Brother Verber of 'Maggody Militia', who apparently bought his theological degree from a mail order college, as having spiritual leanings. Hess, to the contrary, introduces a light element in her affectionate appraisal of the town (pop.755) and its idiosyncratic inhabitants.

In Maggody, euphony is everything, as evidenced in the wonderful names of some of the hillbillies: Rubella Belinda Hanks (Arly's mother) Diesel Buchanon, Bismal Buchanon, Petrol Buchanon and even Arlie's own name which is either Ariel, after Shakespeare's sprite, or misspelled after a photograph once taken of Maggody : Arly tends to believe the latter explanation.

One thing I find slightly offputting is Hess' switching from a third person narrative to a first person when Arlie is in the frame. This frequently happens mid chapter and I found myself having to go back a few paragraphs to work out what was happening. Even so, the action moves apace.

Hess' characters are only sketched but they are sketched well and present a humorous total picture. The events. while containing an element of comedy, still comprise a good mystery.

Initially Arly is called upon to solve the mystery of some burglaries committed while the owners of properties are away from town on holidays.

The discerning reader (of which I was not one) might be able to pick the solution to just HOW the burglars were able to plot their robberies successfully on reading the first part of the book.

The Maggody Militia of the title consists of a group of men led by General Pitts (a self bestowed title) who comes to town against the wishes of Chief Arly Hanks to perform pseudo military manoeuvres during Deer Season.

Naturally, someone is killed. There is another murder associated with the break-ins, all this against the back-drop of Marjorie, the prize sow owned and loved by Raz Buchanon who constantly informs Arly that he doesn't have a still and is certainly not going to tell her where it is; Bigfoot lookalike Diesel Buchanon; blissfully happy and very pregnant couple Kevin and Dahlia Buchanon; the disappearance of Brother Verber who was grimly overseen by the moral Mayor's wife, Mrs. Jim Bob and sought with an eye to matrimony by Kayleen, the widow upon whose property the militia intends to camp out. Mention should not be omitted of Ruby Bee's friend Estelle and her unexpected inheritance from her Uncle Tooly (who was murdered by sheep) of two ostriches which set about terrorising the town.

This is not a deeply plotted mystery but it lays no claim to being a moving experience, just a good fun, shallow entertainment for a rainy day.
