Wednesday July 8, 1998

Threesome's a crowd

Threesome '98's Adelaide Brand X Theatre is known for its performance last year of 'And The Ass Saw The Angel', based on the novel by Australian music iconoclast Nick Cave. Now it presents 'Class of '98', look a inside the mind of a killer and an attempt to understand what makes a school student pick up a gun, walk into their class room, and kill innocent other students.

The play is inspired and developed from the original concept by Scott McCarten, with dramaturgy by Fiona Sprott.

Port Arthur, Dunblane, Hoddle Street, Queen Street, and classrooms across America are invoked. Brand X believes this takes interactive theatre to the next level, as ‘you’ become a ‘hostage’ and bargain for your freedom.

It is a nightmare every parent dreads - to hear on the news that their son or daughter may be a victim of a class room shooting.

Class room violence has become every day news - in just the last nine months there have been 14 children killed and 40 wounded in American schools.

Class of '98 aims to circumvent the outrage and indignation over gun deaths in schools and strikes at the very core of the incidents, giving you a chance to find out some of the real answers.

Brand X Theatre are a young street savvy theatre outfit, who, always wary of authority, are ready to articulate the voice of youth - good or bad.

The play is not, apparently, for the faint-hearted.

Class of '98 is performed at the Lion Theatre from 24 July to 8 August, 8pm $18.00/$10.00.
