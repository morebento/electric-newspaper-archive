Monday May 11, 1998


'French Vision', oil on canvas.


'Cretan Church', oil on canvas.


'Magnols,France', oil on canvas.


'Red Jug', oil on canvas.


'Still Life with Onions', oil on canvas.

Dee Jones
Liberator Gallery, North Adelaide
Reviewed by Dianne Gall

A sense of serenity, of time standing still, images awash with bright clear colours. These are the works of Dee Jones. Whether they be landscapes, portraits or still life's these paintings have a strong sense of order and careful placement all painted with precise detail. Whilst the work takes on the appearance of that of native art, there is an underlying complexity and sophistication that suggests otherwise.

Born in South Australia in 1933, Jones held her first Solo show in 1968 and following this she has held numerous exhibitions around Australia. Travelling to Europe regularly since the early 70's it is during this time that Jones has found much of her subject matter. Her works are idealised views providing a sense of place and documentation of her existence whether in McLaren Vale, Greece, Italy or France. Since 1990, Jones has been visiting a region in and around the South of France and now, after buying a farmhouse 200kms north of Toulouse in France, has decided to leave our shores and permanently reside there. After an initial six months in France, Dee will return to Adelaide to visit her 90 year old mother who still lives here.

Along with France, Greece has been a place that Jones has visited since 1973. In her current exhibition we see a well-constructed painting "Window ö Hydra, Greece". There are elements echoing throughout the painting, such as the flip of the top of the poplars to the outward swing of the white lace curtains and through to the fall of the dress on the figure standing in the room. In this work, Jones has created a feeling of tranquillity as the breeze gently wafts in the window. Other works taken from images in Greece like "Cretan Church" sees the minimal image of a church placed high on a hillside that is then nestled and protected by two poplars sharply cutting into the clear blue sky. Jonesâs observations are meticulous, illustrating the passing of time and life cycles within the landscape and for the people who exist in them.

Other works in the exhibition painted in France display a subtle drenching of light over the subjects but not too much as to dull the colour. There is a wonderful sense of life in these works, you can feel the sunlight fall upon your face as your eye travels along the paths through images like "Fields near Tauriac, France 1" and "Magnols, France". There is a strong sense of order in these works with the rows of threes and lines of crops.

A new found sense of freedom and expression is starting to appear in paintings such as "French Vision". In this work we see a blurring of the edges and a greater subtlety in the colour and brush strokes, it dose not have the stillness of most of the other works in the exhibition, instead we see a new energy springing forth.

Also in this exhibition is a series of still life's that are painted with a clarity of colour and boldness of composition. Perhaps the most successful paintings are "Still Life with Onions" and "Red Jug" both using strong placement of colours and strength of design. "Country Vegetables" is still life that evokes elements of landscape with the table becoming the land and the vegetables drift across it as if they were planted vegetation.

The exhibition concludes 17 May 1998
