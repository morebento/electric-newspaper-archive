Portishead
Thebarton Theatre, Thursday April 23
By Victoria White

Mmm the heavenly voice of Beth Gibbons floating over a very excitable Adelaide audience makes for a great show. To let the mind drift and fantasise. If only all shows and indeed all bands could pull off a show that is not only musically thrilling, but an overall sensory and sensual feast. Ears, eyes, nose, tongue and body. Does it sound a little too much? Well maybe now you know what it felt like.

With a cello, bass, guitar, a great singer and some funky DJ-ing, Portishead really rocked. In a move away from the traditional concert, the stage was dark, a projector aimed behind the band lit up the room with 70's style psychedelic and 90's graphics (the visual sound vibrations were very very cool).

Musically it was faultless. Beth has a vocal range that Bjork would die for, delivered with all the confidence and sweetness of a 13 year-old girl. "Glory Box" sent the room wild. In fact, every song they performed sent the room wild, old and new. The crowd occasionally drowning out the sound of the music with all of their applause.

Lets hope Portishead have changed the music industry to incorporate what it always should have been - music created by professional, talented, creative and indeed brilliant artists.

Portishead shy away from publicity, attempting to retain a grip on themselves and their music. They're determined not to sell-out like so many other bands tipped to be the next big thing. Good on them. So, on second thoughts, they sucked, don't buy their records (Portishead and Dummy), and certainly don't go an see one of their shows. Leave that to all those who thought Thursday night left them less than numb. Thanks Portishead, come back anytime.
