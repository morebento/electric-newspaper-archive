Tuesday April 14, 1998

Rimona Kedem: another view
Adelaide Central Gallery
Reviewed by Dianne Gall

Kedem's exhibition of 26 mixed media works are very personal expressions of a journey of life's experiences and the interactions with various people along the way.

Now in her 60's Kedem's artworks are modest self expressive creations that have been worked, scratched back, layered and have a greatly varied and interesting rich surface as a result.

Works such as 'The Dance', 'The Rubber Doll' and 'Chasing Butterflies' have a lyrical quality with a seductive energy that draws the viewer in to see more and it is at this point the artist's techniques begin to develop the image further. Some images tell of family memories, like 'An Old Postcard' which portrays the artist's parents as they journeyed on a boat from Russia to New York.

The couple are painted with a strong sense of closeness and capture a moment of calm as they embark on their journey. The background is subtly abstracted to draw the viewer to the power of the red veil and flowers.

There is also a sombre aspect in Kedem's work as seen in 'Valentines Day', where a face of a man stares out of the foreground aggressively demanding the viewer's attention.

In the background is a broken face of a woman with one eye completely scratched out and the other stares soft and helpless out of the darkness that is her face. This is a disturbing image of destruction and depravation.

Perhaps the most symbolic and powerful work of the exhibition is 'Vacancy'.

This is a painting of death, veiled by the blurred image of a woman standing by watching the emptiness of her chair that is now a testimony of her existence.

Boldly painted in red, the chair, once serving only as an object in a room, now takes on its own importance and stands alone waiting for the next occupier.

Many of the images are about self acceptance like 'Leaving my Shadow Behind' and self journey like 'Take Off' which places a woman floating over the landscape where all we can see is a billowing dress with two blue shoes to anchor her and she journeys through her dream.

Overall the works are well considered, rich in texture and content. This is an exhibition worthy of spending time discovering the intricacies within the paintings.

Also in Central's Studio Gallery is a collection of small abstract paintings of the landscape by Andreas Christensen and a series of Aquatint prints by Trena Everuss.

In the Student Gallery is an exhibition of large composite drawings by Eight participants of the 1998 New York Drawing Marathon.

All on show until May 4.
