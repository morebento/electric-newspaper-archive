Thursday May 7, 1998

My bonnie lies over the c
Fundamental
Reviewed by Jack Connell

"I decided to work with some new people this time, to shake things up a bit, and the results are even more exciting than I could have hoped for.......I think it's the grittiest, deepest and most original album I've ever made."

So says blues rocker Bonnie Raitt of her latest offering "Fundamental". 1971 saw the release of her debut self-titled album to critical acclaim. Fourteen albums later and Ms Raitt is still going strong. Her performance at Bob Dylan's March '92 Adelaide Entertainment Centre concert was hailed by many as far superior to that of the main attraction.

Bonnie Raitt returned in 1997 for a performance at the Festival Theatre where she virtually blew Jackson Browne right off the stage and reduced many punters to tears with heartfelt renditions from her extensive reportoire.

For this CD Bonnie has enlisted keyboard player/co-producer Mitchell Froom, who was responsible for putting Crowded House on the map, and engineer/co-producer Tchad Blake, who has the likes of Los Lobos, Richard Thompson and Suzanne Vega on his CV. Other notables appearing on this album include Cheryl Crow guitarist Steve Donnelly, NRBQ bassist Joy Spampinato, drummer Pete Thomas (ex Elvis Costello) and Renee Geyer.

While things have certainly been shaken up a bit, this album still contains hallmark Bonnie Raitt, beautifully enhanced by Blake's sparse, crystal clear mix and superb audio engineering skills. There is evidence of former Raitt collaborations and influences such as Little Feat, Jackson Browne, John Hiatt, Willie Dixon and J.B.Lenoir. This gives rise to an alluring blend of blues, largely unnoticed pop with gutsy undertones driven by her individually mellow vocals and very tasty guitar playing.
