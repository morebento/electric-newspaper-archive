Tuesday May 12, 1998

Jeff Buckley: new album released soon
by Andrew Weaver

Jeff Buckley was an amazing man - anyone who saw his amazing live shows will testify to this. Like his late father, Tim Buckley, who died of a drug overdose in his prime, Jeff was taken from the world at a young age on May 27, 1997.

He was, at the time, in Memphis to record to follow up to the hugely successful, and hugely influential, Grace. To be called My Sweetheart, The Drunk, it never was completed, as his band arrived the day after Jeff took his last swim in the Memphis River. On the night that Jeff drowned, he was on his way to meet his band to begin three weeks of rehearsals for My Sweetheart, The Drunk. Producer Andy Wallace, who'd helmed the boards on Grace, was to join them in Memphis in late June to record the album, which had originally been worked on with producer Tom Verlaine.

Assembled under the guidance of Mary Guibert, Jeff's mother, a new record is to be released in May - Sketches (For My Sweetheart, the Drunk) is a collection of Jeff's last studio sessions with his band with four-track home recordings providing priceless and immeasurable insight into the direction his music was going. Disc 1 of Sketches (For My Sweetheart, The Drunk) contains ten tracks from the multi-track studio sessions. Disc 2 contains Jeff's last compositions, personal four-track recordings of new songs and refinements of older songs intended for My Sweetheart, The Drunk.

As the executor of Jeff's estate, it fell to his mother to assist his record label (Columbia) in deciding what to do next - whether to release a compilation of recordings or to simply leave his estate be. She agreed to release a posthumous album, believing that it was what Jeff would have wanted. But, she did have several sticking points.

First, there would be no posthumous overdubbing. The songs would stay where Jeff left them, even if a part was missing. Second, the album would not be an overview of Jeff Buckley music. There were good outtakes from Grace and the Live at Sin-e EP. There were b-sides and pre-Columbia recordings. Some of those still might come out someday (and if recent reports on the Addicted to Noise website are true, this is almost a definite) but not now. The last new Jeff Buckley album would be the songs he was working on when he died, the songs he and his band intended to record in June of 1997 for My Sweetheart, The Drunk.

There were two major sources for recordings of those songs. Jeff and his band had done three sets of official studio demos with Tom Verlaine producing, two of which were done in Jeff's hometown of New York City and one in Memphis, where he died. There was a fourth unofficial session, with Verlaine, recorded with Jeff's longtime friend Michael Clouse acting as engineer.

Verlaine and the band went back to New York at the end of February 1997. Jeff stayed in Tennessee and continued to work on his songs on a four-track recorder. As Jeff was wont to do, new songs were written all the time. He sent tapes to the band members, so they would be up to speed with him. He made a decision to take control of the project himself. Toward the end of April, Jeff started making preparations to bring the band and Grace producer Andy Wallace together in Memphis to record the album he wanted to call My Sweetheart, The Drunk. Andy would fly down in late-June. The band would be there to begin rehearsals on May 29th. Their flight was barely airborne when Jeff and a friend set out to grab a bite to eat. They drove around, ending up at the Wolf River Marina to watch the sunset. The band's plane was in mid-flight when Jeff stepped into the deceptively placid water to cool off with a swim. By the time the plane landed, the Coast Guard and Memphis Police had begun a futile search and rescue mission, which lasted three days before a tourist found his partly decomposed body in a Memphis marina. Before this grisly find it was hoped that we was still alive, perhaps unconscious, washed up on a shore somewhere along the treacherous river.

Over the summer of 1997, Andy Wallace and Steve Berkowitz, Jeff's A&R (artist and repertoire) man, mixed the Verlaine sessions for what could have been a single CD release in the latter stages of 1997. But Mary, sensibly, didn't want to rush out the album.

In the intervening months between that time and the early months of 1998, Columbia Records executives assisted Mary in the time-consuming task of listening to and preserving the many hours of four-track recordings. They were raw and unfinished, with Jeff performing all the parts - tapping the microphone with his fingertips for a kick drum sound, toying with weird effects, simulating various instruments. Unfortunately, even the best of them was sonically flawed, especially when compared to the studio sessions. However, they were the blueprints of Jeff's most recent work, part of his master plan for My Sweetheart, The Drunk. It is these, amongst other recordings that will be able to be heard in May.

Australia was fortunate enough to see this man twice. Fortunately, it appears his legacy will live in, with the promise of new albums to be released. First up in this list is Sketches (For My Sweetheart, The Drunk) which is, predictably, amazing.
