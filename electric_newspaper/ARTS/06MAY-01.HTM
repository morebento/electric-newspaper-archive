Wednesday May 6, 1998

Canon Powershot 600
Reviewed by Tania Liemareff

Most of the pix you see in The Electric Newspaper were taken with a Canon digital camera. Tania Liemareff, an accomplished 35mm photographer, gives her views of the Canon 600.

One of the most impressive features of the Canon Power Shot 600 Digital Camera is its minimal size and compact efficiency. Approximately 159.5mm wide, 92.5mm high, 58.8mm deep and with a weight of 420g (excluding batteries and PCMCIA/ATA card), it is a tiny piece of equipment which can easily be held in with one hand.

Images can be stored in the cameras internal flash media. Either the Canon Compack Flash card (FC-4M) or the Canon Hard Disk card (HC-170M) are recommended for use. Both options will allow you to store more images.

The Canon Power Shot camera has two basic modes in which to photograph. The first is the "Colour mode", which may be used to take pictures of colour scenery and objects. The other is the "Black and White/Text mode", which is mainly used for taking pictures of text.

When photographing in "Colour mode" there is also the added option available for selection of one of three image quality settings. These are "Normal, Fine or Economy". The "Fine" setting provides the highest of image quality available. Whilst the "Normal" setting images are of an adequate, but lesser quality. Finally, the "Economy" setting provides you with a less detailed image. The advantage of using the "Economy" setting is that it uses less storage memory, allowing more photographs to be taken.

Focusing is fixed, "Autofocus" enables the camera to automatically focuses at the centre of each shooting area of the viewfinder. For situations where the subject is not at the centre of the shooting area or if two subjects are side by side, the "Focus Lock" function is activated by first locking the focus on one of the subjects and then re-evaluating what appears in the viewfinder.

The Canon Power Shot also has a "Macro mode" function. The available shooting distance allows you to get as close as between 40 cm and 10 cm to the subject. This as well as having a built-in microphone to enable you to record sound.

To use in low light situations, there is an internal flash unit with an "Auto Flash, Flash On and Flash Off" option. The flash is effective for subjects in a range from 20 cm to 3.5 metres.

The camera has a re-chargable Ni-Cd battery pack, which is easily inserted into a battery chamber found at its base. How long the battery lasts is variable, depending on the quality of images taken or how often the internal flash is in use.

Installing the software application "Power Shot TWAIN Driver", is a simple process. This enables the transfering of images via a camera station, to your computer. There you are able to further work with the images.

Fitted with a Wide Converter (WC-PS28), the lens focal length is closely equivalent to a 28mm lens in the 35mm film format. The wide lens only presents a problem when you are required to capture close-ups of the subject from a disadvantaged position in as crowd of people. The only improvement I would wish for is the availability of a zoom function and/or the option of a different sized lens.

VITAL STATISTICS.

Canon Digital Camera Power Shot 600

Image: 570,000-pixel CCD image sensor

Bits-per-Pixel Rates: 24-bits in colour/8-bit in monochrome

Sensitivity: Equivalent to ISO 100

Storage Media: Internal flash memory (1MB), PCMCIA/ATA cards (4MB or 170MB)

File Formats: JPEG for images, TIFF for thumbnails, Windows Wave for sound

Recorded Image Size: Max. 832x608 pixels, selectable 640x480 and 320x240 pixels

Memory Storage Capacity: 15 images in internal flash memory, 62 images in 4 MB Compact Flash Card FC-4M, or approximately 2,500 images in HD Card HC-170M (Economy mode)

Lens: 7mm f/2.5 (equivalent to 50 mm lens in a 35mm film format), 3.9mm (equivalent to 28mm lens in a 35mm film format), with Canon Wide Converter WC-PS28

Shutter: Mechanical Shutter, with shutter speed 1/30 to 1/500 second

Focus: Auto Focusing (TTL) from 10cm (Macro)

Power Source: Nickel-Cadmium cells

Distributor: Canon Australia Pty. Ltd.
