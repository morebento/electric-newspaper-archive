Sunday April 5, 1998

In the Company of Men
(Palace, East End Cinema.)
Reviewed by Victoria White

If you think that all men are bastards, here's at least two for you.

Bored corporate go-getters, Chad (Aaron Eckert) a handsome, sly, devious creature, and Howard (Matt Molloy), his easily manipulated, love-starved nerdy boss are the men. They're old college buddies, working for the same firm, on their way to a six week stint in the mid-west. They're also fed-up with the lack of control they have over women and relationships.

While waiting in yet another airport lounge, Chad comes up with a plan to both gain some control and feel good about themselves. They have six weeks to find one woman they can both sweep off her feet, make her fall hopelessly in love, and then let her down with an almighty thud.

Within a few days Chad has found the perfect woman, Christine (Stacy Edwards). She's a lonely typist working in their building, she's beautiful, and she's also deaf. To Chad's despicable delight this adds a whole new depth to his plan. Howard is a little unsure but he decides to go along with it anyway. Who knows, maybe he'll get lucky.

As the weeks tick by, Howard falls in love with Christine, Christine falls in love with Chad, and Chad... well, who knows what he's really thinking. The 'game' turns into a mess of lies, love, hidden agendas and cold truths. The story sinks lower than the Titanic. There are no punches held here.

'In the Company of Men' is not physically violent, there are no gunfights or ice-picks, the villains get around with white collars and briefcases, making it even more brilliant yet painful to watch.

Eckert, Edwards and Molloy are well cast and totally convincing in this low budget knock-out by director Neil LaBute. Eckert will probably be shielding off kicks to his groin at least until his next film comes out. Sick, twisted, emotive, disturbing, 'In the Company of Men' is so sharp that its almost too real to get away with it. How black can comedy get?
