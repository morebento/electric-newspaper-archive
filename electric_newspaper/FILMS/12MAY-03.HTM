Tuesday May 12, 1998

Deep Rising
(Wallis cinemas and the Megaplex as from May 7th, Rated MA)

Deep Rising is one of the unexpected hootfests of the year, a hare-brained potboiler that goes for trashy humour and gets it every time. Treat Williams (deadpanning mightily) cruises out to an isolated spot in the South China Sea (where people apparently disappear regularly, begging the question: who'd be stupid enough to go?) with buddy Kevin J. O'Connor and a shipload of knockabout mercenaries (including such odd character actors as Wes Studi and Amistad's Djimon Hounsou [!!!]). Forced to take refuge on a strangely empty luxury ocean liner (well, a super-imposed special effects one, anyway), they realise (long after the audience, who've read the title and been treated to lots of underwater subjective camerawork) that only the double-dealing Captain (loathsome Anthony Heald), an eye-candy pickpocket (Famke Janssen) and a handful of extras have survived the onslaught of rampaging, tentacle-like, computer-generated monsters which suck the juices out of all and sundry in loonily bloodthirsty highlights (such as one unfortunate pulled gorily down a toilet).

With competent enough wizardries super-imposing the beasties and a knowingly absurd tone, Deep Rising is a real find for any fan of sci-fi silliness, monster movies and wall-to-wall bloodshed, with director Stephen Sommers having as much fun as his weird ensemble and moments of inspired hokum that top many of the other movies parodied (Anaconda, for example, had Jon Voight vomited out from the snake's gut, but Deep Rising features one of the main cast popping forth nearly perfectly flayed - the end credits one FX group for "Half-Digested Billy" [!!!]). A guilty pleasure indeed, but with flashes of inspired wit (one baddie manfully goes to shoot himself rather than be swallowed alive, only to find the gun chamber empty) and grue (Hounsou's startling demise has to be seen to be believed - and, even then, you mightn't be convinced), Deep Rising is funnier than The Wedding Singer and features a barmy climax that goes one better than anything in the travesty of Lost In Space. Obviously some of you wouldn't be caught dead at a ludicrous flick such as this but, if you're in the right mood, it's more fun than a barrel of sea monsters.

***
