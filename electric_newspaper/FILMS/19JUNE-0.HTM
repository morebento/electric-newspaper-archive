Friday June 19, 1998

Piss Weak Impact

Deep Impact
Tea Leoni, Morgan Freeman, a tidal wave (Hoyts)
by Victoria White

What do you call a film with lots of mushy story lines and a 5 minute tidal wave? Not a bloody science fiction.

Deep Impact barely even makes a dent on the sci-fi genre. Billed as an ultimate disaster film, the world (or rather America) is about to be knocked about by a massive comet heading straight for New York City. The President of the United States (Morgan Freeman) and his cronies have been sitting on this little secret for about a year, until a "snappy New York journo" (read - piss weak) played by Tea Leoni, uncovers the truth, well sort of.

The time has come to break the news to the American public. Being America and a disaster movie, you would expect nothing short of a full-blown panic, with looting, the burning of buildings, murders on every street corner, mayhem. What you get is .nothing. Not a sausage, just a few tears. Ho Hum. The panic begins way too late as does everything else, maybe they should have called it "While You Were Waiting", and given the lead to Sandra Bullock.

If you don't mind sitting through two and a half hours of happy American families, just for the sake fifteen minutes worth of special effects, then you could well enjoy this flick. Admittedly the tidal wave is very very cool, although it could have been better. The spaceship landing on the comet was also pretty good but more time out of the spaceship please, we already have a pretty good idea of what one looks like.

It wouldn't be worth boring you with the details of all the crappy scenes in Deep Impact, I wouldn't know where to start or finish. On a scale of 1 to 10 of sci-fi/action films, this rates a two (one for the tidal wave, one for the comet). Do yourself a favour, miss it.
