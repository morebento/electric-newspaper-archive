Tuesday May 12, 1998

Swept From The Sea
(Palace East End, The Capri and selected cinemas as from May 7th, Rated M)

This impressively grim and chilly costume filming of Joseph Conrad's novel has great supporting players (Joss Ackland, Kathy Bates and the marvellous Ian McKellen) shamed by the icy presences of stars Vincent Perez (Queen Margot, The Crow 2) and Rachel Weisz (Chain Reaction and Going All The Way). Kooky sea-loving community embarrassment Amy Foster (Weisz) falls for Yanko (Perez), the last survivor of a shipwreck that drowned all his Ukrainian friends, and the two face hatred and alienation at the hands of a narrow-minded Cornwall shoreline population. McKellen thankfully adds some dignity as the local doctor, silently appalled at their treatment, while Bates and Ackland are appropriately reserved as Weisz's guardians, but this one-expression young actress is a tediously remote hippie anachronism and Perez offers a melodramatic, tongue-mutilating accent that would have any actual denizen of the Carpathian Mountains wanting to draw blood. The cruel seas and howling winds are effectively captured by director Beeban Kidron, and McKellen is fine, but the lack of genuinely powerful central characters leave a vacuum in this harsh story of love and (yawn) fate, leaving you not washed away in emotion but merely a little damp.

**½
