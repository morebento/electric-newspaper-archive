Friday April 3, 1998

The Big Lebowski
Reviewed by Peter Wels

Auteur bad boys Joel and Ethan Coen return to their typically twisted style of filmmaking with their latest comedy about mistaken identity, pornography, bowling and Creedence Clearwater Revival.

In The Big Lebowski, the Coen brothers return to the outrageous style of filmmaking that made them famous after their surprisingly restrained - but still excellent - Academy Award winner 'Fargo'.

Featuring bizarre porn-oriented hallucinations and a cooler-than-cool soundtrack, 'Lebowski' plays like a mix of the sheer insanity of 'Raising Arizona' with the quirkiness of 'Fargo', and some typical Coen brothers oddities thrown in.

'Lebowski' stars Jeff Bridges as Jeff Lebowski - a slacker who prefers to be known as The Dude. After the Dude's rug is soiled by a couple of thugs who mistake him for another Jeffrey Lebowski (a millionaire... the Big Lebowski of the title), the Dude goes to the other Lebowski for compensation. But instead of getting the cash for his rug, he gets caught up in a complicated case of kidnapping, mistaken identity and nihilism as the Big Lebowski's wife, Bunny, is kidnapped by a group of nihilists (led by Peter Stormare and Flea of the Red Hot Chilli Peppers) who want a million dollars for her safe return.

If you're familiar with the Coens' films, you know that going into a cinema to see one of their creations will be an experience - for better or for worse. And 'The Big Lebowski' is definitely no exception.

From a technical point of view, it's fantastic. The two hallucination sequences are brilliantly done and will definitely at least raise a chuckle or two.

The Coens also get great performances from their leads - Jeff Bridges (last seen in Ridley Scott's 'White Squall') is hilarious as the Dude (imagine an American version of Neil from 'The Young Ones' and you'll have a pretty clear picture of his character) while John Goodman also gets a few laughs as Walter, the Dude's Vietman vet buddy.

Other memorable performance include those of 'Fargo' actors Peter Stormare and Steve Buscemi (as the Dude's slightly dim-witted friend), as well as Coen brothers regular John Turturro (from 'Miller's Crossing' and 'Barton Fink' as a pederast bowler) and Sam Sheperd as a character known only as The Stranger.

As with most Coen films, 'The Big Lebowski' is the sort of thing you'll either love or hate. If you're not a Coen fan, this probably won't win you over. But if a cocktail of mistaken identity, bowling, Creedence Clearwater Revival, insane hallucinations, nihillists and porn sounds like your kind of thing, 'The Big Lebowski' will definitely hit the spot.

(Rated MA) - Season Commences April 9
