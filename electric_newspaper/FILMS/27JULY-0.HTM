Monday July 27, 1998

The X-Files
(Hoyts and selected cinemas, Rated M)
reviewed by David Bradley

Omigod!!! It finally all fits together!!! Well..... maybe not. In fact, The X Files movie dares go onebetter on even the series, in being hands down the only mass-adulation popcultural phenomenon that squillions of people revere despite it answering nary one question and obscuring the hazy original facts still further.

This zippily paranoid entertainment takes off directly after the season finale (although oddly no one mentions the TV cliffhanger involving the X office being torched) with Mulder and Scully straying further into a Massive Conspiracy That Goes Way Up To The Highest Echelons Of The American Government (for David Duchovny's Fox Mulder reading of that, block your nose and try it) and somehow (the operative word here) scapegoated after not preventing the terrorist bombing of a Dallas building (an amazing setpiece). Pressured to abandon their search for "The Truth" (whatever the heck that is) they, natch, don't, and soon are in the thick of a Chris-Carter-pipedream/ con job drawing in the oozing parasitic slime (the one that vents FX wrigglies that crawl up your pantsleg), hidden biodomes full of raging bees, subversive Pentagon operations (that, of course, cock up mightily), and the requisite flashes of are-they-or-aren't-they otherwordly beings.

All the small-screen characters (except the crooked Krycek) turn up to contribute their inscrutable two cents worth, including Mitch Pileggi's hard-nosed Skinner, John Neville's "Well-Manicured Man", William B. Davis' dried-out "Smoking/ Cigarette/ Cancer Man", and the motley nerdo "Lone Gunmen", while some inspired guest stars offer strong, pleasingly hammy support, especially Martin Landau as a millennial-mad MD and Armin Mueller-Stahl as another crochety guardian of the Truth (well, I think that's what he's doing anyway). Dave Duchovny and Gilly Anderson, blessed with a wider canvas and an M Rating, are also allowed to stretch a bit, and manage to keep their old-shoe characterisations credible and affectionately funny, while also happily able to interject a few curses ("Jesus!" and "Shit!"), crack meaty gags (such as a hammered Fox taking a leak beside a dog-eared ID4 poster), and even clumsily redefine the parameters of their relationship (maybe a bad idea, but it was bound to happen, so just accept it). As far as an expansion of a series into the unforgiving realms of cinema, The X Files (that's the title, not The X Files Movie or The X Files - Fight The Future) is fine, high-style nonsense, hitting the expected heights - and keeping going - and revising the standard paranormal guff into a very enjoyable, mock-philosophical/ biological/ theological thrill-ride, with an end sequence that tops anything in recent "Event" movies for sheer, breathless suspense (and, yes, even though it's all firmly rooted in absolute bare-faced absurdity). The Hell with the detractors: this one will leave you positively over the moon! I Want To Believe.
