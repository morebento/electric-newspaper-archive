Monday July 6, 1998

Crackers
Nova, Trak and Megaplex Cinemas
reviewed by David Bradley

Why exactly is it that whenever anything produced here is supposedly a "comedy", it's either so laid-back as to be comatose (The Castle) or so try-hardedly rough-and-ready to have you leaving the cinema with severe bruising?

Only Warren Mitchell (the naturalised Oz resident notorious as bigot Alf Garnett on British TV), as a boozy Great-Grandad with a false leg, a dark past and a willingness to irritate punks, brings any style to these fantastically exhausting proceedings, as somewhat scrambled and slightly suicidal schoolkid Daniel Kellie attends a nightmare Christmas with the family: long-suffering Mum (Susan Lyons), her new boyfriend Bruno (Peter Rowsthorn, mugging shamelessly), his nutzoid son Christopher Chapman, Grandad (Terry Gill), Grandma (Maggie King) and flirty Aunt Dotty (Valerie Bader), a motley crue of overplayed stereotypes all busting a gut in the service of material which redefines the expression "over-the-top".

Writer/ director David Swann's comedy efforts here are too good-natured to really hate or get too irritated by, Mitchell is good and there are some bright moments (though just as many wind up cringeful - the show-stopping fate of the family mutt has to be seen to be believed and, even then.....) but it's impossible for the eye to watch (let alone the funny bone to respond to) some of the absolute lowpoints of Crackers. It's rather like the titular snack: starchy, filling and full of fat.

**1/2
