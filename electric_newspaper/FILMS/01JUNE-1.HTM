Monday June 1, 1998

HUSH
(Hoyts and selected cinemas for a brief run, Rated M)
reviewed by David Bradley

- Hush is sheer, wretched exploitation: completely wasting talented people (Gwyneth Paltrow, Jessica Lange, Jonathan Schaech, Debi Mazar, Nina Foch, Hal Holbrook), this hypocritically cashes in on audiences' hopeful disgust at "daring", "thorny" or "hot" topics (pregnancy problems, religio-natterings, familial nutcasery), and is brain-softeningly boring throughout.

It's also visually hideous, with ugly photography, a cast who all appear sick or hung-over, and whole sections (like the unremittingly bad-taste climax where Lange runs roughshod over the contraction-suffering Paltrow) obviously reshot long after the fact (and with scant regard for continuity), when the players look far different (Gwyneth, for example, intermittently loses two or three stone during the narrative, despite being preggers), in a frantic attempt to make sense of an impossibly jumbled mess of a story.

Dingbat Schaech loves Paltrow (who gets a nude scene that's so blatantly body-doubled you'll be howling), but his Mom Lange loves him more. Mom damages Gwyn's diaphragm (I'm not making this up) and she gets pregnant (losing her job after barfing on her boss - are you getting all of this?), so the youngsters marry and move in with scheming Mom, who then goes unconvincingly ape, eye-rollingly watching them in bed, inducing Gwyn's labour with strawberry flan laced with horse medication (if only I was making this up!), and being unveiled as the perpetrator of idiotic past insanities. Gwyn's bad enough, scarcely able to conceal her discomfort at the avalanche of cliche, and with the regulation pillow shoved up her nightie, but Lange's an utter embarrassment as the psycho-bitch-from-Hell, descending to the absolute depths of gross cliche and gladly stooping to assorted archaic stereotypes (most notably, the one about ageing actresses making great knife-wielders): flying into murderously hammy rages, her still-beautiful face made to look a skull mask, Lange delivers the most cringingly tawdry performance so far this year.

How this terrific performer could possibly agree to do such a bonafide dog of a thriller as Hush is something that's positively dumbfounding.

*½
