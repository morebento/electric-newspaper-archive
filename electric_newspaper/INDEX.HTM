LETTERS TO THE EDITOR

Do you want to send a letter?

Monday July 27, 1998

Cold hands, warm heart

I read Chris Warrens' article 'Out in the Cold' 24/7/98 with great interest. Late in '96 and early in '97 I raised concerns publicly through the print and electronic media about the reduction of emergency accommodation beds by the Salvos and Vinnies in Whitmore Sq. My comments were challenged and misrepresented by colleauges in both the government and non-government.

The criticisms came from colleagues adverse to commenting on activities of the religious organisations involved. After trying to hush up the changes, the Salvos and Vinnies had to come clean and claimed that the homeless would not be disadvantaged through the reduction of beds - an unbelievable claim they maintain in Mr. Warrens' story. There is nothing sinister in this plan of closing down the only option for many homeless people, it's about backing winners. I made that point when beds where reduced, and maintain it now during the total closure of emergency accommodation.

There is nothing sexy about emergency accommodation; however it is all some folks can cope with during episodes of home!lessness. The transformation of arrangements for these people from 70 emergency beds in Whitmore Square down to 10 beds, for up to nine weeks (with conditions), at the other end of town, doesn't come across as a solution to the ever-increasing amount of homeless persons.

Where will they go after their nine weeks are up? Nowhere! You can't break the cycle these people are in, in just nine weeks. Someone has to fight on behalf of these people, as their situation makes it difficult for them. I wonder what my ex-colleagues are saying now?

Phil Harrison,
Woodville West
