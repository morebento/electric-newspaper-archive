Thursday February 26, 1998

A fair bit to do at Carrick Hill
by Elaine Moore

"Much Ado About Nothing" is a theatrical experience that should be seen by as many people as possible - those who know Shakespeare's plays and those who have never seen one should book a ticket a.s.a.p. and treat themselves to an enthralling encounter.

The play's performance location is on the manicured and aesthetically delightful lawns and garden before the majestic Carrick Hill House. This setting is superb and cannot help but be one of the major reasons for seeing this production. The mere evocation of Shakespeare's words surrounded by beautiful trees and gardens, aloft a gentle hill overlooking Adelaide, with setting sun reflecting colours in the sky and the distant sea, is an enriching feeling to occasion.

Every lover of beauty should yield to the temptation of beholding such an occasion.

The performance of "Much Ado About Nothing" is by Lightning Strike Inc, an Adelaide-based incorporated non-profit arts association. Originating as "Lightning Strike Theatre Experience" in 1994 this innovative company of new and familiar actors has undoubtedly grown successful out of good motivation, promoting and presenting the art of theatre to the community.

"We have always had as our core the firm belief that the 'Theatrical Experience' is one of the most importantant aspects of any theatrical presentation," says the company

True to this philosophy the performance presents a competent and satisfying rendition of Shakespeare's play, most accordingly expressed within the refreshing reality of the beautiful setting. The actors carry the illusion through without error or strain, but with confidence and energy. Individual strengths of performance did vary, but with no hinderence to the play or each other. The cast has strong mainstayers and the talent amongst the group is clearly flourishing moment by moment.

"Much Ado About Nothing" is a classic Shakespeare comedy. It is slowly revealed and kaleidoscopes the tensions of love, hate, and jealousy. The plot reveals the complexities of intrusive manipulation. On the simplest of levels the action can be followed with the mirth of entertainment and intellectually it is satisfying and invigorating to both the learned and learning intellects.

The costumes in this production are striking and attractive, and the lighting and use of space amongst the sprawling audience helps make this an enjoyable Shakespearian experience.

Chairs are provided on the outskirts, beside the rose gardens or the giant hedge. Many people bring a bottle of wine, or buy a glass of wine and picnic, expanding their sensory pleasures.

Having had previous opportunities to experience Shakespeare's artistry in Botanic Gardens and on the cliffs of Cornwall at the Minack Theatre in the U.K., it is with thanks that I am able to favourably compare this performance with such memorable occasions and I strongly recommend you to venture to such an experience.
