Friday March 13, 1998

Performance 10, Location Nil
"Kissing the Goldfish"
Reviewed by Ray Thomas

A sell out at Edinburgh, big crowds at the Hong Kong fringe. Short changed in Adelaide. "Kissing the Goldfish" is outstanding entertainment. An hour of extraordinarily clever musical miscellany which is worthy of a packed audience, this show has been dealt a severe blow by the Fringe organisers.

The location is abysmal! The carpark theatre is, at least, true to its description. A curtained off section of a multi storey parking lot !

Not the right place for such splendidly special sophistication. And an insult to the performers!

Charlotte Bicknell, currently gracing our screens in "This Life" and Sebastian Michael delight and charm us with "raunchy jazz, tongue-in-cheek glamour, delectable kitsch and sardonic wit."

Add to that the absolute brilliance of Musical Director "Harry", who turns the theme from "Neighbours" into a Rachmaninov Concerto as well as presenting us with many other musical goodies, and you have an hour of entertainment that should be one of the fringe's main talking points.

Let's hope the trio returns. But, please, please, please at a more appropriate location.

Kissing the Goldfish
Carpark Theatre
15-17 Frome Street

Until 15 March at 12.15 am.
