Friday March 2, 1998

Flamin freebies in the Festival
by Elaine Moore

With flames of orchestrated fire, the 1998 Adelaide Festival was brought to life on Friday night at the free opening night concert in Elder Park.

About 30,000 people squeezed onto the lawns beside the River Torrens as the State Opera of South Australia with the Adelaide Symphony Orchestra performed Flamma Flamma.

Illuminated spiritual icons and lanterns of many shapes proceeded through the crowds, carefully choreographed for timing with the surreal vocal and symphonic sounds that hauntingly permeated the air.

The visual display of earthly symbols set alight - flaming hands, spirals and human form, burnt a lasting - and favourable - impression on the huge crowd, a reflection of the very special and auspicious Festival now upon us.

The Squeezebox has been quick to set a festive tempo. Zydeco Jump had the joint jumping on opening nigh - toe tapping, hip shuffling, head wobbling, ear fanning accordion music mixes at this innovative venue on the Festival plaza.

Free entry and a line-up of some interesting performers make this nightly gig very popular.

The accordion is featured in all the acts at the Squeezebox, with the execption of random guest performances from Festival shows. Is this is the beginning of a long association with accordions in Adelaide - this modest, yet ingenious instrument that Robyn Archer has brought to our attention?

Other free events during the Festival include Every Night A Wedding, held in Elder Park at 7pm. You are invited to experience weddings of various cultural traditions as couples share their 'special moment' publicly. On Sunday night, white Spanish dancing horses elegantly lead the way for the bride's carriage at a Flamenco Gypsy wedding. Flamenco dancers strutted their stuff before and after the formal proceedings before a huge crowd. Those who had been to the afternoon session of Carmen would have found the sunset ceremony suitably in sympathy to their mood.

Writers Week also began on Sunday and will continue until the 6th of March. At ten o'clock today 'Meet the Author' presents Michele Roberts (UK) and Julian Barnes (UK) and a busy schedule of authors, poets, and launches continues until 6pm.

Wander down to the tents by the Torrens parade ground and enjoy the discussion - admission is free.

There are also three evening Writers' events with writers in the Town Hall at 8pm ($15, $12 & $10).

Another free event worth checking out is the Festival 'Forum', an open discussion/debate on issues related to our Festival program and Arts and Festival events, held every weekday at the Festival Centre Piano Bar at 1.00 pm.

Forum is chaired by one of Australia's publicly practising intellectuals - Phillip Adams from ABC's Radio National so there's the chance to venture away from the radio for some real-life debate.

Today's topic is 'A country gets the art it deserves - art and the political mood'.

Hence, there are a number of free events in the Festival that will help satisfy those who have little money to spare. Yet, even those who feel the pinch should splurge on at least one Festival performance. The Electric Newspaper will continue to bring you daily reviews.
