Wednesday March 4, 1998

Stainless race
by Adrienne Eccles

"Iron Lace": a collection of poetry and prose by South Australian Writers

Writers' Week's East Tent filled up slowly in the fifteen minutes before starting time. Writers and would-be writers, politicians, students, singers and actors on sabbatical, retirees, workers snatching some time off ... sun hats, spring-water bottles, and the smell of peppermint tea.

The work to be launched was "Iron Lace", a collection of poetry and short stories by last year's students of the University of Adelaide's Creative Writing course.

It's not just any old collection of student writing; these aren't just any old collection of students. Professor Tom Shapcott's Master's degree students are for the most part experienced writers. Their work is polished and insightful.

University of Adelaide Vice-Chancellor Mary O'Kane introduced Arts Minister Di Laidlaw, managing to get a plug in for the course and pointing out the Head of the English Department in the third row.

Diana Laidlaw, her Minister's hat at a windblown angle from rushing to her late arrival, took the podium. Shapcott's Chair of Creative Writing is the only Professorship of its kind in Australia. There are other Creative Writing courses in Universities in Australia, but Adelaide is the first to have a Professor. The Chair is jointly funded by the University and the State Government. The Minister joked that there is little subtlety in relations between Government and university: it all comes down to funding. The Vice-Chancellor smiled.

Declaring the book launched, the Minister gave way on the podium to some of the writers who read extracts of their work. Tess Driver began with an extract from her short story "The Lake", reminiscences of expatriate experiences in America in the sixties. ("The neighbours had heard we had a Prime Minister who drowned while swimming. They wanted to know where his security guards were...") She was followed by Maureen "V for Verse" Vale and Glen Johns, both of whom read poems.

The audience sighed and clapped their appreciation for hearing the writers and their works speak for themselves.

* "Iron Lace" is available at Writers' Week from the Unibooks tent for $14.95.
