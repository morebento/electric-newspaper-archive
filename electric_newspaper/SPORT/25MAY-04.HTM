Monday May 25, 1998

Monte Carlo: action all the way

McLaren driver Hakkinen wons the Monte Carlo Grand Prix in commanding fastion, crossing the line 11seconds in front of Fisichella, with Farrari's Eddie Irvine a distant third.

At the start of the race the two McLaren drivers, Hakkinen abd Coulthard both made good starts and remained in grid position as the field funnelled into Ste Devote corner. They are followed by Giancarlo Fisichella and Michael Schumacher. Further back Alexander Wurz maked a very good start to get ahead of Heinz-Harald Frentzen. The only accident victim on a busy first lap was Minardi's Esteban Tuero who hit the wall at Massenet.

In the early laps the two McLarens pulled quickly away from the field with Mika Hakkinen building up a lead of 3.2secs in the first four lap over David Coulthard although the Scotsman was able to pull away from the rest of the field, which was led by Fisichella, although the Benetton driver was having to battle hard to hold on to fourth place with Schumacher trying everything to get ahead.

The McLaren drivers set 12 fastest laps between them between lap four and lap 12 as they battled for supremacy. Coulthard closed the gap to get within two seconds of the leader but then Hakkinen reacted and began to build the lead again. Behind them Fisichella finally managed to break clear of Schumacher. Further back Wurz was having a lonely race in fifth place. Behind him Frentzen ran sixth but he was under considerable pressure from Eddie Irvine. Irvine challenged at the Loews Hairpin on lap 10 and the two cars collided, causing Frentzen to retire.

By the seventeenth lap Hakkinen was 3.7secs ahead of Coulthard but on the next lap the pressure on the Finn eased when David suffered an engine failure at the chicane. This left Hakkinen with a lead of nearly 20secs over Fisichella.

The order remained unchanged until the pit stops began on lap 30. The first to stop was Michael Schumacher, followed into the pits on lap 31 by Fisichella. This left Wurz in second position but under extreme pressure from Michael Schumacher, while Fisichella was back in fourth place.

On lap 37 Hakkinen came in for his scheduled pit stop and on the next lap Schumacher challenged Wurz at the Loews Hairpin. The young Austrian refused to give way and retook the place. As they dived into Portiers Schumacher challenged again and the two banged wheels side by side. Schumacher took the position but the move damaged his left rear suspension and he had to pit at the end of the lap. He rejoined three laps down but charged hard for the rest of the afternoon.

The accident did not seem to affect Wurz. He pitted for new tyres on lap 42 but on his first lap out of the pits he suffered a probable suspension failure in the tunnel and had a huge accident, the car ending up hitting the tyre barriers at the chicane.

Hakkinen maintained his 20sec lead over Fisichella with Irvine third half a minute behind the Benetton. Alesi had moved up to fourth with Mika Salo fifth and a lapped Jacques Villeneuve in sixth.

Jean Alesi was the only major driver on a two-stop strategy and he pitted again on lap 55. He rejoined behind Salo in fifth place and charged hard but retired with mechanical troubles with five laps to go.

Fisichella closed to within 18secs of Hakkinen but on lap 57 he spun at Rascasse and lost another 12secs. In the closing laps Michael Schumacher unlaped himself but on the last lap he made a mistake and collided with sixth-placed Pedro Diniz, removing his Ferrari's front wing.

Both drivers were able to limp to the finishing line.
