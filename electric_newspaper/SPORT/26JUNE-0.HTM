Friday June 26, 1998

Rams Scram ... to Hindmarsh
by Phil Owens

Exactly what more could go wrong! In the middle of a very ordinary season for the Rams, following a public relations disaster on the field in Whyalla last Saturday and amid a rugby league drugs scandal that is possibly the worst in the history of Australian sport, the Adelaide Rugby League Football Club yesterday announced the Rams are leaving Adelaide Oval to play out their home season at Hindmarsh Stadium.

The Rams official announcement stated that their tenancy agreement had been terminated by the South Australian Cricket Association over disagreement over payments associated with catering at their matches.

They also claim that fans will benefit because of superior viewing facilities at the purpose built soccer stadium. Since the National Soccer League fixtures are played in summer and National Rugby League is a winter competition, the arrangement seems sensible.

There was no mention of the Rams playing location for 1999.

It's hard to imagine a more difficult position for the Rams. The team has not won a single game away from Adelaide Oval this season.

Their founding coach, Rod Reddy was unceremoniously fired only a few weeks ago.

And now they've been kicked out of home!

After the debacle of their performance against the modestly credentialled and moderately performing Illawarra Steeler's in Whyalla, an analytical preview of their prospects against the Gold Coast Chargers at Cararra seems pointless.

It honestly seems that some of the Rams supporters and fans have more enthusiasm, committment and rugby league understanding than many of the players!

As usual, there have been significant team changes. New coach Dean Lance seems to have quickly been forced to adopt this method, which was common under his predecessor.

First hand reports of the Rams play against the Steeler's are universally depressing. The performance could be summarised as "inept and uncommitted".

I feel genuine pity for the loyal supporters who drove for 4-5 hours from Adelaide to watch the embarrassing display and then drove straight back from Whyalla.

It's no surprise really that Lance has made six changes to the team. He has also delisted two players and banished them to the "minor leagues" in Brisbane. I wonder if he was upset?

I doubt that the Rams coach could be as upset as the NRL bosses and the management of the Melbourne Storm.

The league confirmed yesterday that Rodney Howe tested positive to a banned performance enhancing drug and the test was confirmed in the duplicate urine sample.

This guy has just had a half season of the best performances by a front row forward since Harragon was at his peak a few years ago. He represented Australia against New Zealand and New South Wales against Queensland only a few weeks ago. Until a yesterday, he was arguably the best prop in the world on current form.

Many commentators and fans are asking "Are drugs the reason that Melbourne Storm, in their inaugural season in the national rugby league competition, are leading the premiership table?"

Should Storm be disqualified from all matches in which Howe played this year?
