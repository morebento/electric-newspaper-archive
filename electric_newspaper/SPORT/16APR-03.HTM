Thursday April 16, 1998

Pick of the NRL round: Cronulla at home to Balmain (7.30 pm Sat 18 Apr)
From Anthony Sinclair in the Tiger's Den

The Balmain Tigers face possibly their greatest challenge of the season thus far when they travel to Shark Park for the Saturday night clash with the Sharks. Cronulla made the Super League Grand Final last year but have only won 2 of their first 5 games this season.

Cronulla welcome the return of Jason Stevens and Danny Lee this weekend. Lee was injured and Stevens was suspended. The loss of Paul Green, Craig Greenhill and Paul Donahey to injury has been felt by the Sharks this season, but this is a strong side.

The only change to the Tigers lineup is Chris St.Clair onto the bench for the injured Jody Rudd. Rudd will undergo a knee re-construction today after breaking and dislocating his leg. We wish him all the best. Darren Senter is still out with a broken hand, he is out for another 2 weeks.

The forward battle will again be a beauty. The Sharks only know one way of taking the ball up and the Tigers will be ready for them. The outside backs will also be staging a contest of their own. Matt Rogers is the form winger and point scorer in the NRL and ET is never to be underestimated but Laloa is coming off his best game ever and Bubba and Webber are rarely outplayed.

The Tiger halves have gained plenty of wraps recently and they will again hold the key. The Cronulla side is much better with Paul Green wearing the #7.

Mitch Healey is famous for his bombs so field position and helping Nevin and the wingers at the back when the bomb goes up will also be important.

In recent years the Tigers haven't travelled well and the Sharks at home are as tough an assignment as it gets. But Melbourne managed to beat them at home and if the Tigers play like they have over the past 3 weeks then we should get the 2 points. The Tigers are favourites on FootyTab, with Cronulla getting 2.5 points start.
