Tuesday May 26, 1998

NRL: Successful Rams lineup off to pluck Magpies
by Phil Owens

The inclusion of Adelaide's leading points scorer centre Luke Williamson is the only change to last Saturday's winning lineup to take on the Western Suburbs MAGPIES in Sydney on Saturday 31st May.

Williamson's return has forced rugged Dave Kidwell onto the interchange bench.

Joe Timani has missed out completely.

The Rams have been served very well by their substitute three quarters Bird, Appo and Kidwell covering for injuries to Williamson, Wayne Simonds, Peter Clarke and Rod Maybon.

Williamson was fortunate to miss only one match after his neck injury in Parkes against the Eels. During his absence, Appo handled the goal kicking duties and landed 5 from 6 attempts against the Panthers.

The team to take on Wests looks like the best outfit the Rams have ever fielded. Amazing when you consider that original squad members Bruce Mamando, Dave Boughton, Craig Kimmorley and Rod Maybon are all out for the season.

Also, former Bronco's forward Brett Galea's calf injury will keep him out of consideration for at least another week. Strong and experienced centre Peter Clarke's knee injury will see him sidelined for another 2 months, and Wayne Simonds will be missing for another 5 weeks.

The strength of the Rams current lineup can be gauged by the "luxury" of having Andrew Hick on the interchange bench. He topped the national rugby league tackle count for round 9 with 41 hits against Parramatta!

If Adelaide's forwards can sustain for 15 minutes the same opening intensity they showed against Penrith and the team plays mistake free in their own territory as they did last week, they should record their first away win of the season and get off the bottom of the table.

They certainly now have plenty of firepower in the backline!
