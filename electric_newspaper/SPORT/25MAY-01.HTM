Monday May 25, 1998

NRL: Rams fleece Panthers
by Phil Owens

Adelaide's recent horrific run of injuries forced coach Dean Lance to send out a squad containing a young and inexperienced backline to meet Penrith on a damp Adelaide Oval under a very chilly Saturday night sky. Ram rookies Bowen, Bird and Appo combined brilliantly on Adelaide's first possession to score an 80 metre try and put the visiting Penrith Panthers behind 6-0 inside the first two minutes if the match.

The stunned Panthers took too long to recover from the early setback. The Rams went on to record their second win of the season with an impressive and emphatic 35-16 victory.

The young backline combination Adelaide fielded on Saturday night showed agility, speed, handling and surprising coolness under pressure. The Rams finally seem to have attacking options out wide to capitalise on the typically strong performances of their forwards.

The Panthers have been performing inconsistently this year. Their strength is their big, fast, skilled and seasoned backline led by the great Greg Alexander. However, they were completely outplayed by the young Adelaide three-quarters, intelligently positioned and fed by Adelaide half-back Noel Goldthorpe.

Outside 'Thorpie', Craig Bowen (five-eighth), Dave Kidwell, Graham Appo (centres), Rod Jensen, Mat Daylight (wingers) and NSL first-grade debutant Dion Bird (full-back) played extremely well.

Adversity can do some strange things. Forced to make changes and find substitutes to cover injuries to regular squad members, Adelaide coach Dean Lance sent out a backline of unheralded players that produced more than any other previous Rams combination.

Warning bells were ringing for Penrith from the moment Adelaide first handled the ball.

Penrith received Adelaide's opening kick-off and completed six plays successfully despite energetic and committed tackling by the Rams. Greg Alexander's punt kick on the sixth play found the west touchline 20 metres from the Adelaide tryline.

From the play-the-ball after the regulation scrum, a short pass from Craig Bowen found Dion Bird speeding into a gap. Bird went through and found Appo in support on his left. He passed to Appo who then rocketed 70 metres downfield, outpaced Panther's flyer Robbie Beckett and touched down. Appo converted his own try with a good place kick from out wide aided by a following south-westerly wind.

After three minutes, it was Rams 6 Panthers 0.

When Penrith first managed to get some ball and some territory, it was clear that their tactics were designed to neutralise Adelaide's forwards, their recognised strength. However, the Panther's game plan backfired.

The Rams forwards stuck to their task, ignoring Penrith's niggling distractions, and made good ground through and near the rucks. Eventually, Penrith were trapped in their goal area and forced to drop kick out from between their posts.

Attempts to rattle the Rams forwards finally came to the attention of referee Mogsheen Jadwat. He penalised Panther's front row forward Mat Adamson for a head-high tackle on his opposite Mark Corvo about 25 metres out and smack in front of the Penrith goal posts.

Appo slotted the penalty place kick through the uprights and Adelaide led 8-0 after six minutes.

A Ram's knock-on after the restart gave the Panthers the loose-head and put-in to a scrum set on halfway. Penrith squandered their first chance to get into the game by spilling the ball.

Adelaide could do little wrong and everything Penrith touched turned to grit in this period.

Adelaide's second try followed a good break from Tony Iro who shedded several tackles advancing the Rams territorially. Goldthorpe nicely weighted a grubber behind the Penrith defensive line and second rower Mark Corvo was on the spot to pick up the crumbs and touch down. Appo converted with a good place kick from out wide.

Fifteen minutes gone and it was Adelaide 14 Penrith 0.

Adelaide's forwards maintained their concentration and intensity of effort. Goldthorpe exploited this with long kicks into touch. This drove the Panthers back into their own territory and gave his forwards little 'breathers' as the scrums were set.

Penrith made very heavy weather trying to get out of their own territory in the face of smothering Adelaide defence.

The Ram's territorial stranglehold was finally broken by a great bust from Panther's second row forward Jody Gall that gave his team great field position. Penrith did not waste their second opportunity in Adelaide territory.

On Adelaide's next possession, Penrith 'points machine' Ryan Girdler adeptly anticipated a long soaring cut-out pass from Goldthorpe to bag a 30 metre intercept try. Girdler easily kicked the conversion. After 23 minutes, Penrith were on the board trailing 6-14.

Girler's try lifted Penrith and their intensity increased in attack and defence. Lock Don Cross produced a big tackle-busting burst up the middle. Adelaide started conceding penalties for infringing the tackled Penrith ball carrier attempting to play the ball.

During this period, a very threatening Penrith raid down the eastern touchline was brought to an emphatic fullstop by a try saving tackle from Dave Kidwell.

Adelaide simply refused to buckle under pressure. Penrith became a little anxious and frustrated. They pushed one pass too many and turned the ball over.

The Ram's forwards, sparked by a timely dummy half run from winger Mat Daylight, rolled Penrith behind the advantage line on successive plays. Adelaide prop forward Mart McKenzie then made a big break on the right. Desperate Penrith defence subsequently stopped Dion Bird just short of a try when the Rams ran on a sixth tackle play.

At this stage, Penrith were really struggling to get out of their half.

They were then dealt a cruel blow when half-back Alexander painfully hurt his shoulder after being tackled late. He was forced to retire. Penrith were to sorely miss Alexander's cool genius in the second half when the game was there to be taken by the visitors.

Worse was to come for Penrith when their centres unleashed a dangerous looking attack down the western side two minutes before half-time. A very poor pass intended for Panthers flanker Beckett found unmarked Rams winger Rod Jensen, who raced 80 metres to touch down in the north western corner. Appo's conversion kick failed but Adelaide went to the break accompanied by a standing ovation leading 18-6.

It was a good lead but not a match-winning break. When play resumed, the Panther's commitment remained as intense as it was in the latter period of the first half. Their cause was considerably helped during this period by a succession of penalties awarded to them by referee Jadwat against Adelaide defenders again interfering in the play-the-ball.

Penrith were however denied by solid Adelaide defence and by counter attacks from Rams skipper Kerrod Walters and Kiwi international Kevin Iro.

The weight of possession and penalty driven territorial advantage trapped the Rams inside their own in goal area twice in this period.

In what was to turn out to be the critical phase of the match, Adelaide repeatedly answered a determined Penrith in defence and attack. Again the Rams rookies featured.

Jensen, Bird and Appo combined well in defence to drive a Penrith attack into touch just short of the tryline in the north east corner. Then Appo, Kidwell, Iro and Corvo successively made hard metres to drive Penrith backwards.

Sixteen minutes into the second half, the persistent Panthers were finally rewarded when Carter and Girdler combined with Sid Domic to put their fullback Peter Jorgenson over for a try. Girdler's conversion place kick was successful.

Penrith were coming back at 12-18.

It was the closest they were to get.

From the resumption, Penrith immediately tore into the Rams defence, trapping them in goal once again at the 20 minute mark of the second half.

An unforced Panther's error let Adelaide off the hook. They fumbled the drop out catch and Jadwat ordered a scrum set 5 metres inside Adelaide territory with an Adelaide feed and loose head. This was the turning point of the game.

Ahead by only a converted try, the Rams pressured the Panthers, lifted by a great run from five-eighth Craig Bowen.

The small frozen crowd gave Jadwat a 'Bronx' cheer when he then awarded Adelaide their first penalty for 20 minutes about 15 metres inside their opponent's half. Appo stepped up to take the punt for the line. Kicking into the wind, he drove the ball over the eastern sideline only 5 metres short of Penrith's tryline.

The damp ball, slippery surface and player fatigue were now contributing to handling errors by both teams. It was evident how much Penrith missed Alexander when they made a complete mess of their kick on a sixth tackle play.

For Adelaide, forwards Mark Corvo and Adam Peek were bursting through the defence at will. Penrith continued to make errors as the possibility of a loss started to become a reality for them.

Sensing an Adelaide win, the crowd urged the Rams onwards.

Adam Peek and then Noel Goldthorpe made great busts up the middle and Rod Jensen came within less than a metre of touching down in the south-east corner.

Penrith recovered the ball but spilled it on their first play inside their own ten metres.

By now the exhausted Panthers were consistently falling off tackles. Kerrod Walters almost got through for a try running from dummy half about 15 metres out.

Again Penrith regained possession and again they surrendered it almost immediately.

Then the game took an interesting twist, starting with the loopiest field goal you could imagine from Andrew Pierce.

After 66 minutes, Adelaide 19 Penrith 12.

Needing to score twice to win, Penrith completely dropped their bundle. Recognising this, the Rams decided to come out and play. And didn't they enjoy themselves!

Twenty-three points were scored in last ten minutes of the game on top of the previous 31!

Ram's lock forward Dean Schifilitti opened festivities with a long powerful burst up the centre of the ruck. Then Craig Bower sliced through the defence, punted high over the fullback‘s head and raced through to secure the ball under a tackle one metre out. Rod Jensen raced through in support, scooped up the ball and dived over to touch down from dummy-half.

Adelaide 23 Penrith 12 in the 72nd minute. Game over!

Appo missed a difficult place kick into the breeze attempting to convert the try but Adelaide went straight back into top gear from the kick restart. Iro cruised straight through the wilting defence. Then Corvo easily pushed clear into space and passed inside to the exuberant Schifilitti who scored a try under the crossbar. Appo added the extras.

Adelaide 29 Penrith 12.

The Rams fumbled the kick off and Dean McGillivray scored a consolation try for Penrith that Girdler converted.

Adelaide 29 Penrith 18.

Appo then produced a piece of magic to entertain the hardy band of rugby league loyalists that braved the chilly conditions. He kicked off low and shallow and regathered the ball in front of a bemused panther's front row. Goldthorpe then sliced straight through the centre and ran 25 metres before unloading to Appo who scored the Rams sixth try of the night. Appo converted from in front on full-time.

Adelaide 35 Penrith 18

The Ram's clearly enjoyed the response of their hardy supporters after the game.

Next week Adelaide meet Western Suburbs at Campbelltown. An away win will see them move off the bottom of the premiership ladder.
