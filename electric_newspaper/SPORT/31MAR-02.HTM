Tuesday March 31, 1998

Rugby League Champs Set Early Pace with Unlikely Company
by Phillip Owens

After 3 rounds of the regular home-and-away program of the inaugural National Rugby League season four teams remain undefeated and one team is yet to win a game.

It would not surprise many that the Newcastle KNIGHTS, last year's Australian Rugby League Premiers, and the Brisbane BRONCOS, winner's of the Superleague Championship, are early NRL competition leaders. A Newcastle-Brisbane Grand Final to end this season is a very appealing prospect because it might help settle the dispute over which was the superior faction in last year's divided competition and could also go some way to healing the pain that is a legacy of the league war.

But who would have predicted that the North Queensland COWBOYS and the Melbourne STORM would also start 1998 with three straight wins? The North Queensland turnaround is astonishing because last year they finished bottom in the Superleague. The Melbourne STORM result is equally unexpected, since they didn't even exist before 1998 and are based in the heartland of the Australian Football League.

It will be interesting to see whether North Queensland and Melbourne can stay on or near the top of the table over the full 24 rounds of the regular season, but based on current form their chances of making the 10 team playoffs can't be discounted.

The 0-3 result for the South Sydney RABBITOS has surprised no one, as sad as it is to see this former great club extend it's losing streak.

There will be many who are puzzled, and no doubt many more who will be pleased, by the poor starts to this season by Manly and Sydney City, both of whom have been slow out of the blocks and have a 1 win 2 loss record, along with another 10 teams, including last year's newcomers, the Adelaide RAMS.

Perhaps some of the more experienced "stars" in the more highly fancied teams have become a little complacent against opposition that they expect to beat based on last season's form? Last Friday night's series of upsets (Balmain beat Manly, Auckland beat Sydney City, Adelaide beat Canterbury) might have reminded coaches and players that last season's form counts for nothing.
