Tuesday May 12, 1998

National Rugby League: Tipsters surrender
by Phil Owens

: The weekend started off OK. By Saturday night most tipsters would have been congratulating themselves on their acute understanding of the game, the players, the weather, the referees and ... well ... probably everything.

Business as usual, or so it seemed.

On Friday night the Adelaide Rams waited until the Canberra Raiders were being issued with Ansett boarding passes before they started to play football. Their 24-18 loss was spot on their average losing margin at Adelaide Oval in 1998.

On the same evening at Leichardt Oval the "million dollar men" from Parramatta managed to scrape home 21-18 against the "work experience" boys from Balmain.

In Auckland, the Warriors showed again that without the national New Zealand jumpers on their backs they can't find the commitment needed to win, succumbing to the Cronulla Sharks by 20-8.

Only an expert, and there's no shortage of these is there, would have predicted that St George were going to inflict the first home defeat on Melbourne Storm. The Dragons had just won five on the trot, so this wasn't a complete surprise. Not to an "expert" anyway.

So Friday night went by more or less as expected.

Saturday served up more of the same. Top teams beating bottom teams. Bottom teams taking turns to beat each other. The natural order of things.

In Wollongong, the Steelers crushed the spirited but completely undermanned Wests Magpies to the tune of 42-20, to keep Illawarra's finals chances realistic.

At Carrara on the Gold Coast, the Penrith Panthers bumbled to a 26-12 loss to the slightly more lowly Chargers.

The big action was expected to be on the Sunday. And so it was. But weren't there a few surprises in store?

The Bears were expected to easily clean up the Rabbitohs, which they duly did to the pleasant tune of 26-14. Souths internal problems persist. They may be forced to try the radical surgery without anaesthetic that the Rams execs pulled last week.

And then, everything seemed to go wrong somehow.

Patchy Canterbury were definitely not supposed to beat the Cowboys in Townsville. The mighty Newcastle Knights couldn't accomplish that task, so how could the Bulldogs do it?

Oh well, experts can cope with exceptions to the rule.

But more was to come.

The purpose of the Sydney City Roosters trip to ANZ stadium was to be sacrificed for the crowd by the Broncos. This would be the 25th straight home win for Brisbane.

Someone forgot to tell the Roosters. They withstood intense pressure, held their nerve, came from behind twice and finally capitalised on Brisbane errors to inflict a savage 26-12 loss on Alfie and the superstars.

That result was a surprise but could not even compete with the shock of Newcastle's 50-16 hiding of Manly!

Last September, it took 79 minutes for the Knights to get on top of the Sea Eagles in one of the most exciting grand finals in many years.

Last Sunday, Manly were reeling at 12-0 after only seven minutes. Then in a four minute period near the end of the first half, Newcastle scored three tries to go to the towels and oranges break leading 30-6.

Game gone!

Sea Eagles coach Bob Fulton claims he can't remember coaching a team that had 50 points scored against it in a game.

I'd be surprised if Fulton even played in a team that was spanked this hard.

There are now five teams with seven wins from nine of the scheduled 24 games in the minor round: Brisbane, Sydney City, Newcastle, Melbourne and St George. North Sydney and Parramatta are just one win behind the front runners. Two points further back is another five team pack with five wins each: Canterbury, Canberra, Balmain, Cronulla and North Queensland.

Manly are scraping along the bottom. Any deeper and they'd run aground.

What's going on?

Well, two things seem certain. Tipping winners is going to get harder. Picking margins will become impossible.
