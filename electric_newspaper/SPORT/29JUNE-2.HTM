Monday June 29, 1998

Chargers Rammed by "bad Boy" Appo
by Phil Owens

They've done it at last! The Adelaide RAMS chalked up their fourth win of the season on Saturday night when they rewrote their scoring record in thrashing the Gold Coast CHARGERS by 40 points to 12. This is the RAMS first away victory for 1998.

It finally had to happen. Sooner or later a good RAMS effort would coincide with a poor one from their opposition. The CHARGERS obliged.

Adelaide's defence in the first half resisted a surfeit of attacking opportunities for Gold Coast. The Chargers enjoyed a wealth of possession in the first 40 minutes due to poor handling and a string of penalties conceded by the Rams.

However, Adelaide showed commitment in defence and intensity in tackles that was missing from their poor display against Illawarra the previous week in Whyalla. Both sides scored two tries in the first half. Jason Nichol and Graham Mackay crossed for the Coast, Alan Wieland (on debut) and Deon Bird touched down for the southerners. Adelaide went to the break leading 12-8 courtesy of goals to stand in kicker Graham Appo.

Improved ball control from Adelaide and poor passing from Gold Coast allowed the Rams to completely bury the Chargers in a second half scoring blitz of 28-4.

It was game over inside 11 minutes.

Rams centre Appo, suspended for disciplinary reasons last week, started the spree early in the second half when he gathered a Chargers fumble, sprinted 40 metres evading a couple of tackles before offloading to winger Rod Jensen who scored under the crossbar.

Five minutes later Appo intercepted a Charger pass ten metres from the Rams line and raced the length of the field to touch down in the right corner.

Three minutes later Jensen helped himself to yet another Coast fumble to run 85 metres and score his second try.

The Chargers fought back but their poor handling in the first half of the second stanza had given the Rams too many opportunities. With speedsters like the agile Appo and strong Jensen, the current Rams lineup can capitalise on their opponents errors.

The damage was done.

Craig Teevan scored a try for Gold Coast, but a few minutes later Appo was at it again, beating two tackles in another 80 metre try scoring run.

To cap of an outstanding performance, Appo finished off a series of Rams passes to score his hat trick!

A very disappointingly small crowd witnessed the Rams rack up the highest score in their short history and saw Appo set an individual point scoring record.

Appo's personal tally of 24 points eclipsed the previous individual Rams scoring record of 18 set by Appo in the win over Penrith four weeks ago.

The victory reversed Adelaide's 16-8 loss to Gold Coast in round 5 in Adelaide.

The Rams now join Penrith and Wests to share 16th spot on the ladder, one win clear of bottom placed Souths and Gold Coast.

The away win, while overdue, suggests Adelaide may yet be able to salvage something positive from what has been a season of turmoil, underachievement and missed opportunities.

Adelaide 40 tries to Appo 3, Jensen 2, Wieland & Bird; goals to Appo 6

Gold Coast 12 tries to MacKay, Nichol & Teevan

Crowd 3,897
