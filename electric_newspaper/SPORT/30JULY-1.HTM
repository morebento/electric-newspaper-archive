Thursday July 30, 1998

Crows miss the score: what now for the eight?
by Brendan Harris

Twelve seconds left on the clock. The Crows are down by a point. All 36 players are inside the Crows' 50 metre line. Nigel Smart uncannily finds space, he takes several steps and kicks. Will the ball travel far enough? Is the accuracy there? Its a high ball! Its got the distance. It will be a goal and a win! It will certainly collect a point and draw! Some confusion. The boundary umpire extends his arms and agony grips the hearts of thousands, thousands of miles away in Adelaide. The Dockers have won!

A 100 point turn-around from the Football Park annihilation of round 2. The Crows had lost another one they were expected to win. They seemed assured of a spot in the top few - but now have a real chance of actually missing the eight. The taunt of one hit wonders will have some extra sting!

It was an exciting final quarter of football. Three quarter time and the scores were level: 

Fremantle 	9	11
Adelaide	10	5

With 13 minutes to go, Fremantle was 11 points in front.

Vardy's goaled and reduced the margin to 5 points.

Another win at the centre bounce to Fremantle, a goal to Brown and the margin was restored.

12 minutes remained and Ricciuto's long screw punt finds Jarman who gets number eight.

The Margin was 5 points.

Ten minutes to the final siren and Fletcher goaled and Fremantle extended their lead to 11 points.

Modra ran into an open goal and the margin was again reduced to five points.

Nine minutes to go and Jarman marked and missed an attempt at goal.

The Dockers' Brown shot from 40 metres but missed and scored a point. The margin was five points to the Dockers.

Six minutes to go and Connell, the West Australian, goals and the Crows were a point in front!

Callaghan scores a point and the scores were level: 97 points.

There was plenty of time left: four and a half minutes on the clock.

The Dockers scored a point: 98 to 97.

With less than two minutes to go Jarman lined up for a set shot, 55 metres out.

The match riding on his shoulders.

It went out-on-the-full. 

51 seconds
44 seconds
29 seconds
16 seconds

Then Smart repeats the mistake, out-on-the-full.

Dockers' by a point

Bickley on the ground.

Round 17, Friday night. Its going to be a long weekend! 

Fremantle Dockers 	14	14
Adelaide Crows	15	7

It got worse.

Port Adelaide won against Hawthorn and may replace the Crows in the eight during the next few weeks.

Could Port steel a place in the eight at the expense of the other SA team?

At a 50:1 chance for the flag, it would hurt many.
