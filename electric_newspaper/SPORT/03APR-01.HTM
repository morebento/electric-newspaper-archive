Friday April 3, 1998

Rams beware injured and smarting Panthers
by Phillip Owens

Adelaide travel to Sydney to take on the Panthers at Penrith Oval this Sunday in a round four clash in the National Rugby League competition. Penrith were pounded underfoot by the galloping Broncos last weekend in Brisbane whereas Adelaide celebrated its first win of the season after Canterbury snatched defeat from the jaws of victory last Friday night at Adelaide Oval.

So far both teams have one win and two losses for two premiership points (equal 8th discounting points scored and conceded) along with another ten teams in the 20 club NRL. The loser will fall into the fourth rung of the premiership table. The winner will remain 4 points adrift of the competition lead.

Last year in the Adelaide club's first season, Penrith was the only team to lose twice to the Rams. Can Penrith break their duck against Adelaide?

Their chances of extracting payback on the Rams have been set back by the loss of Craig Gower, the Australian Superleague hooker forward, who unfortunately broke his hand last week. However, unlike last season's team, this Panther's outfit contains the backrow forward Mark Geyer. The volatile and controversial Geyer is one of the most dangerous attacking players in the game. He destroyed the Rams outside defence last year in their Superleague clash with the now defunct Western Reds. Geyer was also given a very hefty suspension from that game at Adelaide Oval, and no doubt he wants to get square. This Sunday's game is his first opportunity. Geyer needs no motivation for this game.

Geyer may also prove more effective now he is back with his mates at Penrith. Panther's centre Ryan Girdler has extended last year's try scoring blitz and, along with Brisbane's Steve Renouf, is the leading tryscorer (five in 3 matches) so far this year. Adelaide's defence must neutralise Geyer's wide running bursts or Girdler and the Panther's three-quarters will be in position to accept Geyer's offloads and make the Rams pay dearly on the scoreboard. Adelaide lock forward Andrew Pierce will start opposite Geyer. He therefore has an important job on his hands in defence. He can count on support from his team mates, especially halfback Noel Goldthorpe and hooker Kerrod Walters for cover, but Pierce must make the first tackle on Geyer count on Sunday or Adelaide may struggle. If the Rams hard working forwards like Cann, McKenzie, Corvo and Galea can get on top of the Panthers pack in attack and defence, as they do with most opposition, Penrith's exciting halfback Greg Alexander will not get as much latitude with the ball. If Goldthorpe and the Rams forwards can deny Alexander both time and space this will prevent him from getting his attack moving forward before he delivers the pass. This will go a long way to minimising the attacking impact of Geyer and Girdler.

On the other hand, Penrith will find Adelaide have more attacking options than they had to contain last year. Noel Goldthorpe's return after serving a one match suspension plus the excellent form of his stand-in last week, Craig Kimmorley, has permitted coach Rod Reddy to select both little men in the starting lineup. Kimmorley and Goldthorpe are good defenders, they time their passes well and have good kicking games. Kimmorley moves to five-eighth and displaces Chris Quinn to fullback in the run on team. These three players plus Kerrod Walters, who is in outstanding form, plus impact player Tony Iro, are sure to create many anxious moments for the mountain men on Sunday. Rams wingers Joe Tamani and Wayne Simonds have shown they know how to get the ball down over their opponent's tryline when given a sniff of space, and their hard running centre partner Peter Clarke knows how to line up gaps in the defensive line before receiving the ball. The running of second row forward Marty McKenzie may also surprise the Panthers defenders.

In other matches, the clash between the front running Melbourne Storm and the North Sydney Bears is the pick of the round. This will be the first rugby league premiership game played in Melbourne in this historical season. There is almost as much interest in the attendance as there is in the game.
