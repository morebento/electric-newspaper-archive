Tuesday June 9, 1998

"Wrong Way" Iro inspires as lowly Rams tame the mighty Dragons
by Phil Owens

Ten seconds before fulltime, Adelaide second row forward Tony Iro ran 40 metres with the ball, the WRONG way down the field. He was cheered by his team mates and by the crowd on his frivolous loopy gambol and he was half-heartedly pursued by befuddled St George defenders. Then the clock ran out on a superb, heart-stopping game of rugby league. Needing to deny St George possession, Iro's only aim was to keep the ball to secure a well deserved Adelaide 22-20 win.

Bottom-placed Adelaide's win over top-placed St George is the first evidence that the Rams have the potential to win more frequently than they have so far this season.

The Rams showed all of the things expected of a genuine rugby league team ... discipline, control, aggression, skill, speed, support for the ball carrier, focus in defence, commitment to the tackles and good old guts and determination.

The Rams performance was remarkable, considering the key ball playing team spots of half-back and five-eighth were filled by “temps”, the ever reliable and resilient Chris Quinn and rookie Deon Bird.

Newly-confirmed Rams' coach Dean Lance cleverly rotated his three-quarters roles in attack and defence. Iro, Quinn and Bird all alternated as first receiver. Appo alternated between fullback in possession and centre in defence.

Twenty minutes before Iro's crazy last minute antics, a humorous and happy ending to the evening for the Adelaide Rams and the 8,500 hardy fans who braved the arctic conditions was nowhere in sight.

Half-way through the second half, St George were cruising along at 20-8 and ready to make it ten wins in succession. Unfortunately for the competition leaders, they left the cruise control on for too long.

Within 16 minutes, Adelaide racked up 14 unanswered points.

Adelaide's win over the premiership co-leaders should lift them from the bottom of the table.

The players, obviously very happy with their gutsy fightback, received a standing ovation from their supporters before they left the field of play.

The dour first half gave no warning of the excitement ahead.

Adelaide kicked off first and immediately showed St George they were in for a tough night at the office when their first tackle of the match rattled Dragon's forward Daniel Wagon.

The Ram's rushing defensive efforts in the first half gave the stars of the St George backline, five-eighth Tony Mundine and centres Mark Coyne and Jamie Ainscough, very limited opportunities in the first 30 minutes.

The Rams scored first when centre Luke Williamson successfully struck a penalty goal after 7 minutes.

He repeated the dose ten minutes later when St George were penalised for illegally stripping the ball from an Adelaide attacker a few metres short of their try line. Referee Paul Kalina found Daniel Wagon guilty of a professional foul and sent the offender to the “sin bin” for ten minutes.

The Ram's seemed very likely to score a try in the movement. Any chance was killed off when Wagon pulled the ball from the grasp of Alan Cann while he was being held by another St George defender.

David Boughton came on at around this time to give Tony Iro a breather. Iro had been much more involved in the action early in game than is customary for the talented New Zealand test player.

Boughton paid special attention to Dragon's key playmaker, Mundine. The Adelaide forward hit the St George five-eighth with crunching tackles on two notable occasions.

St George came very close to scoring several times in the first 25 minutes. Young Graham Appo used his speed and agility to rescue several balls kicked into the in goal area. Rams centre David Kidwell made a try-saving tackle to end a threatening Dragon's attack in this period.

The high flying Dragons inevitably got onto the scoreboard with a beautifully crafted team movement that eventually provided their slippery winger Jimmy Lenihan with a four pointer. Dragon's fullback Lee Murphy successfully goaled the conversion.

The teams went to the ten minute break with St George leading 6-4.

The Rams were behind on the scoreboard at half-time but the only people at Adelaide Oval unsatisfied with the first 40 minutes were the Dragons. None of the Sydney or Brisbane newspaper sports columnists predicted an Adelaide win. Footytab gave the Rams 14 and a half points start.

Adelaide offered fewer fumbles (4) than St George (6) in the first half. The Rams did concede more penalties (7 to 5), so opportunities in possession were about even. Most of the penalties conceded by Adelaide were for tackles above the shoulder.

Rams coach Dean Lance might have worked some sort of magic during the break. Within 3 minutes of the resumption, Tony Iro drew the St George blind side defenders into a slide near half way and neatly timed a pass to a motoring Mark Corvo.

The strong Adelaide front row forward burst through the Dragons defence, raced thirty five metres downfield and found his ever alert winger Mat Daylight charging down the right touchline in support. Corvo drew the last defender, fullback Murphy, and threw a lovely pass to provide Daylight a clear passage to the corner.

The difficult conversion attempt at goal by Luke Williamson was unsuccessful.

Behind 8-4, the Dragons lifted their intensity. Jeff Hardy cleverly exploited the soft footing to repeatedly sprint out of dummy half, eluding the markers who were glued to the mire and picking up 7 to 8 metres at will.

The Rams defence was quickly scrambled allowing Dragons fullback Murphy enough opportunity to elude three despairing attempted tackles and touch down under the posts in the 47th minute. He goaled to convert and St George were back in front 12-8.

It was all Saints for the next ten minutes. Murphy slotted over a penalty in the 55th minute to the derision of the crowd who believed the referee made a fundamental error. This took Saints out to 14-8.

A minute later the Dragons produced a classy team movement that saw Ben Kusto score a try in a very handy position for the conversion. Murphy had no problem with the goal conversion and Saints were now out to 20-8.

With 20 minutes to go and Adelaide needing two converted tries to tie, St George started to toy with the opposition when they had the ball. After all, they hadn't lost a game for almost three months and they were playing one of the bottom teams.

Their overconfidence was unfounded.

Adelaide were to stun the premiership pretenders with three unanswered tries!

Inspirational performances by several of the more experienced Rams, especially Iro, Walters and Quinn, lifted the Rams to a great team effort in the last 25 minutes.

Adelaide's ball control in this crucial period of the game was faultless. At the same time, St George repeatedly coughed up the ball in powerful Rams tackles or simply fumbled the ball. Adelaide's defence during this period surprised St George.

Kerrod Walters' passing from dummy half was particularly good in this period of sustained Rams possession. He delivered the ball beautifully to Iro on several occasions and the tall second rower created opportunities for Adelaide's fast outside backs.

This pair of internationals combined in the 60th minute to give Appo a sniff of chance out wide on the left and the video referee confirmed that the diminutive fullback had somehow snuck in for a four pointer. Dragons 20 Rams 12

Adelaide's defence lifted and St George struggled to get out of their own territory. Chris Quinn really got the pulse rates at Adelaide Oval racing when his persistent attempts to pressure Mundine's kicking game were rewarded with a great kick charge down that Quinn regathered to put the Rams into a great attacking position.

A couple of plays later Walters pass found Iro whose short grubber kick into the Dragons' in goal area in the 67th minute was pounced on by the irrepressible Appo. Dragons 20 Rams 16.

With 12 minutes to play, St George should have taken control of their game to put the Adelaide minnows away. But they failed to take the opportunity. A Dragons' knock-on and then a penalty gave the Rams their chance.

And they carefully constructed play to take advantage.

In the 77th minute, a pass from Walters at dummy half found substitute Sam Falaafi who threw a long high ball to Kidwell. His pass found the flying Daylight who touched down near the right hand corner.

Scores were then level.

Graham Appo then kicked his only goal of the evening to put the home team in front 22-20 with three minutes to play.

Adelaide executed a sensible “no passing” set of allowed six plays and St George turned the ball back to their opposition in their first tackle play.

Game over (almost)!

Tony Iro's impetuosity confirmed the result.

Can the Rams win their first away game of the season when they travel to Olympic Park next weekend to meet rugby league debutantes Melbourne Storm, who are currently THIRD in the NRL rankings?
