Monday June 29, 1998

Broncos back to Front Tonight
by Phil Owens

The second half tryscoring blitz by mercurial Adelaide rugby league speedster Grahan Appo against the Chargers on Saturday sees the Rams full-back come winger come recalcitrant come centre close in on the season's leading tryscorers.

Only 7 players have scored more tries than the dynamic Appo and three of them are performing on one side of the advantage line tonight!

Brisbane take on Sydney City to complete round 16 this evening. The Broncos will be keen to reverse their loss to the Roosters a few weeks ago. There's no love lost between these two teams, and that includes their coaching and administrative staff. This will be full on!

Melbourne's loss to Auckland on Saturday means that a Brisbane win will see them back on the top of the premiership table by virtue of their huge positive balance of points scored and points conceded.

At an average of a tick over 32 points a game, Brisbane are by far the top scoring team in the NRL. That's a clear converted try per game better than the second top scoring team, Norths.

The top three tryscorers after 16 rounds all come from Brisbane, namely Steve Renouf, Darren Smith and Darren Lockyer.

Muzzling that lot will be Sydney City's biggest problem tonight. Plus, of course, Alfie Langer.

Leading the muzzling department at the moment are Newcastle and Melbourne who are averaging 14 points conceded per match.

Brisbane, with an average of 15 points scored against per game, is not doing too shabbily in defence either.

For comparison, the Adelaide Rams are on average scoring only 15 points and having 26 scored against them. If you think that's bad then you wouldn't want be Tommy Raudonikis, coach of the Western Suburb's Magpies, who are conceding about 30 points a game.

Fortunately, the Broncos don't have to face the Bronco's!

Unfortunately, the others have no choice and tonight it's the Rooster turn.

Sydney City have a very good defensive record, conceding only 17 points per match, and are playing at home, but I'll tip Brisbane to win and go back to the front!
