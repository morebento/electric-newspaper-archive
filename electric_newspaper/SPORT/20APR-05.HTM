Monday April 20, 1998

Tipsters flameout as Cowboys, Storm and Tigers crash through
National Rugby League, Round 6 roundup
By Phil Owens

As was the case with the AFL, few pundits picked last weekend's NRL winners.

The only correct outcome predicted, and this was a unanimous view, was that Adelaide would lose.

The quality of football was generally very good, and the number of upsets added spice to what is proving to be an exciting season.

Surpise number one was Manly's demolition of the previously unbeaten Brisbane on Friday night. In only their second win of the year, the Sea Eagles never let the Broncos into the match. Brisbane's defeat may have something to do with the ommission of veteran half back Alan Langer and inclusion of his Manly opponent, Geoff Toovey, in the Australian team to meet New Zealand on Friday.

Saturday was full of surprises. The biggest was North Queensland's win over the previously unbeaten Newcastle Knights. The Cowboys' were coming off their first loss of the season, a flogging at the hands of the Broncos. The rapid return to the winning list by the Cowboys against such tough opponents suggests that the North Queensland team is going to figure in this years playoffs, and may have the character to win finals matches. The upsets continued in the other Saturday fixtures. In very close matches, Illawarra climbed out of the cellar to register their second win of the season, beating Canberra 22-20, and Balmain continued their winning streak by sneaking out of Shark Park with an 11-10 win over Cronulla.

Sunday was full of interest and rewarded fans with some exciting football. The game between the Melbourne Storm and the Paramatta Eeels at Paramatta Stadium, the last half of which was broadcast by the ABC on PNN (AM 972), was a gripping encounter. The lead changed several times in a "one-end-to-the-next" contest. Storm eventually washing the Eeels 32-22. The Bears clobbered the Warriors 44-26 at North Sydney Oval, which should leave the Kiwi's smarting in the leadup to Friday's test against Australia. The remaining Sunday games were between teams that have been running hot and cold. St George capitalised on Steve Carter's time in the sin-bin to skip away and outlast Penrith 30-24. South Sydney completely reversed the previous the result and almost the score in their 41-10 drubbing of the Magpies, and Canterbury bounced back to the winning list thrashing the Gold Coast 50-10.

The competion has now separated into two groups. The top nine teams have won four or five games, followed by eight teams with two wins and three with only one victory this season. Brisbane, Newcastle, Balmain, Melbourne and North Queensland head the table with a loss each (ten competition points), closely followed by Norths, Sydney City, Parramatta and St George on eight points. There's a four point gap to the rest. Canterbury are currently in the top ten on for and against percentages.

Canberra, Manly and Cronulla are currently outside the top ten. Expect these perennial finalists to move up the ladder in the weeks ahead.

See you at Adelaide Oval on Saturday night when ET, Rogers and co turn the Rams into shark bait.
