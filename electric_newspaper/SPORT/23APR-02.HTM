Thursday April 23, 1998

Why is Melbourne better than Adelaide?
by Phil Owens

The Adelaide Rams rugby league team was blown off the Sydney Football Stadium in the rain to the tune of a dry weather score (50-12) last Friday night. Coach Rod Reddy put this and other recent sheepish performances by Adelaide down to "teething" problems. These, the press was informed, are common experiences with new clubs.

The Rams have lost five games in six rounds.

The two point victory over Canterbury in round 3 was a great match.

But, Adelaide were not competitive in their losses, being lapped by their opponents on every occasion.

Reddy's "teething" argument would be more convincing were it not for the Storm from Melbourne.

Melbourne Storm, in their FIRST season, are sensational co-leaders of the National Rugby League, having lost only one game.

The Rams, in their SECOND national season, are rock bottom of the premiership table at the one-quarter mark of the regular home-and-away minor round.

Why is Melbourne more successful than Adelaide?

Why is the Storm as good as any team and the Rams worse than every team?

Were these questions considered by the board of the Adelaide Rugby League Club before chairman Peter Wylie announced on Monday that coach Reddy had 100% support from the board?

Wylie denied there was any substance to rumours that the Rams' coach was about to get the axe.

Followers of the "greatest game of all" remember Rod "Rocket" Reddy as a truly great player for St George, for New South Wales, for Australia and for Queensland State-of-Origin. He was an outstanding lock forward with speed, strength and superb ball handling skills.

However, Reddy is relatively inexperienced as a coach at this level, the Rams being his first stint at the helm in first grade.

In contrast, Melbourne coach, Chris Anderson, is very experienced, having previously successfully coached Canterbury in the Winfield Cup and Wigan in the English first division. In his playing days, Anderson was a classy winger for Canterbury. Like Reddy, Anderson also represented NSW and played tests for Australia.

A further contrast between the Rams and the Storm is in their management. The CEO of Storm is John Ribot. Now here is a person that knows a lot about football AND a lot about managing a successful football club.

Ribot started his senior playing career as a lock and second row forward with Brisbane's Western Suburbs Panthers and represented Queensland in that position in the days before state of origin. He was lured to Sydney where he became a devastating winger for the Western Suburbs Magpies, representing NSW and Australia.

After hanging up his boots, Ribot eventually became CEO of the Brisbane Broncos, the team that changed the very nature of rugby league in the last ten years. More recently, he played a senior management role with News Corp and Superleague.

Perhaps both club team coach and CEO are ingredients in Melbourne's success story?

Perhaps too much is being expected of Reddy?

Does he have access to a "brains trust" and team infrastructure support that is similar to Adelaide's opponents?

If not, can Adelaide remedy this?

See you at the centreline on Saturday night.
