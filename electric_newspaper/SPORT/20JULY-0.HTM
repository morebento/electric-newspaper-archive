Monday July 20, 1998

Rain robs Rams
by Phil Owens

In a tighter than usual round of NRL fixtures the Cowboys withstood twenty minutes of sustained pressure from the visiting Rams at the end of a close encounter at the rain-soaked Malanda Stadium in Townsville on Saturday night. Adelaide and North Queensland were awarded two tries each as the visitors went down by 14-10.

This was a tough call, in many ways.

You can sense the pressure to get one of the top ten spots and a chance to compete in the playoffs is foremost in the player's minds from a glance at the scorecards for round 19.

The North Sydney Bears 34-10 drubbed Melbourne Storm in a top ten clash and St George just failed to threaten Newcastle. Most of the other tussles were close run affairs between clubs close to each other on the premiership table below the “top six”.

And another flogging of Souths and Wests.

Sydney clubs have serious problems. Unfortunately, their managements have been slower than most to recognise the changes in the external environment that have occurred that affect sport in general, if not rugby league in particular.

Managers, coaches and players are aware that in six weeks time the season will abruptly reduce from ten games a week to four when the business end of the season gets underway.

The number of games per weekend will thereafter rapidly nose dive as the ten teams that finish in the top half of the twenty team comp play off for the right to a capture a unique trophy.

The inaugural NRL championship might be a very nice start to a bright new 1999.

Reputations are on the line, contracts are under review and the future is at stake.

Not to mention the commitment of sponsors!

Exposure in the playoffs is the minimum expectation of most of those paying the bills. Finals exposure is the holy grail for all involved.

Well ... it's time.

Following on the heels of the cliffhangers between Auckland and Balmain (21-20) and between Parramatta and Canterbury (7-6) and the 14-all draw between Cronulla and Penrith, the Cowboys and the Rams added further to their two-year history of close matches.

The result, as usual for these protagonists, was frustrating for the Rams.

The rain, wind, travel and referee proved too much for the visitors from Adelaide.

It's now arithmetically safe to discuss the Rams playoff prospects. The Cowboys have ended the dream.

The teams, as usual, were evenly matched. This isn't much of a surprise.

Rams' coach, Dean Lance, played under North Queensland coach Tim Sheens when they were both in Canberra.

Adelaide Rams captain and hooker, Kerrod Walters, was matched up against his brother Steve, hooker for the Cowboys.

You'd imagine these fellahs would have little difficulty figuring out what the other guys will do next.

Wind and rain have dogged Adelaide's confrontations with North Queensland.

On Saturday night, the conditions were primarily responsible for the Ram's Graham Appo's inability to kick the goals needed to give Adelaide four wins in a row.

The electric Ram's centre speedster is a very accurate but relatively inexperienced goal kicker. His kicking strength was tested by the heavy and slippery ball.

The Ram's crossed their opponents tryline twice in the last quarter of play but were denied by referee Steve Chiddy on both occasions.

The Rams are reported to have lodged an official complaint to the NRL about the referee's decisions.

But, with so many games each weekend, and the poor capacity of the Rams and the Cowboys to draw audiences on television in Sydney and Brisbane, there was no video referee on hand to assist.

In other matches, the Dragons showed that discussions between St George and the Illawarra Steelers to amalgamate and form the St George Steel Dragons in 1999, have not dimmed their undoubted ability to be effective in the finals.

Saints were down 28-12 midway through the second half of their match with ARL premiers Newcastle but produced incredible counter-attack to get within a major score of stealing an impossible win. The Knights shakily hung on to win 28-22.

Sydney City obliterated Wests 62-4 in an embarrassment to the league.

See you at Hindmarsh stadium next Saturday afternoon when the Rams show the Warriors just how hard it can be to beat Adelaide at home.
