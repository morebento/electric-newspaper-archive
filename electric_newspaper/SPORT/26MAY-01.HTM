Tuesday May 26, 1998

NRL: Rams take Steelers to Whyalla
by Phil Owens

Why Whyalla?

According to Clipsal Adelaide RAMS General Manager of Marketing, Mr. Keith Sumner, "the Rams are supporting the National Rugby League in its overall objective for each club to play one home game in the country".

The Adelaide Rams rugby league team has already been involved in one such initiative. On May 16th they played their Round 10 fixture against the Eels at Pioneer Oval in Parkes. This was a home game for the Eels which they moved to western NSW from their base at Parramatta Stadium in Sydney's western suburbs.

Taking the national game bush sounds like a great idea, but the exercise seems more like an NRL "directive" than an "objective".

With the NRL stated policy including a reduction in the number of teams in their competition from the current 20 to 14 for season 2000, it seems some clubs are very keen to follow the directives from headquarters.

Who can fault them? Club survival is a big issue. In the case of the Rams, also at stake is the survival of rugby league itself in Adelaide.

Rams management seem acutely aware of this.

At Adelaide Oval on Saturday May 23rd, the occasion of Adelaide's impressive win over Penrith, the club launched two petitions aimed at helping the Rams survive the post 1999 cut.

One petition is to go to the NRL asking for Adelaide to remain part of the condensed 2000 competition.

The other petition is to ask Channel bloody Nine to televise games at a more reasonable hour than midnight Friday and midnight Sunday. The present offerings are literally a nightmare. Surely telecasts of NRL games would attract better audiences in Adelaide than "Pacific Drive"?

In addition to taking the players to Whyalla for a round 15 match against the Illawarra Steelers at Bennett Oval on Saturday 20th June (2.30 pm kickoff time to be confirmed), the club will also "bring along the Clipsal Superscreen, the Toop and Toop cheer girls, our sound system and all the other trimmings that go into making the Rams experience an entertaining outing for the family" according to Mr. Sumner.

Will they take the mascot? Or will they just take the Ram's head and call for local volunteers?

Praising the cooperation of the Whyalla Economic Development Board for its assistance, Mr. Sumner said "we're working on finalising travel and accommodation packages for our season ticket holders and members, and we're also planning a special post match function at which the people of Whyalla and surrounding country areas can meet the Rams players".

I wonder if the committed core of 7,000 Adelaide rugby league fans who have stuck with the team at Adelaide Oval through rain, frost, defeat and victory, and who cannot travel to Whyalla for the occasion, will be compensated with a live telecast of the match?

I won't be holding my breath awaiting a decision on that.

In their "take league to SA country" exercise, the Rams will travel to Whyalla on Friday June 19th. They will do some promotional work and undertake a training session at Bennet Oval.

If this exposure to first grade national rugby league is successful in spreading interest in the game, then changes in Channel Nine's programming are essential if the interest is to be maintained and further developed.

Lot's of great players have come from outside metropolitan Sydney and Brisbane. Rural centres like Innisfail, Woy Woy, Cherbourg, Cessnock, Wagga Wagga, Mt Isa, Casino and many, many others have produced some of the best the game has ever seen.

If the champions of the future can't see the game, how will the game see them?
