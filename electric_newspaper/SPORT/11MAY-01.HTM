Monday May 11, 1998

Spanish Formula 1 GP: Hakkinen hot, Coulthard cool
Barcelona 10 May 1998

In perhaps the least-surprising race result since records began, Finland's Mika Hakkinen completed McLaren's weekend-long dominance of the Circuit de Catalunya by winning the Spanish Grand Prix. He took advantage of his pole position to lead the race from start to finish.

Even Hakkinen's team-mate David Coulthard was unable to challenge him. Coulthard hadn't felt comfortable with his McLaren's set-up before the race. But he still maintained an indomitable lead over the rest of the pack and his second place was never in danger.

Ferrari's ups and downs The real action took place further down the field. A less-than-special start by Ferrari's Michael Schumacher allowed both Giancarlo Fisichella's Benetton and Eddie Irvine in the other Ferrari to slip ahead. Ferrari's solution was to rush Schumacher through his first pit-stop, allowing him to exit inches ahead of Fisichella. Irvine managed to tuck in behind his team-mate on the pit lane exit to push Fisichella down into fifth.

But Ferrari's hopes of clinching both third and fourth places were to be dashed. Minutes later, Fisichella tried to go around the outside of Irvine and misjudged it, and the pair of them span into the gravel. The headache nearly worsened when Schumacher was awarded a 10-second penalty for pit-lane speeding, but he managed to fight back to finish third.

Wurz does better Rookie Alexander Wurz managed to restore some honour to Benetton. He proved the team's dazzling display in qualifying was no accident by finishing fourth.

There was rejoicing in the Stewart garage after they managed not just to get both cars through the full race distance, but to win points as well. Rubens Barichello finished fifth, and Jan Magnussen - whose future with the team is uncertain - came 12th.

More Williams woe Further back, the Williams team's slide into mediocrity continued. Having qualified in 13th place, Heinz-Harald Frentzen suffered a spin on the first lap. He had to pit in for a new nose cone and spent the rest of the race battling through the pack towards an eighth-place finish.

Jacques Villeneuve did better, fending off an assault by Sauber's Johnny Herbert to secure a single point for Williams.

Another loser at the Circuit de Catalunya was Jordan. Having promised so much during testing and qualifying, Damon Hill couldn't disentangle himself from the middle of the pack and eventually retired with a blown engine. Ralf Schumacher was never in the running, eventually finishing 11th.

Arrows miss target The black-liveried Arrows cars exited under a black cloud, both engines blowing simultaneously a third of the way into the race. Pedro Diniz had already had to begin the race from the pit lane after a battery problem left him stranded during the parade lap.

The Prost team could have done better, too. Jarno Trulli managed ninth, but Olivier Panis was lucky to be classified 16th after pulling off the track five laps from the end with a blown engine.
