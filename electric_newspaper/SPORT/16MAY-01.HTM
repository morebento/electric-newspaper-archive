Saturday May 16, 1998

NRL Round 10 Preview: The rich will get richer and the poor will get flattened

Newcastle and North Sydney.

We are to treated to a beauty first up tonight, Bears and Knights at Marathon Stadium (Newcastle). They've both recently been flogged by the Broncos and have each thrashed Manly. If Newcastle win it will be their third straight victory over North Sydney including last year's preliminary ARL final. They've all been close games, and Norths really should have beaten the Knights in their opening round this season. With four of the Novocastrians getting ready for State of Origin One in a week, maybe they'll have other things on their mind. I tip Norths to win this one but it will be very close. Expect a big crowd.

Souths and Melbourne

Gee, I wonder who'll win tonight's game at the Sydney Football Stadium? Souths have more problems than Flash Gorden. Players at the pub instead of training. Players undermining the coach. The coach complaining about the club management. Storm to win by 30 plus.

Canterbury and Auckland

The Bulldogs will be too tough at home on Saturday afternoon. The Warriors don't travel very well and their recent form has been very ordinary. Canterbury by ten.

Parramatta and Adelaide

If the Eels don't take the Rams seriously, AND if the Rams start with intensity, AND if the Rams hang onto the ball, AND if the Rams make the first tackle count all Saturday night at Parkes, they have a chance of an upset. I'd love to tip a Rams win but I can't really see it. Losing by less than 30 will be an improvement for Adelaide. Parramatta by 12.

Cronulla and Brisbane

While Parra are pulverising the Croweaters, the Broncos should be gutting the Sharks at Cronulla. Except for the venue and the position of Cronulla on the table, this is a replay of last year's superleague final. Expect a big crowd to turn out at Shark Park to support the locals and to see (and boo) the superstars from Brisbane. Expect a Broncos win, too.

St George and Illawarra

Meanwhile, up the road from Cronulla at Kogorah Oval, the Dragons will be trying to stay on the top of the table with Brissie, Sydney City, Melbourne and Newcastle. The Steelers will be tough opposition but Saints are on a roll. Dragons to make it seven wins in a row.

Canberra and North Queensland

An interesting match up that might ultimately turn out to be a very significant game important game takes place at Bruce Stadium on Sunday afternoon. With most of the Green Machine either in hospital or on the sidelines, the Raiders are struggling. However, Cowboys seem to be sliding down the table after their great early season. This might be the stage of the competition where Canberra moves into the top ten after a very ordinary start. A case of passing each other travelling in opposite directions? But, Cowboys coach Tim Sheens knows more about the Raiders than anyone. The outcome might be determined by which team carries the least injuries onto the field. I'll stick my neck out and pick North Queensland to get this, but it will be tight.

Penrith and Balmain

The Panthers have so many injured players that the home ground advantage probably cannot compensate. Balmain are due to get back to their overachieving ways shortly, after giving the heavyweight Bulldogs a scare last week. Everything points to a Tigers win.

Western Suburbs and Gold Coast

The Chargers managed to clean up Penrith easily last week whereas the Magpies have been beaten from pillar to post by every team they have met. This will continue at Campbelltown on Sunday I fear.

Sydney City and Manly

These are the only teams that have beaten Brisbane this year. The Sea Eagles really are struggling, however. After last week's total capitulation to Newcastle, warning bells must be ringing at Manly. The Bulldogs are running along very nicely, including a last match win over Brisbane. City should be too strong at the Sydney Football Stadium.
