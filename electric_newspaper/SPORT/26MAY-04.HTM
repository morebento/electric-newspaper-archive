Tuesday May 26, 1998

NRL: Rams chase Magpies in cellar

Even if Adelaide confirm their good form of the last two weeks, in which they narrowly lost to Parramatta and comfortably beat Penrith, by achieving victory over Western Suburbs this weekend, what is there to look forward to in September for South Australian supporters of rugby league?

The team that becomes the NRL minor premiers will finish the season with probably 38-40 premiership points from 19 or 20 wins from the 24 regular games. Ten teams will contest the September playoffs and the tenth placed side will probably finish with 24-26 points from 12 to 13 wins.

For the Rams to make the playoffs, they would need to win at least ten of their last thirteen regular games. Their form indicates this is out of the question, despite the exciting promise that their new outside backs add to their traditional forward strength.

Adelaide face in sequence Western Suburbs (18th), St George (1st), Melbourne (3rd), Illawarra (13th), Gold Coast (20th), Balmain (9th), South Sydney (17th), North Queensland (12th), Auckland (15th), Brisbane (4th), Manly (16th), North Sydney (7th) and Newcastle (1st) to complete the minor rounds.

Assuming Adelaide victories against all of their opponents currently outside the top ten and losses only to the high flying Dragons, Knights and Broncos, the Rams would still have to beat other top ten teams the Storm, Sea Eagles and Bears to win ten out of the next thirteen. Not impossible, but not likely either.

Adelaide can realistically make the top fifteen however. It all starts this week.

South Sydney, Western Suburbs, Adelaide and Gold Coast are involved in a significant series of Round 12 matches this weekend as the inaugural National Rugby League competition reaches the half way mark.

These four teams are at the bottom of the standings with only two wins apiece for four premiership points. On percentages of points scored over conceded, Souths (66%) are 17th, Wests are 18th (61%), Adelaide are 19th (49%) and Gold Coast are 20th (47%).

Fourteenth, 15th and 16th placed Penrith, Auckland and Manly on eight premiership points each are two wins clear of the four-pack in the cellar.

The cellar is going to be rearranged this weekend.

The Gold Coast CHARGERS have a difficult task advancing when they host joint competition leaders the St George DRAGONS at Carrara. Saints have just won eight games straight.

The task facing the Souths RABBITOHS is probably even more difficult. They travel to Marathon Stadium to face the Newcastle KNIGHTS, the other co-leaders of the premiership.

The Wests MAGPIES and the Adelaide RAMS ... well, they play each other.

This is a once in a season chance to pass the wooden spoon to somebody else.

Rams supporters might think things are tough. Traditional Magpie diehards KNOW what tough is.

Wests miserable season so far reads in sequence as follows: they were outclassed by St George (18-36) and beaten by Melbourne (16-26) before they completely annihilated Souths (42-6). They were then brought down to earth when they were squashed by Newcastle (18-34), creamed by Sydney City (22-4) and buried by Souths (10-41). They were competitive in their next game losing to St George (8-12) and then blown away by Melbourne (16-54). They were then tortured by Illawarra (20-42). They beat Gold Coast (24-14) and subsequently lost to Balmain (12-20).

Adelaide's record is only slightly worse: they were beaten by North Queensland (8-18) and then by Manly (6-22) before they outmuscled Canterbury (22-20). They then collapsed completely against Penrith (12-54), lost to Gold Coast (8-16), capitulated to Sydney City (12-50) and were outskilled by Cronulla (12-22). Things got decidedly worse when Canterbury turned them into a rack of lamb (30-4). Rod Reddy was then told his services were no longer required as coach. Under Dean (“Communicator”) Lance the Rams went down by a converted try to Canberra (18-24), were competitive against powerful Parramatta (2-10) and looked the goods against Penrith (35-18).

The Wests-Adelaide confrontation is an important milestone for both teams. Both are struggling but improving. Both believe they can win this one. And they're right!

The game will be played in Sydney.

Of course these days the players and the coaches “only look ahead at the next game, doing it one match at a time”, but ... there's an excellent chance that the winner between the Rams and Magpies will close within one win of the three-pack that is currently two wins above them.

Auckland are travelling this weekend, which they do just as badly as Adelaide. The Warriors face the sixth placed Eels at Parramatta and are yet to win away from Ericsson Stadium. Tough to predict an Auckland win there.

Penrith will host North Queensland, who are in the middle of a slump. The Cowboys clawed out a win against the Raiders in Townsville last weekend to reverse the trend but Penrith win most games at home. Could be close.

The Bulldogs host the Sea Eagles at Belmore. Canterbury were completely disgraced by the Broncos in Brisbane last week whereas Manly put the cleaners through the Sharks. Both teams have been inconsistent. Could easily go either way.

While I'll admit that everyone, myself included, is having a hell of a time predicting winners this season ... and that's a good thing, I'm not complaining ... the chances of Auckland and Penrith and Manly ALL recording wins this round is not high.

I expect Adelaide to beat Wests at Campbelltown and close the gap on the 17th placed Auckland to two points.

They need to. Because the following week the St George juggernaught will arrive at Adelaide Oval. The Dragons, of course, have played more games in Adelaide than any other visiting team. Also, the recently dismissed Rams coaching staff came from the Saints. There's bound to be some feeling there.
