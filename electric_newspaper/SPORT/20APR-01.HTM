Monday April 20, 1998

All power to Port!

In the end, Port Adelaide handled the conditions better. In appalling weather, the Power and the Crows rewarded 41,000 spectators at Football Park yesterday with a display of hard, committed football.

For much of the game there was little in it with the teams level at half time but Port booted 5.2 to 2.11 in the second half to win by nine points.

When James kicked a goal early in the last quarter to put Adelaide seven points up, the Crows looked set for victory. But Port persisted and the ball was rarely out of their forward lines in the last 15 minutes of the match.

The Port centreline was on top throughout with Kingsley gaining 26 possessions and Dickie 24, both wanting the hard ball in the wet conditions much more than their opponents.

Many Adelaide players gave the impression that they were waiting for it to all happen for them, while Port made the most of every oportunity. Other results were a tipsters nightmare with Fremantle finishing 17 points ahead of the Bombers at Waverley Park; Melbourne coming from three goals down two-thirds of the way throught last quarter to beat Carlton at Princes Park; and, Collingwood dominating the Friday night game against Richmond at the MCG.

Sydney and The Western Bulldogs remain undefeated and will clash next Sunday at the SCG. Sydney finished 19 points in front of West Coast to win for the first time at the WACA Ground, while the Western Bulldogs pulled off one of their rare wins at Kardinia Park, beating the Cats by 15 points after leading all day.

For the second week in a row, the Hawks crumbled in the second half. Up 27 points at half time they lost to St Kilda by eight. And North Melbourne defeated Brisbane at the MCG by 17 points.
