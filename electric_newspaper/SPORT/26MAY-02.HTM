Tuesday May 26, 1998

Blues Marooned at Home
by Phil Owens

What a game! What a finish!

In the final minute of the rugby league State of Origin No. 1, Queensland produced a miracle 90 metre two-play try scored by interchange player Tony Carroll to silence the Sydney Football Stadium crowd of 36,000 who were already celebrating a New South Wales victory.

When the full-time siren sounded, Qld were behind 22-23 on the scoreboard with the try conversion kick to come. From about 15 metres to the right of the posts, fullback Darren Lockyer calmly booted the place kick through the slot and over the bar.

Queensland had won the first State of Origin match 24-23 in the three match 1998 series.

What an incredible game!

The Maroons started well from the opening kickoff by the Blues. After both teams had completed two full sets of possession, NSW had been driven back about 40 metres.

Both teams showed skill, class and intensity right from the start. And, typical of origin rugby league, the pace was furious.

There were no errors, fumbles, knock-ons, air passes, missed tackles or penalties for the first six minutes.

The Blues were the first to be forced into double error.

A dropped ball and a penalty put Queensland deep inside New South Wales territory. After several probes at the impregnable Blue defence, Queensland halfback and captain Alan Langer set up the first try.

Alfie ran across the defence to his right and kicked a reverse grubber. The cross-angled kick bounced off a Blue leg, rolled and bounced into the NSW in goal area, where a diving Kevin Walters touched down near the right goalpost. Fullback Lockyer added the extra two points, and after seven minutes it was Queensland 6 New South Wales 0.

It was all Queensland for the next ten minutes.

In the middle of this period came one of three critical plays in the game. The most important play was of course the match-deciding last minute. Another occurred about 15 minutes from the end of the second half, when the injured and exhausted New South Wales defence successfully repelled an unrelenting Queensland onslaught and hit back with a length of the field try.

But in the 11th minute of the first half, silly play by Maroons left winger Mat Sing cost Queensland a certain six points.

With Steve Renouf carrying the ball in the clear, ahead of the defence and 25 metres from the crossbar, Sing ran to his right and collided with pursuing NSW centre Terry Hill, taking him out of the play.

It was totally unnecessary. No one in the world was going to run down Renouf.

Acting on the report of a touch judge, referee Halligan, who did an excellent job overall, correctly declared "no try" and awarded NSW a relieving penalty.

This lucky let-off inspired the Blue forwards into action. They proceeded to completely dominate the rest of the first half. The inexperience and lesser strength of the Qld forwards was exploited leading to three unanswered tries to New South Wales before half-time.

In this period, the NSW forwards smashed their opponents in attack and defence.

The first to capitalise on their forward domination were NSW captain and five-eighth, Laurie Daley and fullback Tim Brasher.

Brasher ran at an emerging a gap just wide of the ruck and Daley, dragging the defence to his left by running left threw a beautifully timed reverse pass and Brasher was through. The former, and possibly future, Australian fullback then outpaced the cover defence to score an excellent try.

Blues halfback Andrew Johns failed with the relatively straightforward conversion kick, just shading the uprights.

This became a pattern and, in the end, turned out to be New South Wales' weakness.

Qld 6 NSW 4

With some space and some time with the ball in his hands, courtesy of the dominance of Paul Harragon, Dean Pay, Rod Howe and Nick Kosef near the ruck and at the advantage line, Johns got his general kicking game in order and started doing what everyone predicted.

He bombed the hell out of the Maroon's wingers and fullback. The Blues three-quarters supported him well with great chases.

And, eventually, as everyone predicted, the Maroons backs erred.

The first came from Lockyer, but the chase gave him no room. He leapt into the air for the ball but hit two NSW players doing the same thing and propelled the ball forward, about 2 metres from his own line. Daley showed amazing ball juggling skills and agility to scoop up and secure the ball and bounce over for a four pointer.

Johns narrowly failed to kick the conversion from out wide.

NSW 8 Qld 6

A few minutes later, Queensland right winger Wendell Sailor fumbled a high ball with veteran NSW centre Andrew Ettinghausen right in his face.

From the ten metre scrum, Daley ran, slowed, stood the defence up and hit the speeding Brasher right on the chest with an excellent pass. The NSW fullback, making the extra man in attack, drew the last of the defence and gave right winger Rod Wishart a clear run to the line.

Johns again very narrowly failed to convert from out wide.

NSW 12 Qld 6

A few minutes before half time, Andrew Johns finally scored points from a kick, scoring 1 point from a field goal from about 35 metres out.

At half time, NSW led Qld by 13-6.

The Blues performance in the last 25 minutes of the first half was very impressive, but there were some danger signs.

Daley, who was having an absolute blinder, limped off a few minutes early with what seemed to be a calf problem.

Harragon, who had been very effective in attack and defence, and had been spelled to the interchange bench a couple of times, also went off early, reportedly for attention to his persistent back injury.

Queensland coach Wayne Bennet may have recognised this, because the Queensland forwards started on fire in the second half, led by debutant Martin Lang from the interchange bench. Bartrim and Larson also tore out some valuable yards at the ruck.

Within 15 minutes, Queensland scored two converted tries from grubber kicks behind the New South Wales defensive line inside their ten metre line. These kicks were both from the little master himself, Alfie. Langer scored one of these and Steve Price scored the other.

Suddenly the Blues were staring at up at a 13-18 scoreline.

It was now or never.

In a desperate series of "keep the ball alive" passes, NSW finally ran the Maroon defence silly and lock Brad Fittler speared through from 15 metres out to touch down.

Johns again very narrowly failed to convert.

Qld 18 NSW 17

With less than 15 minutes remaining Queensland desperately tried to put NSW away. They threw everything at the injured and tiring Blue defence. Renouf and Smith both came within less than half a metre of scoring tries.

Then came the dramatic and exciting last eight minutes.

Wishart comfortably fielded a Kevin Walters kick a couple of metres out from his line, stepped inside a defender, and raced into the clear.

He was chased along the sideline by Mart Lang (a prop forward no less!) until he was eventually overhauled by Mat Sing and tackled 35 metres out from the Queensland line. Good hands saw the ball cleared to Brasher, then Ettinghausen and onto Menzies who scored wide on the left.

Johns again very narrowly failed to convert.

But NSW led Qld 23-18 with just six minutes left.

Most thought "that's it, game NSW".

But not Queensland.

Inside the last minute, ten metres from their own line on the second tackle, Lockyer surprised everyone except Ben Ikin by booting the ball 50 metres down field along his right touchline.

Ikin recovered the ball, was immediately trapped but somehow got a pass away. I lost count of the number of sets of hands the ball went through in the next play. Inside passes, outside passes, off loads in tackles _ no, it couldn't possible be, could it?

And then with 45 seconds left Tony Carroll crossed over, ran closer to the posts and scored Queensland's fourth try.

NSW 23 Qld 22

Siren.

Lockyer successfully converted. He didn't miss one kick at goal all night!

Qld 24 NSW 23

What a great game!

Queensland 24 (tries to Walters, Price, Langer and Carroll, four goals to Lockyer) defeated New South Wales 23 (tries to Wishart, Brasher, Daley, Fittler and Menzies; one field goal to Andrew Johns)
