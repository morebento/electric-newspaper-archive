Friday April 17, 1998

National Rugby League, Round 6 Predictions
By Phil Owens

This weekend's fixtures will go some way towards deciding the composition of Australia's first truly representative international rugby league team in two years. So we can expect some of the best individual and team performances of the season as players vie for spots in the side to meet the strong Kiwi lineup on April 24.

There are some potentially classic confrontations that should make this the most interesting round so far.

FRIDAY

Manly v Brisbane at Brookvale Oval (Sydney)

The red hot Brisbane Broncos should repeat the pasting they gave the Manly Sea Eagles in the opening round of the competition six weeks back.

However, the desperate Eagles may have more incentive. If Manly don't start winning soon they might as well start planning for the playoffs in 1999.

A win over the undefeated Broncos would provide the turning point for the underachieving Manly team. A loss could see them relegated to the cellar.

On the other hand, the Brisbane players know that good performances in front of Bob Fulton, who coaches both Manly and Australia, will help their chances of getting a green and gold jersey on Sunday when the Australian test team will be announced.

Broncos in a canter.

Sydney City v Adelaide at Sydney Football Stadium (Sydney)

Sadly, the question here isn't who will win but rather by how much.

Rod Reddy has made seven changes to the Rams selection that shot themselves in the feet against the Chargers last week.

Roosters easily.

SATURDAY

Canberra v Illawarra at Bruce Stadium (Canberra)

With their eyes on the representative opportunities the Raiders appear to be finally running into some form. Last outing they continued Manly's nightmare with a 26-20 win at Bruce Stadium. Manly led 20-2 at one stage.

The Steeler's succumbed to St George in another close one (19-12).

A win for Canberra would put them into the top ten and keep Illawarra in contention for the fiercely sought 20th spot.

Raider's too classy at home.

Cronulla v Balmain at Shark Park (Sydney)

This promises to be the most exciting and entertaining match of the season so far.

The Sharks seem to be struggling (two wins and three losses) but so far they have been playing the better teams in the competition, except for their 6-2 win over Souths in round 4.

The Tigers have been a breath of fresh air this year, with good old fashioned guts and spirit and never-say-die determination on display.

Both teams can counter punch, and like to play fast open football.

The incentive for the Sharkie's will be to make ground on Balmain (4 wins, 1 loss) who are ahead of them on the table.

Too close to call.

North Queensland v Newcastle at Cowboys Stadium (Townsville)

The Cowboys came down with an almighty crash last week when they were absolutely outclassed by Brisbane. They get no respite this week when they take on the other undefeated team, Newcastle. Out of the frying pan and into the fire!

This match will provide a good indicator of where North Queensland are likely to finish at the end of the year. They certainly have the experience, but do they have the character? This might turn out to be the most important game the Cowboys play in the regular season.

Newcastle took good care of Cronulla (24-10) last week, but the suspension of Paul Harragon has weakened the Knights. His absence plus the home ground advantage tip the scales the Cowboys way, but probably not by enough.

Knights to win a very tough encounter.

SUNDAY

North Sydney v Auckland at North Sydney Oval (Sydney)

This is a very interesting match. The Auckland Warriors provide the bulk of the New Zealand test team, and this is their last hit out before they take on Australia next week. Their coach, Endacott, is also the Kiwi test coach. Both Auckland coach and team therefore have a lot at stake here.

The North Sydney Bears have shown consistently good form over 5 rounds of competition, despite their 7th position on the premiership ladder. Their two losses were close run affairs against the undefeated Newcastle Knights and the high flying Melbourne Storm. They handed out a 42-8 hiding to the Rabbitohs last weekend.

The Warriors have been patchy, but last week they ended Melbourne's undefeated run.

The strong and disciplined Norths should win at home in a close one.

Canterbury v Gold Coast at Belmore Sports Ground (Sydney)

Both teams have a 1 win 4 loss record and will be fighting to get out of the cellar.

The Chargers are coming off their first win which was against wooden spoon aspirants Adelaide. Last weekend the Bulldogs were beaten four tries to two by Parramatta.

This game will be lost by one team, rather than won by their opponents.

Chargers to win because they will stick to their game plan and make fewer mistakes, something that Canterbury can't seem to manage.

Penrith v St George at Penrith Football Stadium (Sydney)

This is a match-up between two teams in the top ten, the Panthers currently trailing the Dragons by one win.

Both teams appear to be running hot and cold. Penrith followed up their thrashing of the Rams with a terrible display against Balmain. St George are coming off a win against the Steelers.

Saints by a whisker.

South Sydney v Western Suburbs at Sydney Football Stadium (Sydney)

Another fight to the bottom, with one team guaranteed to stay there unless the match is drawn.

The Rabbitohs have shown that they can play, and reports indicate that Julian O'Neill has put in some excellent displays at five eighth. With Tim Brasher and Chris Caruana behind and outside him, they'll cause problems if Souths get enough ball.

The Magpies, however, get a great chance for their second win of the season, and their irrepressible coach Tommy Raudonikis and inspirational captain, the evergreen Paul Langmack, should be capable of lifting them.

Wests to win this one by sheer desperation, determination and fear of their coach.

Parramatta v Melbourne at Parramatta Stadium (Sydney)

Plenty of incentive in this beauty between the fourth placed Eeels and the fifth placed Storm, who have both won four and lost one.

Both teams contain top flight backs and clever experienced forwards.

I'll go for Storm in this "moment-of-truth" game for the underachieving Eeels, but I won't be surprised if it goes the other way. I will be surprised if it's not a great contest.
