Sunday May 10, 1998

National Rugby League: Little Mac on, ref off, as crowd cheers Rams
By Phil Owens

The Adelaide Rams went down 24-18 to the Canberra Raiders in their round nine NRL clash at Adelaide Oval on Friday night. Adelaide reversed a lacklustre discordant performance that put them behind 18-0 after 55 minutes to come storming home and almost snatch the match.

Unfortunately for the Rams, their dominance for the last 25 minutes of the game was punctured by a piece of individual brilliance from the Raiders half-back, "Little Mac" Andy McFadden.

Adelaide's attack was hamstrung by what appeared to me to be referee Moghseen Jadwat's complete inattention to the 10 metre rule at the play-the-ball. This allowed the Raiders defence to stand up offside for most of the game.

Ten minutes from full time, the diminutive McFadden recovered the ball from a scrum that he fed in centrefield. He immediately accelerated between the close defence and showed pace and swerve to stand up the Rams debutant full back, Graham Appo, race 40 metres and score under the posts.

David Furner added the extras comfortably, to push Canberra to a 22-14 cushion.

McFadden's solo effort was the decisive play of the game.

Earlier, two tries to Raiders fullback, McFadden look-alike, Mark McLinden, and another to big David Divoco, appeared to have the local leaguers dead and buried at 18-0 with three-quarters of the game gone.

To their credit and to the appreciation of a crowd of around 8,000 the Rams came back very strongly in the last quarter and almost snatched the game from the visitors.

The Rams finished with a spirit and intensity that was absent during the first quarter of each half. Adelaide were unlucky not to manage a draw.

The Adelaide team produced some scintillating play variations that had the crowd on their feet several times in the last 20 minutes.

A few minutes from full time, the Rams produced a classic team try.

After stretching the Raiders defence out wide on the left, long accurate passes from Walters and Golthorpe saw the ball spread wide to the right where Graham Appo chimed in from full back. His short pass to Luke Williamson allowed the Adelaide centre to crash over the try line near the right touchline.

This was the best try seen at Adelaide Oval this season.

Trailing 24-18 with three minutes to play, the Rams threatened to score but the Raiders managed to hang on.

Mal Meninga and his injury-depleted team were fortunate to get away with the two competition points.

Both teams scored four tries each.

Great cover defence from Adelaide winger Jensen right on the corner post also prevented a fifth Raiders major score.

In the end, the difference on the scoreboard was the goal kicking of David Furner for Canberra. He was presented with four simple conversions of Raiders tries close to the uprights.

All the Rams tries were scored out wide. Luke Williamson narrowly failed with two difficult kicks. Appo was successful once from two opportunities in similar positions.

For Canberra, Brett Hetherington was impressive hitting the ball up from deep inside his own territory. Several times he shredded Adelaide's defence close to the ruck.

Furner also had a great game for the Raiders, producing two excellent 40 metre busts by bashing through the first tackle.

Aside from two moments of woeful defensive errors, the Rams tackled well.

Both McLinden's tries came from Canberra's ability to stand up the Rams sliding defence out wide through well timed inside passes on simple scissor plays.

Tony Iro sparked the Rams fight back by finishing off their first try scoring movement with a well timed run and dummy pass.

Iro was involved in almost everything in the last 20 minutes.

Bruce Mamando celebrated his return from injury almost a year ago with a blockbusting four pointer, crunching through tackles and carrying two Raiders defenders across their tryline.

Adelaide's fightback won them the respect of the crowd. The defeated Rams were given a standing ovation for their gutsy comeback.
