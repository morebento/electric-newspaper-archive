Wednesday June 3, 1998

National Rugby League: Who said they don't have Fritz in Sydney?
by Phil Owens

A great start and a late rally from the Adelaide Rams rugby league team were not enough to defeat the Western Suburbs Magpies at Campbelltown on Sunday. Wests climbed out of the cellar winning 36-24 in a thirteen try minimal tackle version of the game, and thus left Souths, Gold Coast and Adelaide at the bottom of the standings.

Rams five-eighth Craig Bowen scored a try after only 6 minutes which centre Graham Appo converted to get Adelaide away to a 6-0 start. Subsequently, poor handling by the visitors provided Wests with twice as much ball as Adelaide in the first half.

Big Darren Fritz, recent escapee from North Sydney and former star at Illawarra, tore the Adelaide forwards apart. Magpie tries in the 10th, 12th, 20th and 27th minutes saw them run out to a 20-6 lead.

Adelaide struck back with a try to Mark Corvo in the 38th minute, but Wests answered immediately to have the game in hand 24-10 at half-time.

The Rams improved their work rate in the second half to outscore the Magpies 14-12 for the section, but the damage had already been done.

For Adelaide, experienced utility back Chris Quinn returned from injury and was one of Adelaide's better players. Lock Dean Schifilliti, who has been performing consistently well for the Rams this year, unfortunately injured his shoulder.

It was Darren Fritz's day. Unfortunately it was spoiled when the referee sent Fritz from the field one minute from the end, when he “brushed off” Adelaide half-back Noel Goldthorpe. Both Fritz and Goldthorpe may have to answer the NRL judiciary over the incident in which the Magpie forward was attempting to play the ball.

The Rams have now failed to beat both of the bottom teams they have faced, Gold Coast and Wests.

This Saturday they host the St George Dragons.

Saints have just won nine games straight and currently lead the NRL with last year's ARL premiers, Newcastle.

On Tuesday Adelaide named the following training squad of 19 from which the 17 to meet St George at Adelaide Oval at 7.30 pm on Saturday June 6th will be selected.

The main changes to the proposed starting 13 are the inclusion of Quinn at five-eighth and Peek at lock. Peek comes in for the injured Schifilliti.

Ram's coach Dean Lance must have thought long and hard about where to include Quinn. So, Bowen has been pushed to the bench. Bird has held his spot at fullback. Appo and Kidwell have swapped centre for wing. This is a very flexible backline.

Good enough to threaten St George?
