Monday June 29, 1998

New Zealand's Simon Crafar briefly leads Mick Doohan in an exciting Assen GP.
Record-breaking Dutch Grand Prix

Record-breaking in more ways that one! There was a record crowd, more than 140,000 at the legendary Assen circut - one of the oldest on the GP calendar. This was particularly impressive considering The Netherlands is still in World Cup fever. Secondly, it way Grand Prix's youngest ever winner - Marco Melandri, who at 15 years old beat championship leader Kazuto Sakata in the 125's.

But the heaviest action was, of course, in the 500's, where New Zealander Simon Crafar won his his first ever podium in a Grand Prix.

It was without doubt one of the most exciting 500 races this season.

Mick Doohan (Repsol Honda) won the Dutch Grand Prix for the fifth year in a row holding off a strong challenge from Max Biaggi (Marlboro Kanemoto Honda) and Simon Crafar (Red Bull Yamaha). Alex Crivillé (Repsol Honda), who led the points table coming into Holland, faded to a distant sixth place, meaning that Biaggi, who opened the season with a win in Japan, goes back into first place in the championship, but by only three points (118-115) over Doohan.

Beating Doohan at Assen would have been a tall order since the Australian has won for four straight years prior to his win on Saturday, but Biaggi kept the pressure on over the final laps hoping to force Doohan into a mistake or to get an opportunity to slip past on the brakes for the final chicane. Doohan neither broke under the pressure nor did he leave an open door to the Italian.

Simon Crafar on his Dunlop-shod Yamaha (all the other front-runners were on Michelin), rode a brilliant race and actually led briefly when Doohan dug one of this knee sliders into the grass and was, he said, "nearly pulled off the bike." Doohan was back past before the finish line and, in fact, lead every one of the twenty laps.

Fourth was Brazilian Alex Barros (Gresini Honda), who always goes very well at Assen. He and Norick Abe (Yamaha Team Rainey) were in the five man battle for the early lead. Abe‚s machine stopped only four laps from the end with mechanical problems when the Japanese rider was holding fifth place. Barros was eventually fourth.

Carlos Checa (Movistar Honda Pons) the winner two weeks ago in Madrid, came home in a lonely fourth place, nine seconds back of Barros and eight seconds ahead of Crivillé.

Luca Cadalora, making his first ride on the factory Suzuki, lost the front and went down when chasing Crivillé, but Suzuki‚s other rider, Nobustsu Aoki, in spite of feeling the effects of a big crash in Friday‚s final qualifying session, came home seventh in front of Repsol Honda‚s Tadayuki Okada, making his return after breaking his wrist in a practice crash for the Spanish Grand Prix.

Ralf Waldmann (Marlboro Team Roberts) was a non starter on his Modenas after breaking his wrist in the final qualifying session on Friday while John Kocinski (Movistar Honda Pons), already injured after crashing in the Grand Prix of the Community of Madrid, crashed again in the rain on Friday and withdrew from Assen and also from next week‚s British Grand Prix in order to give his badly cut finger (the little finger of his left hand) time to heal.

With only twelve points separating Biaggi in the lead from Checa, who dropped back to fourth, the points spread over the first four places is the narrowest that has been at any times after seven rounds. But, in spite of having failed to score in two rounds, Doohan has now made up 23 of the 25 points that Biaggi took from him at Suzuka.

The victory by Doohan allowed him to make his 88th podium appearance, equaling the FIM record of 88 for the 500 class held by Giacomo Agostini.

250cc - 1st 250 win for Rossi

Valentino Rossi won his first 250cc Grand Prix today as he dominated the race, taking the lead on lap 6 and winning by 19 seconds at the flag. However it wasn't a good day for his Aprilia teammates as both Tesuya Harada and Loris Capirossi retired on lap 9 with mechnical failure.

Olivier Jacque and Jeremy McWilliams led the pack away at the start - though McWilliams was forced to pit for a stop/go penalty having jumped into the third row before the green light. Within half a lap the power of the Aprilias made itself felt as Capirossi and Rossi both burst to the front and proceeded to clear off.

By lap 3, Harada had also recovered from a slow start and moved into 3rd place, leaving the Hondas of Perugini and Jacque to fight over 4th - though this was settled two laps later as Perugini rammed the back of an early-braking Jacque, taking them both out at turn 3.

On lap 6 Rossi went ahead of Capirossi and stepped the pace up - though by that time they were well ahead of the chasing pack of Ukawa, Aoki, Tsujimura and D'Antin. Then came his teammates' retirement, and it was left to Rossi to cruise around 22 seconds ahead of the rest of the field.

The battle for second place stepped into high gear for the final two laps, as six riders - D'Antin, Ukawa, Aoki, Tsujimura, Fuchs and Porto all sensed they were in with a chance. With one lap to go Fuchs made the decisive move into 2nd, with Aoki in 3rd.

125cc - Youngest ever GP winner - Marco Melandri

15-year old Italian Marco Melandri became the youngest ever GP winner today as he beat championship leader Kazuto Sakata to the line after a last-lap overtaking move at the final chicane.

After setting fastest time by a long way in practice, Sakata looked like the runaway winner as he took the lead from Mirko Giansanti on the first lap and opened up an immediate lead. However Melandri managed to get a break on the following pack of Tokudome, Cecchinello, Giansanti, Scalvini, Locatelli, and Petit and within 2 laps had caught Sakata and shadowed him for the rest of the race.

With half a lap to go, Milandri went into the lead, but Sakata fought back two turns later. Then came the youngster's decisive move, as he held the outside line on the bend before, giving him the run into the chicane and the drag to the line by just a few thousandths of a second. In a surprise third, Tomomi Manako made an amazing charge from a distant 8th place to beat the chasing group of 5 at the flag.

Doohan wins, trails Biaggi by three points / First 250 Win for Valentino Rossi / Marco Melandri is Youngest Ever Grand Prix Winner at 15 6/27/98

First 250 Win for Valentino Rossi

At the same track where his father Graziano won the 1979 Dutch TT on a Morbidelli 250, nineteen year old Valentino Rossi (Nastro Azzuro Aprilia), won his first ever 250 race on the only surviving works Aprilia after his team mates Tetsuya Harada and Loris Capirossi retired with mechanical problems at half distance when trailing Rossi. Leading Honda riders Olivier Jacque and Stefano Perugini went down only four laps into the race when Perugini hit Jacque on braking. Jacque reinsured his already broken foot. The excitement of the final laps came from the battle for second, third and fourth between Jurgen Fuchs (Docshop Aprilia), Haruchika Aoki (F.C.C. TSS Honda), Luis D‚Antin (Antena 3 Yamaha) and Tohru Ukawa (Benetton Honda) with the four finishing in the above order. Fuchs starting from the first row, dropped back to last place when the engine of his Aprilia loaded up and refused to rev coming out of the first corner. The bike finally chimed back in and the German thrilled the Assen crowd with his charge through the field. Argentina‚s Sebas Porto (PR2 Mitsubishi Elaion Aprilia had his best ever result, finishing sixth in front of Takeshi Tsujimura and Jason Vincent (Padgetts HRC Shop Honda). Britain‚s Jeremy McWilliams jumped the start from the fourth row and was called in for a stop and go penalty, but still managed to come back up to 12th place.

Marco Melandri is Youngest Ever Grand Prix Winner at 15 Fifteen-year-old Italian Marco Melandri (Benetton Matteoni Honda) stalked thirty-one- year-old former World Champion Kazuto Sakata (UGT 3000 Aprilia) of Japan and made his move on the last lap to become the youngest rider ever to win a Grand Prix. (The previous youngest ever winner, Ivan Goi, who was sixteen when he won the Austrian GP of 1996, finished back in 14th place.)

Tomomi Manako (UGT 3000 Honda), who is now joint second in the run for the title and a distant 34 points back of Sakata, came through from a dismal start to take third place, setting a new Assen 125 race lap records in the process.
