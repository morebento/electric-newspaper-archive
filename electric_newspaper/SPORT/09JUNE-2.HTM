Tuesday June 9, 1998

World superbike championship: Slight win, in Chili weather

After his third place in race one, Italian Pier Francsesco Chili revelled in the wet conditions to take the second race win at the Nurburgring on Sunday. The win puts Chili into second place in the championship behind fellow Ducati rider Troy Corser, who suffered a broken gear lever in race one dropping him to seventh, followed by a third place in race two.

New Zealander Aaron Slight, who was looking to dominate the meeting after qualifying and a race one win, crashed out in the second leg but remounted to finish a fighting fourth.

Second in both races was Honda's Colin Edwards.

Britain's Carl Fogarty had a miserable time and could only struggle around to two 13th places.

Race One was a fairly subdued affair. Kawasaki's Akira Yanagawa led the pack away from the line, followed by Edwards, Corser, and Haga. Edwards soon took the lead, while fast qualifier Lavilla crashed out on the first lap.

As Edwards cleared off into a substantial lead, Slight was working his way up through the pack, battling with Haga, Chili and Corser. By mid-race Slight had pulled ahead of Yanagawa for second place, while Corser was dropping back with a faulty gearchange - first into the clutches of Chili, and finally on the last corner to Suzuki's Peter Goddard who had fought his way up to the slowing fellow countryman.

Early championship sensation, Yamaha's Noriyuki Haga had an eventful race - initially fighting for third with Slight and Chili, he ran off the track on lap 9 but rejoined and managed to get past Corser and into 5th place by the end.

Up front, on a rapidly drying track, Slight set fastest lap after fastest lap and closed down his Texan teammate. With 5 laps to go he moved into the lead and drew away for an easy win.

In race two, Chili's bike setup was just right and he dominated
preceedings. Edwards
led into the first corner, while Suzuki's James Whitham was forced onto the
grass.
Haga was briefly in second, with Slight in third. On the second lap Chili
got past Slight
and up into second, and then into the lead past Edwards. 


After that the race was largely uneventful with only brief drama as Aaron
Slight
crashed out whilst in second, only to remount and take fourth overall. The
atrocious
weather took control and the riders splashed around in torrents and floods
of standing
water.


Said Chili: "It is good to come away with two podiums in such terrible
conditions, but
maybe if I had the same set-up in the first race as I had in the second, it
may have
been two firsts! In the first race I tried to push hard, but didn't get
enough grip and I
couldn't control the bike. If I had pushed harder, I would've crashed for
sure. In the
second race we softened the suspension and dropped the rear a little and
that made a lot of difference. The bike wasn't perfect, but it was much
better and I was able to
ride as I wanted. I enjoyed the race and I know that, in the rain, if my
Ducati has a
good set-up, I can win races."


Championship leader Corser was philosophical after the meeting. "The first
race
would've been a lot better if the gearlever hadn't broken off after about
five or six laps.
I decided to try and keep going and I had to change gear by pushing down on
and
lifting up the stalk with the toe of my boot. It meant that I had to think
about braking
a lot before I had to brake. So, I reckon seventh was OK."


"In the second race, we softened up the bike and I felt that it was an
improvement for
sure. Not 100%, but better. Frankie got away and there was no chance of
catching
him, so I diced with Colin. The bike misfired a few times out of corners
and I was a
bit worried that I might not make the finish. It was a relief when I saw
the chequered
flag, but then the bike cut out and stopped completely before I could
complete the
cooling down lap!"
