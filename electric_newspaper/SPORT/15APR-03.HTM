Wednesday April 15, 1998

And then there were two: NRL Round Up
By Phil Owens

After five rounds of the National Rugby League competition, only two sides remain undefeated, Brisbane and Newcastle. These teams also won the 1997 grand finals of the now defunct Superleague and Australian Rugby League competions.

The Broncos and the Knights will meet on only one occasion in the regular home-and-away series, in round 8 next month at Marathon Stadium, Newcastle. Promises to be a beauty.

We can expect the Australian side to meet New Zealand in the Anzac Test to be dominated by Broncos and Knights.

The other two early season pace setters stumbled to their first defeats of the competition. The Melbourne Storm lost 12-16 to the Auckland Warriors in Auckland. The Brisbane Broncos gave the North Queensland Cowboys an absolute hiding, 58-4, at ANZ Stadium (Brisbane).

Melbourne (102 points scored and 78 points conceded) and North Queensland (78 and 95) are now 5th and 6th behind Balmain (3rd, 114/56) and Parammatta (4th, 112/68) who have also won four games and lost one.

NO NIKAU IN NEW ZEALAND TEAM FOR ANZAC TEST

New Zealand rugby league national coach and sole selector Frank Endacott announced the following squad to meet Australia on Thursday APRIL 24.

Matthew Ridge (c), Sean Hoppe, Ruben Wiki, Richard Blackmore, Richard Barnett, Gene Namu, Stacy Jones, Logan Swann, Steve Kearney, Jarrod McCracken, Quentin Pongia, Syd Eru, John Lomax, RESERVES Robbie Paul, Kevin Iro, Tony Iro, Joe Vagana

If the Kiwi's get some ball and can control it, their backs can certainly put any team under pressure with their skill and speed. New Zealand also look very strong up front.

STUART SERIOUSLY ILL

Raiders' halfback, Ricky Stuart, who has been suffering from migraines for a couple of weeks, was yesterday flown from Canberra to Sydney in a lear jet owned by Kerry Packer for examination by the specialist staff at St Vincent's Private Hospital. The veteran national and state-of-origin star suffered "turns" on Friday and Tuesday. He was unable to walk to the aircraft.

Stuart is undergoing neurological tests at St Vincent's aimed at determining the cause of his deteriorating condition.

MANLY FACING ROCK BOTTOM

After 5 rounds of the inaugural National Rugby League competion, 17th place on the premiership table is occupied by the Manly Sea Eagles, with one win, four losses, 62 points scored and 106 points conceded. There are currently six other teams at the bottom of the table with one win so far: Canterbury, Wests, Illawarra, Gold Coast, Adelaide and South Sydney.

Are Manly the same team that played in the last four Australian Rugby League grand finals?

Obviously not.

The departure of front row forwards David Gillespie and Mark Carrol has reduced Manly's ability to go forward at the ruck and create space and time for their creative halves, Toovey, Lyons and Field. The absence of hard working Des Hasler and Owen Cunningham might also be showing.

Can things get better for the Sea Eagles?

Not likely this weekend, because they meet the undefeated Brisbane at Brookvale (Sydney). Brisbane are scoring an average of better than 33 points per match and conceding less than 12 points per game. Further on the debit side, Manly's coach, Bob Fulton, will be distracted from preparing the Sea Eagles for their clash with the Broncos because he is also coach of the Australian team. Brisbane's coach, Wayne Bennet, has no such distractions. FootyTAB gives Manly 11.5 points start.

Depending on the results of the games between Adelaide (19th) and Sydney City (8th), between South Sydney (20th) and Wests (15th), and between Gold Coast (18th) and Canterbury (14th), Manly could be rock bottom of the table on Monday.

If the Rabbitohs beat Wests and Brisbane beat Manly, the Sea Eagles will drop to 18th spot. If the Chargers manage a win against the Bulldogs, which on current form is not unlikely, Manly will drop to 19th. If the Rams score 7 more points against Sydney City than Manly do against Brisbane, and if the Rams concede 25 less points in their clash than Manly do in theirs, the Eagles will have landed - in the cellar! A Broncos flogging or a Rams victory could do it to Manly.
