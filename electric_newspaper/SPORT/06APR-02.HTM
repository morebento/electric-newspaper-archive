Monday April 6, 1998

Storming along with the Knights, Cowboys and Broncos
by Phillip Owens

Melbourne STORM continued their remarkable rugby league start with a 24-16 defeat of consistent semifinalists the North Sydney BEARS before a full house at Olympic Park (Melbourne) on Friday night.

In a great result for the first rugby league team based in Melbourne to compete in a national series, Melbourne won their first home game in front of 21,522. That was the largest NRL gate of round four, surpassing the 17,404 at Marathon Stadium (Newcastle) on Sunday who watched the KNIGHTS, last year's ARL premiers, defeat the injury depleted Western Suburbs (Sydney) MAGPIES 34-18.

The Melbourne newcomers have now won four straight. They share the competition lead with similarly undefeated Newcastle, Brisbane BRONCOS and Townsville-based North Queensland COWBOYS. Alan Langer returned to his best form as Brisbane swamped the Gold Coast CHARGERS 40-18 at Carrara (crowd 13,126). North Queensland dug deep into their reserves to edge out the Canberra RAIDERS 14-12 in Townsville.

Melbourne take on the Chargers this weekend and must fancy their chances of making it five straight. Newcastle meet Cronulla in what shapes as a desperate game for the Sharks.

At least one team will drop out of the four way share of the lead when the Cowboys get their chance at the high flying Broncos. This top of the table clash is the pick of round five.

There are currently no Sydney teams in the top four!

The best placed Syndeysiders are the unheralded Balmain TIGERS and the underachieving Parammata EELS, who have each lost one match. Balmain surprised all the experts with their win over the Manly SEA EAGLES in round three. The TIGERS 24-10 win over the Canterbury BULLDOGS extended their recent good form. Parammatta flogged Manly 34-10 in front of 14,366 at Brookvale.

Balmain next meet Penrith in a game that looks fraught with danger for the patchy Panthers. The unexplicably bumbling Bulldogs are at risk of going the same way against the upstarts from Balmain.

Penrith, Sydney City, North Sydney and St George make up the top ten with two wins and two losses each. Cronulla have also won two and lost two. Another eight teams have managed one win in the first four fixtures, including glamour sides Canberra and Manly, who are opposed in round five.

Newcastle (122), Penrith (116) and Brisbane (108) have scored the most points and Adelaide (114), Gold Coast (112) and Western Suburbs (102) have conceded the most.

Adelaide host Gold Coast this weekend in an attempt to get clear of the bottom of the table. The CHARGERS will be looking for their first win.
