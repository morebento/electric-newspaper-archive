Thursday March 19, 1998

Talonted Sea Eagles better duck

The weekend took on a more promising aspect for despondent fans of the Adelaide Rams rugby league team over the last couple of days.

The chances of the Rams upsetting the highly favoured Manly Sea Eagles during their second round National Rugby League fixture at Brookvale Oval (Sydney) increased slightly as a consequence of the fall out from last Friday night's game between Manly and the Brisbane Broncos.

Manly lost the game with Brisbane, lost the argument with the referee, lost the fight with the judiciary and lost the services of their blockbusting winger, Hopoate.

The New Zealand international was suspended for two weeks as a result of some tackles he effected against Brisbane. Manly coach Bob Fulton seems to have declared referee Harrigan to be public enemy number one, and has suggested the whistle blower "get a hair cut". He also mentioned legal action against the referee.

One wonders whether Manly have indeed "lost the plot", as Harrigan indicated was the case with Fulton.

Fulton, who is also the current Australian test coach, will no doubt get his charges to focus on the task before them this weekend, but it does appear that there have been plenty of distractions this week for him and for the Sea Eagles captain, Geoff Toovey. Toovey escaped from the judiciary last night without a suspension.

Manly have such depth of talent, experience and speed, that few would tip the Rams this weekend. However, if the Adelaide side can show its customary defence, it might be possible to frustrate Manly into error.

The Rams showed flashes of brilliance in attack against North Queensland, and they appear capable of surprising Manly if their ability to finish improves. Adelaide cannot afford to squander possession because Manly's clever inside backs will set their strong and mobile backrow loose.

Adelaide's best chances appear to depend on better defence and ball control than they showed last week. But Manly must not underestimate the Rams. Complacency and distractions appear to be Manly's biggest concerns.

While the Rams players and coaching staff ponder a means of ruffling the Eagles feathers, the hair must be standing up on the backs of the necks of their management and fans. They received a shock when the lights came down in a hurry yesterday. The thought of that happening during one of the wind squalls last Friday night must have sent chills down many spines. If this problem is not immediately resolved permanently, the Adelaide Rams management appear to have no alternative but to look for a "safe" venue to play under lights on Friday nights. I know I'll be staying very well clear of the telescopic light towers at future matches. If the Adelaide Oval owners cannot guarantee my safety at this facility, I can guarantee that my next of kin will direct our insurance company to them before my funeral service is held.
