Monday June 22, 1998

World Superbike Championship: Round 6 - Misano, San Marino. Slight does the Double as Frankie suffers

Castrol Honda's Aaron Slight did the double today, comfortably winning both races in the San Marino round of the WSB Championship. The wins put Slight within 6 points of championship leader Troy Corser, who rode safely for two second places, but could not match the New Zealander on the day.

Carl Fogarty did his championship chances no harm with a 4th and 3rd, while the day was a disaster for Yamaha sensation Noriyuki Haga, Ducati's local hero Pier Francesco Chili, and Suzuki's Peter Goddard, who all failed to score a point with two DNFs each.

Race One

Slight dominated race one, winning easily from championship leader Troy Corser, and Colin Edwards. Despite everyone complaining about the slippery track surface, Slight pulled ahead on lap 8, got his head down and wasn't troubled throughout the race.

From the outset it hasn't been Pier Francesco Chili's weekend, despite being the crowd's favourite at his "local" track - and race one was a disaster as he crashed out on lap 1, his third crash of the weekend - he fortunately looked unhurt as he walked back to the pits. Also on the dirt was Scott Russell, but the Yamaha-mounted American managed to fight his way back up through the field and into 8th place at the flag.

The lead was fought in the early stages between pole sitter Troy Corser on the Ducati, the two Castrol Hondas of Slight and Colin Edwards, and Noriyuki Haga on the Yamaha. But on lap 8 Slight moved ahead of Corser and started to make a break. In fact Corser slowed considerably mid-race, allowing both Edwards and Haga to pass as he sat back to conserve tyres. On lap 17, when he decided to make his challenge, the Australian had already left it too late and struggled with back markers and both Haga and Edwards before he finally made the telling pass on Edwards and into second place with two laps to go.

Haga had been controlling lurid slides throughout the race, but on lap 22 he pushed it too far and crashed, fortunately unhurt. Also hitting the tarmac earlier in the race was Suzuki's Peter Goddard.

Behind the leading four, Carl Fogarty made the best of a mediocre start, and passed Suzuki's James Whitham and Kawasaki's Akira Yanagawa to close in on the leaders towards the end of the race - but the gap was too large to make any real impression.

Race Two

In race two, it was Carl Fogarty who led the grid away, with Troy Corser tucked in behind him, and the rare sight of Suzuki's James Whitham in third. But by lap 2 Slight had moved up past Whitham into 2nd, and set his sights on Fogarty, who had dropped behind Corser. After only a further couple of corners Slight dived under Fogarty to take second place, and then sat in a comfortable position as both he and Corser opened up a gap.

For Pier Francesco Chili it was another DNF as he pulled into the pit garage with mechanical failure - a disappointing birthday for the Italian, who celebrated his 34th yesterday. Also unlucky was James Whitham, who had been holding a solid 5th place when he dumped it in the gravel on lap 9. His teammate, Peter Goddard, also joined the two DNF club with mechanical failure on lap 13.

With six laps to go, Slight went to the front, and it seemed that there was little Corser could do to prevent the popular New Zealander from taking his first double victory ever. Fogarty completed the podium, with Slight's teammate Colin Edwards just getting the better of a charging Akira Yanagawa on the last lap. The final member of the dual-DNF club was Yamaha's Noriyuki Haga, who crashed for the second time whilst dicing with teammate Scott Russell on the last lap of the race.
