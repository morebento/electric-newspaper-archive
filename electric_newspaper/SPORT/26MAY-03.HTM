Tuesday May 26, 1998

National Rugby League: Round 11 Review; Results and Standings
by Phil Owens

Aside from Adelaide's upset win, Auckland and North Sydney also produced surprises.

The Warriors overcame the Raiders 25-14 in a match marred by controversial refereeing decisions.

The Norths win over Sydney City was not that big a surprise, but the margin was a shock. The Bears put the cleaners right through the Roosters to dump Sydney City from a share of the competition lead.

Melbourne and Illawarra produced the first drawn result of the year. The Storm and the Steelers are now on odd numbers of premiership points, with the rest on even numbers. This might turn out to be significant in the eventual scramble for spots in the top ten.

St George struggled before easing away from Gold Coast. The Dragons have now won eight games straight to share the lead with last year's ARL champs, Newcastle, with nine wins from eleven starts.

Despite having most of their players backing up after State of Origin duties the Brisbane Broncos were far too strong for Canterbury.

Manly finally got back to the winner's list by thrashing their bayside opponents Cronulla.

In other matches, Balmain struggled to beat Wests, Parramatta beat North Queensland amid more contentious referee decisions, and Newcastle were unconvincing in their defeat of lowly Souths.
