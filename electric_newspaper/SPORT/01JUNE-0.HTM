Monday June 1, 1998

Doohan snatches second place in French 500 cc world championship
by Piston Stoned

Australian Mick Doohan, the four-time world 500 cc motorcycle champion, passed Brazillian Carlos Checa in the second last corner of the last lap, to take second place in a gripping 500 cc race at the Paul Ricard circut in France last night (Australian time).

Spain's Alex Criville, who had passed race leader Checa only a minute earlier, took victory in a race that at times resembled the frantic lead-swapping which usually characterises a 125 cc race.

Doohan had grabbed pole in practice, and uncharactisticly sped into an early lead, with Kenny Roberts Jr. left on the grid with a stalled engine as the lights went green.

In the first few laps Criville and then Checa caught and passed Doohan, and soon Max Biaggi and American John Kocinski caught up with the three race leaders. Biaggi, who was leading the championship before this fifth round of the world championship, then started a serious duel with the American ace, banging elbows coming out of one corner as Biaggi refusing to yield the racing line.

After that, Biaggi dropped further back, and although he was not passed by another rider, he eventually crossed the line a distant fifth.

"He bent my clutch lever, but no problem, that kind of thing is normal in racing," Biaggi said after the race. What a guy!

Meanwhile, Checa was looking the race winner - until he locked the front brake going into turn one with three laps to go. Criville squeezed through into first, with Checa fractionally behind and Doohan on his tail.

But Checa could not hold off the world champion. On the last lap, Doohan managed to dive underneath the Italian and grabbed second place.

"I've had a lot of tyre problems here in the past, so Michelin made a special tyre and it was great," said Criville, the only rider to run hard compound tyres front and rear, after the race.

"I was steady and consistent at first, but when Carlos passed me I started to really try and from then on everything was perfect. I'm really happy to win here because a lot of Spanish fans made the trip, and it's fantastic to lead the championship."

Mick Doohan wasn't quite as pleased. "My bike wasn't carburating so well out of the turns. I knew if I got past Alex and Checa in the twists, they'd just blow back past again on the Mistral," he said.
