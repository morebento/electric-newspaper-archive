Thursday March 19, 1998

Lightning bolts for thunder birds

With only one day to go before the start of this year's natioanl netball season, the Adelaide AAMI Thunderbirds has announced its 10-player squad for the opening game against the Melbourne Phoenix.

The 10 players for Friday's grand-final replay at ETSA Park are:

GSSharon Purser
GAMarisa Romeo
WAAlex Hodge
CRebecca Sanders
WDPeta Squire
GDKathryn Harby (capt)
GKSarah Sutter (v.capt)
ResVicky Neale
Cassie Mogg
Marnie Winter

Coach Marg Angove said the squad of 10 included two new players in Cassie Mogg and Marnie Winter who would be wearing the AAMI Thunderbirds uniform for the first time.

She said all 10 players were fired up for the grand final showdown with Melbourne Phoenix.

"With the anticipated closeness of the competition, it is more than desirable that we start the season on a good note," she said.

"However, we will be without our Australian squad goal attack, Jacqui Delaney, for the first couple of games due to the birth of her baby daughter, Yasmin. This gives Purser and Romeo an opportunity to produce their best and push for future selection on the return of Jacqui."

The goal circle was not the only area where players would feel the pressure, particularly with the new NNL rules of unlimited interchange of 10 players.

"These rules gives coaches an opportunity to take players on and off and as a consequence of this, players may find themselves in different circumstances than previously experienced," Ms Angove said.

"I have talked about this with my team and they are aware of what could happen depending on the course of the game."

The opening match for the Thunderbirds against the Melbourne Phoenix will be the first ever national league game at South Australia's new netball stadium, ETSA Park, at Mile End and the match begins at 7.30pm.
