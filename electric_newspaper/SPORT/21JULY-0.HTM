Tuesday July 21, 1998

Drug bug in tour lure

Laurent Desbiens, who missed the Tour de France two years ago while serving a drug suspension, remained in the overall lead following the ninth stage.

With searing temperatures of over 40 degrees punishing the riders for the second straight day, Leon Van Bon of the Netherlands won the sprint for the stage victory over Germany's Jens Voigt.

Because of the heat and two days of mountain stages coming up, most of the favorites conserved their strength, allowing lesser riders to vie for the stage victory.

Desbiens is not expected to hold the lead in the mountains. The Frenchman finished nearly four hours behind defending champion Jan Ullrich in last year's final standings, placing 127th.

The 10th stage on Tuesday covers 123 miles from Pau to Luchon and features four major climbs. Then comes another stage in the mountains with five more big climbs.

Last year, Ullrich took control in the Pyrenees and never lost the lead. For the moment, he is fifth (3:21 behind) but he still has large margins over his major rivals.

Bo Hamburger of Denmark is 1:18 behind Ullrich in sixth, with American Bobby Julich seventh in the same time as Hamburger.

Italy's Mario Cipollini, who won two of the first stages in sprint finishes, dropped out. Cipollini usually drops out before the racing gets difficult in the mountains.

Desbiens was suspended for six months in 1996 after he tested positive for the steroid nandrolone following a race in France. His background added yet another element to the drug controversy shadowing this race.

First, Festina's director Bruno Roussel was detained by police for questioning along with the team doctor. They were moved to Lille for further investigation.

Tour officials decided to expel the Festina team from the three-week race Friday after Roussel admitted supplying performance-enhancing drugs.

Roussel, team doctor Erik Ryckaert and team masseur Wily Voet were still in police custody and are expected to appear in court July 24. Before that the rest of the Festina team may be summoned by police in Lille.
