Wednesday June 17, 1998

Reshuffled Rams lose try machine
by Phil Owens

Change seems to be the norm for the struggling Rams. As with last season, the position of pivot is proving to be the most unstable. With Cris Quinn out for a week with injury, coach Dean Lance has selected 19 year old Sam Faalafi as five-eighth for Adelaide's home game against Illawarra on Saturday in Whyalla.

Faalafi, a Rams scholarship holder, has played one game for the Rams so far, coming off the bench against the Dragons. The talented novice threw the critical pass to Kidwell that saw Daylight touch down to level the scores against St George in the Rams exciting win at Adelaide Oval on June 6th.

Faalafi is the second rookie tried as five-eighth for Adelaide this year. Craig Bowen could be considered a little unlucky not to be given another chance. Lance must be impressed with Faalafi's attacking skills. His positional play in defence was not very convincing against St George.

In a shock move another of the Rams talented rookies, Graham Appo, a regular in the starting thirteen for the last month, has been dropped from the starting lineup for "disciplinary reasons", along with Darrien Doherty.

Appo scored Adelaide's only try last round but has been replaced on the wing by Rod Jensen, who has appeared on the flank for Adelaide in earlier rounds this season. Doherty's exclusion has given former Brisbane Broncos forward Brett Galea a spot on the interchange bench.

It must have been a severe breach of club discipline that earned Appo his penalty. Even though he has played less than half of the regular games with one of the lowest ranked teams he is a stunning FIFTH on the season try scoring list, with ten four pointers to his credit.

The leading season tryscorer so far this year is Steelers winger Barret with 12. Only Girdler (Penrith), Renouf (Broncos) and Smith (Broncos) have scored 11 tries this season.

In the only other change to the team that was beaten soundly by Melbourne Storm at Olympic Park (Melbourne) last Saturday, big Alan Cann will start from the bench and Andrew Hick moves from the bench to start in the second row.

Despite the 24-4 scoreline against them, Adelaide were not thrashed by the second placed Melbourne. Poor attacking choices with a strong following wind in the first half, a couple of unlucky refereeing decisions, some poor passes and a couple of missed tackles was essentially the difference between the Rams and Storm.

However, Adelaide has now failed to win away from Adelaide Oval in all seven attempts this year.

Their chances against Illawarra look reasonably good. The 12th placed Steelers are currently just a win outside the top ten with 6 wins, a draw and 7 losses. This is well ahead of the 18th placed Rams with three wins and a staggering eleven defeats.

Illawarra aren't travelling all that well at the moment, having lost at home 36-18 to the 9th placed Balmain Tigers last weekend.

The Steelers have scored 65 points more and conceded 54 points less than Adelaide in the 14 rounds played. The stats therefore say Steelers by eight points. If footytab gives you better than 8.5 points start for Adelaide, you might back the Rams to win this one.

A Rams win at Whyalla would see Adelaide climb up to the group currently running 15th-17th that includes Penrith, Manly and Wests.
