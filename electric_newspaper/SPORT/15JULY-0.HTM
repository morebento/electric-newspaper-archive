Wednesday July 15, 1998

Rams on a roll
by Phil Owens

Two-thirds of the inaugural National Rugby League season has passed and Adelaide centre Graham Appo finds himself as the competition's leading tryscorer (15 touchdowns) along with Darren Smith from the pace-setting Brisbane Broncos. Amazingly, the Adelaide Rams have scored 126 and conceded 30 points in their last three matches. They aim for four wins in a row this Saturday in Townsville.

The "Clipsal Adelaide Rams" have come a long way in the last two months.

While other clubs delay signing players for next year pending amalgamation discussions for the year 2000 comp, the Adelaide Rugby League Football club is offering three year deals to players coming off contracts at the end of this year. They believe that the Rams will make the "last fourteen" of the NRL next century.

The players and supporters are doing their best to ensure the dream comes true. The SACA and the SANFL have helped in their perverse way.

With enthusiastic fans now lining both touchlines at Hindmarsh, the atmosphere was electric at the first game at the new home. And didn't the Rams wingers love it!

Crowd support is a big factor for players, coaches and for team survival in the compressed competition of the future.

So far this year, Sydney teams are averaging 10,000 fans per game whereas the non-Sydney siders are averaging 13,500. Brisbane, Newcastle and Melbourne are the big drawcards.

The Rams are averaging 7,400 for home games this year, down on last year's Super League achievement of a healthy 15,300.

Western Suburbs (the Magpies, based in Campbelltown, Sydney) have a similar average home attendance. Gold Coast (6,400) and South Sydney (6,200) are faring worse.

The Rams have managed to put their act together recently.

After ten of the 24 rounds of the regular season the Rams were stone motherless last in the standings with one (narrow) win to their credit. Eight rounds later they are 16th in the 20 team national competition with six wins.

Original coach Rod Reddy was in charge for the first eight games. Under his direction, Adelaide had triumphed only once, over Canterbury 22-20. Under current coach Dean Lance, the Rams have won five from ten.

Adelaide's polished and disciplined display at Hindmarsh Stadium in round 17 was a genuine joy to witness. They showed their dedicated supporters they have strength, skill, speed and, most importantly, confidence as they first stood up to, then wore down and finally absolutely flogged 12th placed Balmain Tigers 52-0 in a display reminiscent of Canberra or Brisbane at full flight.

Their execution was ruthless.

Clearly, the Rams are no longer the easybeats of the NRL.

Last weekend they continued the good form by making mincemeat of the South Sydney Rabbitohs at the Sydney Football Stadium 34-18.

Once again the Adelaide forwards dominated their opposition and established a platform for the "Walters and Goldthorpe" show. Kerrod Walters (hooker) and Noel Goldthorpe (halfback) used their considerable skill and experience to create opportunities for their younger and faster teammates.

The Rams forwards have played very well all year, but the return of hard-running Darien Doherty from injury and the acquisition of strong and tireless Alan Wieland have eased the pressure on Mark Corvo, Alan Cann, Andrew Hick and Adam Peek. These are the "unsung" heroes of Adelaide's recent eye-catching exhibitions.

Adelaide's backline showed flashes of brilliance in the first half of the season but has recently displayed lethal form. Long accurate passes from Goldthorpe and Walters or short passes from Tony Iro are now finding flying marks in the shape of Graham Appo, Dave Kidwell, Mat Daylight, Rod Jensen and Deon Bird.

These outside backs ("three-quarters"), supported by lock forward Dean Schifilliti, are blowing holes in the opposition defence out wide. Just look at what they did to Souths, Balmain and Gold Coast.

Adelaide has speed to burn out wide.

The settling of the backline combination has obviously improved the player's understanding of each other's styles and they've gained confidence in their teammates to do their designated job in the match plan. This has reduced the number of dropped balls, air passes, knock-ons, fumbles, handovers and turnovers, and thus reduced the amount of self-inflicted defensive pressure.

The improved handling and positional play has also been instrumental in moving the scoreboard rapidly along in the Ram's favour.

Instrumental in the Rams recent form reversal has been the performances of Noel Goldthorpe and Kerrod Walters.

As halfback, "Thorpie" is generally the first receiver and in charge of strategy and tactics. His positioning, catching, pass selection and kicking have been on song for about a month.

At dummy-half, Walters is the first to handle the ball and clear it to Goldthorpe or select one of the forwards to receive.

These classy players are the keys to the Rams attack vehicles.

Unfortunately, the Rams may be without both next year.

Sadly for Adelaide supporters we may only have three more opportunities to see the great Kerrod Walters play again.

The Rams captain announced this week he would not be with the Adelaide club in 1999.

With an eye to the future, Adelaide have signed brilliant young hooker Luke Priddis from Canberra to a three-year contract, and only offered the 31 year old veteran a one year deal.

Kerrod will certainly attract a better offer from elsewhere, probably from England or New Zealand.

Those of us who regularly attend Rams home games regard Walters as the class player of the Rams for both 1997 and 1998.

Before he came to Adelaide, he played 220 first grade games between 1988 and 1996 for the top-line Brisbane Broncos along side his twin brother Kevin at five-eighth and their childhood mate Alan ("Alf") Langer at half-back. In that time he won a premiership medal with the trend-setting Broncos.

During that time he fought a long-running battle with his brother Steve, then the hooker for the Canberra Raiders, for the favour of the Queensland and Australian selectors for the chance to wear the State of Origin maroon and the Australian green and gold.

Kerrod Walters represented Queensland in three state-of-origin series and played eight test matches for Australia. Magnificent achievements.

He is the crowd favourite in Adelaide, where his passing, running and cover-defending skills are greatly appreciated and admired.

If he stays fit, we may be lucky enough to see this great player perform again at Hindmarsh Stadium on the afternoon of Saturday 26 July against Auckland, on the weekend of August 7-9 against Manly and finally on the weekend of August 14-16 against North Sydney in round 23.

Unless, of course, Adelaide manage to win their next six games against North Queensland, Auckland, Brisbane, Manly, North Sydney and Newcastle and make the play-offs!

So the Rams head north to take on the 13th placed Cowboys in Townsville on Saturday night. The game is not televised.

These teams last met on a rainy Friday night at Adelaide Oval way back on March 14th in the season opener. North Queensland won 18-8 basically on Adelaide's dropped ball.

The northerners went on to successively beat Balmain, Parramatta (currently 3rd) and Canberra (currently 8th) before their season imploded in round five when they were annihilated 58-4 in Brisbane by the Broncos. They then lost consecutively to a string of top teams: Newcastle, Sydney City, Cronulla, Canterbury and Parramatta.

Since then the Cowboys have won against Penrith, Wests and Gold Coast and lost to St George, Melbourne, Illawarra and most recently to Balmain.

They will be tough to beat at home and the humid conditions may not suit Adelaide. The 7-30 pm start is a bonus for the Rams, avoiding the afternoon heat.

Kerrod Walters will be playing opposite his brother Steve, hooker for North Queensland.

Adelaide's team for the Townsville clash contains only minor forced changes from the recently successful combination.

Tough, talented and speedy centre Dave Kidwell unfortunately broke his arm against Souths last weekend. He is replaced by Danny Grimley who has been playing impressively off the bench in the last fortnight.

Adelaide's first choice centre earlier in the season, Peter Clarke, may get a spot on the interchange for the second week in a row as part of his rehabilitation following a knee injury two months ago.

Chris Quinn will be missing at five-eighth courtesy of his "coat hanger" and the judiciary.

In a very interesting decision, coach Dean Lance has move Dean Schifilliti from lock forward to five-eighth. "Schiffers" came to Adelaide as an established hooker but has been playing lock for the last two months and gets better on each outing.

In the forward pack reshuffle, Tony Iro moves back from second row to the familiar position of lock, Darien Doherty moves back from the front row to second row and Andrew Hick comes back from the bench to start in the front row.

This is a tough, skilled and generally hard working pack of forwards, better than the set that easily matched the Cowboys in round one.

The Adelaide backs are lethal and in exhilarating form.

The Rams should make it four in a row on Saturday.
