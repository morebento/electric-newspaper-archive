Monday March 16, 1998

Cattle crush sheep
by rugby leage reporter, Phil Owens

The loyalty and patience of Adelaide's rugby league fans were further tested on Friday evening when the inaugural National Rugby League season opened with simultaneous matches in Auckland, Brisbane and Adelaide. The North Queensland COWBOYS defeated the Adelaide RAMS 18-8 under lights at Adelaide Oval.

The intermittent rain in Adelaide on Friday afternoon increased in intensity from about 15 minutes before the start of the first grade fixture and continued throughout the night. The rain, the lights, the slippery surface and greasy ball, combined with a chilly, occasionally gusting cross wind from the sou'west, provided less than ideal conditions for players, officials and fans.

This was an eagerly anticipated match for a number of reasons. Most rugby league supporters are pleased that the war between Super League and the Australian Rugby League ended in a "unified" competition, and are hopeful that 1998 will be a better year than 1997.

Also, the opening clash for the Adelaide RAMS at home to the COWBOYS had some obvious interest. The Townsville-based North Queensland Cowboys collected the Super League "wooden spoon" last year but they had the "wood" on the Rams in 1997. Their coach, Tim Sheens, is highly respected as a result of the outstanding performances of the Canberra Raiders under his direction in the past.

The Cowboys' starting lineup includes a front row of Lomax, Steve Walters and Roberts, backed up by Dale Shearer at fullback. It is an extremely talented and experienced outfit. On paper at least, the Cowboys appear capable of beating most opposition.

Most Adelaide fans would have found the Rams rookie performance last year reasonable. Their poor "home" record was one of their biggest handicaps. It was always going to take more than a few months for these displaced easterners to settle in to South Australia and regard Adelaide Oval as home. A better home record is likely this season.

There was also considerable interest in the debut performances of the Rams' new signings, especially half back Noel Goldthorpe. Some observers predicted Goldthorpe's presence as first or second receiver behind the ruck would provide the Rams with more options when in possession, and take pressure off Kerrod Walters, Maybon and Quinn, all of whom produced outstanding attacking displays last season.

Unfortunately the 11,000 plus crowd had their committment rewarded in the worst possible way when the Rams failed to gather a well judged kick off from the opening whistle and handed the opposition the ball to feed a scrum packed about 25 metres from goal. The Cowboys were thus on the attack from the start of the match, and did extraordinarily well to control the ball for the next 25 minutes considering the appalling conditions.

During this period, the Rams conceded penalties for ruck infringements, one of which let Ferris open the Cowboys account. The Rams defence uncharacteristically hesitated and missed tackles, presenting Steve Walters with what looked like a soft try but was the result of sustained pressure from the Cowboys and good anticipation, stepping and a dummy pass from Walters.

The Rams also completed the categories on the error sheet by committing the other major mistake, failure to control the ball when in possession. One dropped the ball was scooped by Shearer who immediately sprinted 40 metres to score near the posts, despite the desperate attempted cover attention of Goldthorpe.

It would not be fair to blame the Rams completely for the 14-0 scoreline after 25 minutes, without praising the Cowboys defence, which was exemplary during this period. The North Queenslanders were mobile, disciplined and enthusiastic in defence during this period and this contributed significantly to the early spate of Adelaide errors.

After this the bad news for Adelaide ended, and they proceeded to dominate the latter period of the first half. Unfortunately, the game was lost by then.

The introduction of Kerrod Walters, who was team captain last year but started this game from the bench, immediately transformed the Rams in attack and defence. Goldthorpe, who had already made his presence felt in defence, now started to find some space and some time with the ball in his hands. This was to become a feature of much of the play in the second half.

The Adelaide forwards more than matched their better credentialled opposition, and Cann proved very difficult for the Cowboys defence to contain on several occasions.

The Rams recovery came from one of his bursts down the blind side in the first half. Cann broke a couple of tackles at the advantage line and, showing an unexpected degree of speed, drew the cover defence well to set up his supports. His inside pass was deflected by a defender into the path of the Rams right winger, Timani, who toed the ball ahead, outpaced Cowboys back rower Cunningham, regathered on the line and scored the Rams first try for the season in the right northern corner late in the first half. The third referee delayed play for quite some time before the score was awarded. Goldthorpe was unsuccessful with the attempted conversion, and the players went to the break with the Rams trailing by 14-4.

The second half continued in a similar pattern to the last period of the first half. Ball control was poor on both sides but this was not surprising considering the conditions, with rain persisting throughout the second stanza. Each team scored an unconverted try during the second half, Nandruka for Townsville and Maybon for Adelaide. The Rams also planted the ball behind the Cowboys line on two other occasions but each time a try was disallowed for an infringement in the lead up play. Both teams varied their play intelligently in the second half, which produced patches of exciting attack and assertive defence. The Cowboys defence appeared fragile down the centre on a few occasions but they were very solid in this aspect out wide.

So it was a dreadful result for Adelaide. Hopefully, the next time they turn in 20 minutes of mistakes it will be at the end, rather than the start, of a match. Speaking of starts, Rams coach Reddy puzzled most fans by excluding Kerrod Walters from the starting lineup. Considering how important the first 10-15 minutes of a game can be in determining the result, it will be interesting to see whether he persists in this strategy.

Adelaide's next opponents, the Manly-Warringah SEA EAGLES, have played in the last three (ARL) grand finals, and are favoured to do so again this season. The Sea Eagles are smarting from the spanking they received from the BRONCOS in Brisbane, and provide quite a challenge for the Rams. The clash between Toovey, the energetic and vociferous Manly half back, and Goldthorpe, should be a feature of the game. The Rams showed in the second half against the Cowboys they have the capacity to produce exciting attack, and Manly have shown many times that they are susceptible to sustained pressure. However, if the Rams reproduce their error count from last Friday against Manly they will be buried.
