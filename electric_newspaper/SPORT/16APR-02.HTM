Thursday April 16, 1998

Rams to the slaughter?
NRL Round 6 Preview: Adelaide v Sydney City
by Phil Owens

At 7.30 pm Friday 17th April, the Adelaide Rams are due to take to the field at Sydney Football Stadium to confront the star-studded Roosters. Only a supreme optimist could predict an Adelaide victory on recent form.

Flogged by Penrith two weeks ago, beaten and bashed by Gold Coast last week, the Rams field yet another new combination.

Noel Goldthorpe is back as captain, with 1997 captain Kerrod Walters starting on the interchange bench as he did against the Cowboys. This move can't be reward for performances against the Chargers last week, because Goldthorpe had an extremely poor game and Walters produced yet another exemplary display.

Among the backs, centre Peter Clarke has been moved to the bench. Tamani and Kimmorley are out. The pack has also been reshuffled, partly due to injury to Brett Galea.

The bits and pieces Adelaide outfit lines up against a settled Roosters combination that is big, strong, fast, skilled and exudes class.

Last weekend, Sydney City easily accounted for the Western Suburbs Magpies four tries to one. They have also beaten Cronulla and Illawarra this season, with losses to the Bears and the Warriors.

I expect Barnhill and Pickering to dominate the ruck for the Roosters, and Lam and Fittler to cause headaches for the Rams defenders on the fringes. Cleary and Sing are likely to see plenty of ball and they only need a little bit of open space to cause some serious damage on the scoreboard.

My heart says Rams but my head says Fittler and co. Roosters by 15.
