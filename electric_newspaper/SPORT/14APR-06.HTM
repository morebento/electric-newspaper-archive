Tuesday April 14, 1998

Charges Ram Adelaide
by Phil Owens

Gold Coast took their first win of the season defeating Adelaide 16-8.

Last Thursday night Adelaide Oval's smallest rugby league crowd of just over 7,000 had their eardrums assaulted by the public address system before the Rams' round 5 home game against the Gold Coast Chargers.

The Adelaide fans then had their eyesight and comprehension tested as the Rams produced a first class display of ineptitude. I lost count of the number of times Adelaide fumbled the ball.

Adelaide's biggest problem was lack of respect for the ball. In a competition as intense and fierce as the National Rugby League, points are difficult to score against all opposition. Adelaide do not seem to understand that you need to have the ball in your hands to score tries.

Yet the Rams scored two tries against themselves by passing the ball directly to their opponents. These costly errors were the differences between the two sides on the night.

A worse performance is hard to imagine.

Adelaide half-back Noel Goldthorpe had a major shocker. He knocked on several times, threw passes to no one, threw one pass clean over the side line and was tackled with the ball on several occasions. These are almost terminal offences for a half-back.

Goldthorpe could not sell a dummy to a nursing mother, let alone his opposite number Wes Patten. The Charger's half-back, by contrast, was a constant threat with the ball in his hands. He is very nimble and slippery. Patten also managed to play off-side in defence throughout the game. Neither the referee nor the touch judges seemed to mind on most occasions.

Goldthorpe's obvious replacement, Craig Kimmorly, is unfortunately not available for the rest of the season. Sadly, he sustained a serious ankle injury during the game. Playing at five-eighth, Kimmorly set up Adelaide's only major score of the game with a well judged inside pass to Iro about 40 metres out. Iro hit the ball at speed and was stopped by the desperate Chargers cover defence a couple of metres short of the tryline. Dean Schiffilliti backed up and scored under the posts. It was a good reward for these three, although Iro was also guilty of some poor handling on other occasions.

Adelaide's prospects for the season were further deflated by serious injuries to hard working forwards Marty McKenzie and Brett Galea. The Rams finished the match against the Gold Coast with an empty bench, so serious was the damage inflicted by the Chargers.

Kerrod Walters (hooker and dummy-half), Chris Quinn (full-back) and Alan Cann (front row forward) produced their typical high class efforts. Wingers Joe Tamani and Luke Williamson tried hard to get involved but the ball rarely got to the inside backs, so atrocious was the Rams handling around the ruck area. Adelaide centre Peter Clarke showed exceptional speed to run down the Charger's full back and prevent a Gold Coast try.

Coach Rod Reddy and half Goldthorpe must have had a very interesting conversation after the match.

So where does this leave Adelaide with one fifth of the regular season gone? Sharing 14th with Canterbury, Wests, Illawarra, Manly, Gold Coast and South Sydney, who all have won one and lost five. On points scored and points conceded, only South Sydney (54-130) rank below Adelaide (56-130).

One suspects that Adelaide may have to crash right to the bottom before they can turn this around. Their next opponents are Sydney City (3 wins 2 losses). This could be a repeat of the debacle against Penrith on the Rams last trip to Sydney.
