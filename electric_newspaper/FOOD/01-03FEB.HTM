Tuesday, February 3, 1998

Peaceful pasta: The Aldgate Cafe
By Elaine Moore

There are a couple of things unique about the Aldgate Cafe. Firstly, its pizza toppings are among the most innovative you'll find in South Australia. Where else can you have the flavours of Thai cuisine - fresh ginger, lemongrass and coriander presented on a pizza? The selection is extensive enough to satisfy most discerning tastebuds. Vegetarians are well looked after, cheeseless pizzas are available and a choice of pizza pastry and base sauces are offered.

Another unique thing about this cafe is its two locations - Aldgate and Summertown - both under the name of the Aldgate Cafe. Maybe that's more peculiar than unique, but then the name assures you of a consistent standard, so much so that if you saw an Aldgate Cafe in Gumeracha you'd know what to expect. Both offer menus of pizza, pasta and pan-fried dishes, soups, salads and desserts for dine-in, take-away or home delivery.

The alfresco courtyard dining at Aldgate has the added attraction of a majestic 100 year-old elm which umbrellas over the paved terrace and nearby creek. The setting is tranquil and relaxing, a refuge for day and night-time wildlife such as birds and possums.

Prices at both cafes are reasonable with pizzas from $7.50 to $21.00 for 'party size'. A choice of five types of pasta and several sauces; $7.40.

The Scaloppine and Cotoletta dishes have several choices of sauces at both $7.90. And soup of the day; $3.50. The choice of food is varied and negotiable, with staff happy to accommodate personal tastes.

Sam Carey is a loyal employee who has a past history working for owner/managers Kym and Susan Gertig. Sam returned to work at the Aldgate Cafes after working in 'classy' yet less satisfying cafes in Melbourne.

"There' s a good feeling amongst the staff, which means we often get together after work - so there's a closeness which makes it an enjoyable place to work.

"There are regulars who come to dine four times a week and at Summertown the cafe tends to be very locals orientated."

It certainly makes a change for Hills dwellers to have the comfort of a late night cappuccino as Adelaidian plain dwellers have. And with the innovative fine foods of these two cafes they are an alternative venue and fair competition to those city cafes.

Check it out.

The Aldgate Cafe; Strathalbyn Road, Aldgate, in the Adelaide Hills.

Monday and Tuesday 3-10 p.m.,
Wednesday and Thursday 10 a.m. - to 11 p.m.,
Fri, Sat, and Sun. 10 till late.

Home deliveries from 5.30 p.m.
