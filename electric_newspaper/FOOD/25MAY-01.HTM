Monday May 25, 1998

Another Ebenezer Surprise from Barossa Valley Estate

The 1996 Ebenezer Cabernet Sauvignon has recently been released by boutique South Australian winery, Barossa Valley Estate.

This Cabernet Blend is one of three wines in Barossa Valley Estate's award-winning Ebenezer range.

An area in the north eastern corner of the Barossa where generations of grape-growers have nurtured low-yielding vineyards proved the inspiration behind the Ebenezer range of wines.

Along with the Cabernet Sauvignon, the Ebenezer range also includes a Chardonnay, and Shiraz, with the 1995 Shiraz also recently released.

This wine is predominantly a Cabernet Sauvignon blend. The other components are carefully-chosen varieties that represented the best of the 1996 vintage - Cabernet Franc, Malbec and Merlot were all considered.

Following fermentation, separate parcels of wine were matured in new French oak for 12 months before being blended into the finished wine.

The 1996 Ebenezer Cabernet Sauvignon is an intense plum red in color , with a lifted, attractive bouquet displaying plum, spice and a hint of violet.

Winemaker, Fiona Donald described the 1996 Ebenezer Cabernet Sauvignon as "extremely flavoursome", with a palate of plum fruit, complimented by coffee and cedary oak characters.
