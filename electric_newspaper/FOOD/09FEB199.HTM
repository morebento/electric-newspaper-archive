Monday February 9, 1998

A day in the Southern Vales
By Adrienne Eccles

Adelaide sits on the doorstop of the finest wine-producing areas in Australia. The Barossa and Clare Valleys are known internationally, but the Southern Vales are equally attractive.

You can't beat a long leisurely drive through the Southern Vales on a sunny weekend, and what better pastime than to visit some of the smaller wineries that enrich the area.

Fox Creek Wines (Malpas Road, Willunga). Fox Creek has only been open for a couple of years but they already have a Bushing King and Queen title under their belt and winemakers Sparky and Sarah Marquis are acquiring an enviable reputation for the things they do with shiraz.

Take the road from McLaren Vale to Willunga, and turn west along Malpas Road. Fox Creek is about 1k further on. It's a family affair managed by a retired doctor and his wife: the winemakers are their daughter and son-in-law, the vineyard manager is their son, and another daughter can be found running the office/cellar door operation with her new baby in a basket on the floor.

The tasting room is a small cottage set back among the vines, with some nice areas around it for a picnic, if you're so inclined.

Taste their premium shiraz. It's big and full of personality, the sort of wine that talks back to you. Sparkling burgundy buffs should try Vixen, a sparkling red that bears no resemblance to lemonade and raspberry. Both of these are marvellous drops, perhaps on the expensive side ($35 and $20).

For a more affordable wine try their JSM, a shiraz-cabernet blend, still a well-rounded mouthful that goes will at the dinner table or on a picnic.

But don't drive past: Fox Creek is worth visiting.

Wirilda Creek (McMurtrie Road, McLaren Vale). For the Complete Tourist Experience, drop in to Wirilda Creek on McMurtrie Road just south of McLaren Vale. This is a relatively new place, venture up the long drive to where the Cellar Door is half-hidden behind a stretch of cultivated scrub. To get to the tasting counter, you have to make your way through the restaurant/café. The day I dropped in most tables were full.

The menu is short but interesting, redolent of East End Rundle Street cafés, and wines consumed at the table can be purchased at cellar door prices. For the shiraz, that means $15 a bottle. And it's big, peppery, flavoursome and warm.

If not inclined to sit in the café, lean against the tasting counter, or sun yourself on the verandah over coffee, wine and fried goat's cheese, you can play boules on the specially prepared lawn or wander around through the cultivated scrub-land at the front of the establishment.

Hugo Winery (Elliot Road, off McLaren Flat Road). Tastings at Hugo Wines are made in a demountable metal building, the sort you see on construction sites: a plain setting for honest wines.

The emphasis at Hugo is on straight varietals, with plenty of fresh fruit character. It's a small operation, a family winery that produces around 5,000 cases in all per year.

Try the shiraz: it's good. 100% estate grown grapes, with 15 months' American oak treatment, it's an award winner with two gold medals, a silver and a bronze to its name. It's under $15 a bottle, with lots of peppery spicy notes and a touch of cloves. I bought some to put under the bed in the expectation of it mellowing down to that marvellous butter-and-vanilla flavour good shiraz develops with time but alas and alack, I've already drunk it all. I'll just have to go back..

Scarpantoni Estate Winery (Scarpantoni Drive, off Kangarilla Road, McLaren Vale). The winery is a triumph of 70's pseudo-Spanish architecture, but there's nothing kitsch about the wines. Winemakers Filippo and Michael Scarpantoni are busy boys these days, turning out wine for the export market and the domestic consumer, and winning plenty of awards.

Shiraz lovers are well advised to try the Block 3 Shiraz, at $15 a bottle. This is a wine that will develop for years, if you can leave it in the bottle that long. For a drink-now treat, stock up on School Block, a cabernet-shiraz-merlot blend at $12.50.

A new offering from Scarpantoni Estate this year is Black Tempest, a full bodied sparkling burgundy priced slightly higher at $25 a bottle. This promises to be a wine-lover's sparkler, full of flavour and punch: buy one for a special treat.
